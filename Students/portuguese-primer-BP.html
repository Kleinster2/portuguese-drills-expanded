2. Regras das Vogais — Vowel Rules

The letter E

The letter E usually sounds like ‘bait’ (ê), and sometimes like ‘bed’ (é). The closed sound is written with a circumflex (Ê), the open sound with an acute accent (É).

Closed Ê (like ‘bait’):

mês (month, MÊHS) • três (three, TRÊHS) • inglês (English, yhn-GLÊS)

EU diphthong:

eu (I, ÊH-ooh) • meu (my/mine masc., MÊH-ooh) • seu (your/yours, SÊH-ooh)

E before consonant (like ‘bait’):

ele (he, ÊH-leeh) • eles (they masc., ÊH-leehs) • você (you, vôh-SÊH)

Open É (like ‘bed’):

tela (screen, TÉH-lah) • vela (candle, VÉH-lah) • céu (sky, SÉH-ooh)
zero (ZÉH-rooh) • regra (rule, HÉH-grah) • perna (leg, PÉHR-nah) • mel (honey)
ela (she, ÉH-lah) • elas (they fem., ÉH-lahs)

The letter O

The letter O usually sounds like ‘boat’ (ô), sometimes with circumflex. It can also sound like ‘bought’ (ó), sometimes with the acute accent.

Closed Ô (like ‘boat’):

comida (food, kôh-MEEH-dah) • doce (sweet, DÔH-see)
sinônimo (synonym, see-NÔH-knee-mooh) • Amazônia (ah-mah-ZÔH-knee-ah)

OU diphthong:

ou (or, Ô-oo) • ouro (gold, Ô-oo-rhoo) • outro (other, Ô-oo-trooh)
sou (I am, SÔ-oo) • estou (I am, ehs-TÔ-oo) • comprou (bought, koum-PRÔ-oo) • vou (I go, VÔ-oo)

Open Ó (like ‘bought’):

bola (ball, BÓH-lah) • rosa (rose, RHÓH-zah) • moda (fashion, MÓH-dah)
Flórida (FLÓH-ree-dah) • Califórnia (kah-lee-FÓHR-knee-ah) • cópia (copy, KÓH-pee-ah)

Final -E = “ee” in BP

BP Rule: Words ending in -E or -ES are pronounced with an “ee” sound.

nome (name, NÔH-mee) • nomes (NÔH-mees)
pobre (poor, PÓH-bree) • pobres (PÓH-brees)

Final -O = “ooh” in BP

BP Rule: Words ending in -O or -OS are pronounced with an “ooh” sound.

lado (side, LAH-dooh) • lago (lake, LAH-gooh)
gato (cat, GAH-tooh) • tudo (everything, TOO-dooh)

The letter U

The letter U is pronounced like a double OO (as in “boot”).

rua (street, HOOH-ah) • luz (light, LOOHZ) • lua (moon, LOOH-ah) • fruta (fruit, FROOH-tah)
menu (mêh-NOOH) • aula (class, AH-ooh-lah) • roupa (clothing, HOH-ooh-pah)

Nasal Vowels

Vowels followed by M or N in the same syllable, or accented with the tilde (ã), are nasalized. Also: muito, muita, muitos, muitas (very, much, many).

Ã / AN nasals:

não (no, not, NÃ-oom) • mão (hand, MÃ-oom) • pão (bread, PÃ-oom) • cão (dog, CÃ-oom)
São Paulo (SÃ-oom PAH-oo-looh) • alemão (German man/language) • alemã (German woman)
mãe (mother, MÃ-ym) • andar (to walk, ãhn-DAHR)

EN / EM nasals:

bem (well, BÊH-yn) • sem (without, SÊH-yn) • cem (hundred, SÊH-yn)
também (also, tãhm-BÊH-yn) • entre (between, ÊH-yn-tree)
nuvem (cloud, NOOH-veyn) • então (then, êyn-TÃ-oom)

IN / IM nasals:

sim (yes, SEEYN) • assim (like that, ah-SEEYN)

ON / OM nasals:

bom (good, BÔH-oom) • com (with, CÔH-oom) • som (sound, SÔH-oohm)
ontem (yesterday, ÔH-oom-teh-yn)

UN / UM nasals:

um (a/one masc., OÕM) • uma (a/one fem., OÕ-mah) • mundo (world, MOÕHN-doo)

MUITO (special nasal):

muito, muita (very/much, MOÕeen-too, MOÕeen-tah)
muitos, muitas (many, MOÕeen-toos, MOÕeen-tahs)

3. Regras das Consoantes — Consonant Rules

J — JA, JE, JI, JO, JU

J sounds like the fricative S in “vision” (transcribed as SZ / ʒ).

jantar (dinner, szãhn-TAHR) • loja (store, LÓH-szah)
hoje (today, ÔH-szee) • jeito (manner, SZÊH-y-too) • beijo (kiss, BEH-y-szoo)
jogo (game, SZÔH-goo) • anjo (angel, ãhn-SZOOH)
ajuda (help, ah-SZOOH-dah) • junto (together, SZOÕN-too)

G — GA, GE, GI, GO, GU

GA, GO, GU sound as in English. GE and GI sound like J (the “vision” sound).

gato (cat, GAH-too) • gelo (ice, SZÊH-loo) • página (page, PAH-szee-nah)
lago (lake, LAH-goo) • seguro (insurance, sêh-GOO-rooh)

C and Ç

CA (kah), CE (seh), CI (see), CO (koh), CU (koo). The Ç (cedilha) always sounds like S.

casa (house, KAH-zah) • você (you, vôh-SÊH) • cinema (see-NÊH-mah) • conta (bill, KOHN-tah) • cubo (cube, KOO-boo)
preço (price, PRÊH-sooh) • Suíça (Switzerland, sooh-EEH-sah)

S between vowels = Z

The S between two vowels sounds like a Z.

casa (house, KAH-zah) • mesa (table, MÊH-zah) • coisa (thing, KÔH-y-zah)
rosa (rose/pink, HÓH-zah) • Brasil (brah-ZEEoo)

Z at end of word = S

Z sounds like Z in “zebra”, except at the end of a word, when it sounds like S.

zona (zone, ZOH-nah) • feliz (happy, feh-LEES)
vez (turn/time, VÊHS) • talvez (perhaps, tah-ooh-VÊHS)

L at end = W sound (BP)

BP Rule: The L at the end of a syllable or word is pronounced as W (AW, EW, IW, OW, UW).

papel (paper, pah-PÉW) • alma (soul, AW-mah) • hospital (ohs-pee-TAW) • hotel (oh-TÉW)

H at start = silent

The H at the start of a word is always silent.

hora (time/hour, ÓH-rah) • homem (man, ÓH-meyn)
hospital (ohs-pee-TAW) • hotel (oh-TÉW)

R and RR

R at the beginning of a word, or RR anywhere, sounds like the H in “hot.” Any non-initial single R is pronounced as in English “red.”

R initial / RR = H sound:
correr (to run, koh-HÊHR) • carro (car, KAH-hooh)
rua (street, HOOH-ah) • rio (river, HEEH-ooh) • rede (network, HEH-gee)

Single R (not initial) = R sound:
caro (expensive, KAH-rooh) • areia (sand, ah-RÊH-ya) • prato (dish, PRAH-too)
pergunta (question, pehr-GOON-tah) • palavra (word, pah-LAH-vrah)
obrigado/obrigada (thank you, oh-bree-GAH-doo / oh-bree-GAH-dah)

CH = SH

CH is pronounced like SH.

chave (key, SHAH-vee) • chegada (arrival, she-GAH-dah) • China (SHEEH-nah)
chocolate (shoh-koh-LAH-tchee) • chuva (rain, SHOO-vah)

NH = nasal Ñ

NH is pronounced like the Spanish Ñ.

minha (my fem., MEE-ñah) • unha (fingernail, OOH-ñah)
conhecer (to get to know, koh-ñeh-SEHR)
banho (bath, BAH-ñooh) • sonho (dream, SOH-ñooh) • nenhum (none, neh-ÑUHM)

LH = LY

LH is pronounced LY.

ilha (island, EEH-lyah) • toalha (towel, toh-AH-lyah)
trabalhar (to work, trah-bah-LYAHR) • eu trabalho (trah-BAH-ly-ooh)
colher (spoon, koh-LYÉHR) • mulher (woman/wife, moo-LYÉHR)
olho (eye, ÔH-ly-ooh) • molho (sauce, MÔH-ly-ooh)

D palatalization (BP): DI = “gee”, final DE = “gee”

BP Rule: The syllable DI anywhere in a word, and DE at the end of a word, are pronounced “GEE” (like the “gee” in “gee whiz”).

DI: dia (day, GEE-ah) • dinheiro (money, gee-NHEY-rhoo) • diretor (director, gee-rheh-TOHR)
prédio (building, PREH-gee-oo)

Final DE: cidade (city, see-DAH-gee) • verdade (truth, vehr-DAH-gee)
saudade (nostalgic longing, sah-oo-DAH-gee) • onde (where, ÕUN-gee)

T palatalization (BP): TI = “tchee”, final TE = “tchee”

BP Rule: The syllable TI anywhere in a word, and TE at the end of a word, are pronounced “TCHEE”.

arte (art, AHR-tcheeh) • antigo (old/vintage, ahn-TCHEE-goo)
chocolate (shoh-koh-LAH-tchee) • quente (hot, KÊHN-tchee)

GUE, GUI, GUA

GUE = as in “guest”; GUI = as in “guilt”; GUA = GOOAH.

água (water, AH-gooah) • igual (equal, EEH-gooah-ooh) • aluguel (rent, ah-looh-GEH-ooh)
guia (guide, GUEEH-ah) • seguir (to follow, sêh-GUEER)

QUA, QUE, QUI, QUO

QUA = KWA, QUE = KEH, QUI = KEY, QUO = KWO.

quase (almost, KWAH-zee) • quarto (bedroom, KWAHR-too) • quanto (how much, KWÃHN-too)
quatro (four, KWAH-troo) • quarta-feira (Wednesday, KWAHR-tah FEH-y-rah) • qual (which, KWAHL)

quente (hot/warm, KÊHN-tchee) • querer (to want, keh-RÊHR) • eu quero (I want, KEH-roo)
que (what/that, KÊH) • quem (who, KÊH-ym)

aqui (here, ah-KEY) • quinta-feira (Thursday, KEYHN-tah FEH-y-rah)

Consonants not followed by a vowel (BP)

BP Rule: Consonants not followed by a vowel get an inserted “ee” sound.

ritmo → “rítimo” (rhythm, HEEH-tchee-moo)
pneu → “pineu” (tire, pee-NÊH-oo)
Detroit → “Detroiti” (dêh-TROY-tchee)
Internet → “Interneti” (yhn-têhr-NÉH-tchee)
Nova York → “Nova Yorki” (NÓH-vah y-ÓHR-kee)
iPad → “iPadi” (eye-PÉH-gee)
Big Mac → “Bigui Méqui” (BEE-guee MÉH-kee)

5. Sequência de Apresentação — Self-Introduction Sequence

Eu sou — I am (ÊH-ooh SÔH-ooh)

Used for nationality, ethnic origin, degree, profession, marital status — anything permanent or true on a medium-to-long-term basis.

Eu sou americano/a. • Eu sou canadense. • Eu sou italiano/a.
Eu sou solteiro/a. (single) • Eu sou casado/a. (married) • Eu sou viúvo/a. (widower/widow)
Eu sou advogado/a. (lawyer) • Eu sou engenheiro/a. (engineer) • Eu sou professor/a. (teacher)
Eu sou aposentado/a. (retired)

Eu sou de — I am from (ÊH-ooh SÔH-ooh GEE)

Cities are neutral (no article). Most other nouns (countries, etc.) take a definite article.
de = from, of (gee)

Eu sou de Nova York. • Eu sou de Los Angeles. • Eu sou de São Paulo. • Eu sou de Lisboa. • Eu sou de Londres.

Articles: masculine singular = o; feminine singular = a; masculine plural = os; feminine plural = as

Most countries ending in -a are feminine; others are masculine.

O Reino Unido. • A Argentina. • O Japão. • A Austrália. • Os Estados Unidos.

Eu falo — I speak (ÊH-ooh FAH-looh)

Eu falo inglês. • Eu falo espanhol. • Eu falo francês. • Eu falo português.

e = and (EEEH) • um pouco = a little

Eu falo um pouco de espanhol, um pouco de alemão, e um pouco de francês.

Eu moro em — I live in

Eu moro em Nova York. • Eu moro em Los Angeles. • Eu moro em Miami.

Eu trabalho em — I work at/in

Eu trabalho em tecnologia. • Eu trabalho com finanças. • Eu trabalho no hospital.

6. Contrações — Contractions

DE + articles (from/of the)

The preposition de (from, of), when followed by a definite article, merges into a single word.
ContractionComponentsPronunciation
dode + odooh
dade + adah
dosde + osdoohs
dasde + asdahs

Eu sou do (de+o) Japão. • Eu sou da (de+a) Argentina.
Eu sou dos (de+os) Estados Unidos. • Eu sou das (de+as) Ilhas Virgens.

A few cities are masculine: Eu sou do Rio (de Janeiro). • Eu sou do Porto. • Eu sou do Cairo.

A few countries are neutral (no article): Eu sou de Portugal. • Eu sou de Israel. • Plus Cuba, Costa Rica, etc.

States can be masculine, feminine, or neutral: Eu sou do Maine. • Eu sou da Califórnia. • Eu sou de Connecticut.

EM + articles (in the)

ContractionComponentsPronunciation
noem + onooh
naem + anah
nosem + osnoohs
nasem + asnahs

Eu moro no (em+o) Japão. • Eu moro na (em+a) Argentina.
Eu moro nos (em+os) Estados Unidos.
Eu moro no Rio de Janeiro. • Eu moro no Porto.

Streets (rua) and avenues (avenida) are feminine:
Eu moro na rua Bleecker. • Eu moro na Park Avenue. • Eu moro na Broadway.

15. Possessivos — Possessive Adjectives

Possessives agree with the thing possessed, not the possessor. In BP, dele/dela is commonly used instead of seu/sua to avoid ambiguity.

Usage note: dele/dela/deles/delas come after the noun: “o carro dele” (his car), “a casa dela” (her house). These are invariable — they don’t change for the gender/number of the thing possessed, only for the possessor.
