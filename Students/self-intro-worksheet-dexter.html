Key Verbs — First Person Singular (Eu)
In Brazilian Portuguese, the pronoun Eu (I) is often dropped in casual speech because the verb ending already tells us who is speaking.
Verb (Infinitive)MeaningEu formExample
serto be (permanent)souEu sou o Dexter.
morarto live (reside)moroEu moro em Brooklyn.
falarto speakfaloEu falo inglês.
trabalharto worktrabalhoEu trabalho em um escritório.
Articles before names: In BP, we use o (masculine) or a (feminine) before a person’s name.
→ Eu sou o Dexter.  |  Eu sou a Maria.

No article before professions: Unlike English, Portuguese does NOT use “a/an” before professions.
→ Eu sou professor. (I am a teacher.)  —  NOT *Eu sou um professor.

Teacher Notes — Minha Primeira Apresentação

Student Profile

Dexter is an absolute beginner in Brazilian Portuguese. He is a native English speaker who also speaks Spanish, which gives him a significant advantage with cognates, verb conjugation patterns, and Romance language structure. He lives in Bed-Stuy, Brooklyn, and works a 9-to-5 schedule. This is his very first Portuguese lesson.

Key advantage: Dexter’s Spanish knowledge means he will likely intuit the verb conjugation system quickly (e.g., soy → sou, hablo → falo). Use Spanish as a bridge, but watch for false friends and interference (e.g., Spanish does not use articles before names, but BP does: o Dexter, a Maria).

Teaching Tips

  • Leverage Spanish cognates: profesión → profissão, nacionalidad → nacionalidade
  • Highlight key differences early: articles before names, de contractions (do, da), nasal vowels
  • Encourage pronoun dropping — Sou o Dexter sounds more natural than Eu sou o Dexter
  • Build toward a full self-introduction paragraph by end of session
