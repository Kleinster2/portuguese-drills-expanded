Condicional e Imperfeito — Quando usar cada um?

Português Europeu • Student: Marvin • Date: _______________
Formação do condicional: infinitivo + terminações do imperfeito de haver
-ar: falaria, falarias, falaria, falaríamos, falariam
-er: comeria, comerias, comeria, comeríamos, comeriam
-ir: partiria, partirias, partiria, partiríamos, partiriam
Irregulares (raiz curta): dizer → dir-  |  fazer → far-  |  trazer → trar-
⚠ Uso em português europeu — regra importante:

Para pedidos educados, desejos e conselhos, os portugueses usam o imperfeito, não o condicional:
• Queria um café, por favor. (não “quereria”)
• Podias ajudar-me? (não “poderias”)
• Eu, no teu lugar, descansava. (não “descansaria”)

O condicional usa-se em:
• Hipóteses com “se”: Se tivesse tempo, viajaria mais.
• Futuro do passado: Ele disse que viria amanhã.
• Escrita formal e notícias: O ministro teria dito que...

B. Hipóteses com “se” — verbos irregulares (condicional)

Lembra-te: dizer → dir-, fazer → far-, trazer → trar-. As terminações são iguais.
Ex: O meu amigo (dizer) diria que isso é uma má ideia.

19. Se tivéssemos mais espaço, nós (fazer) _______________ uma festa em casa.
20. Eu nunca (dizer) _______________ uma coisa dessas a ninguém.

Teacher Notes — Condicional Worksheet 2 (Marvin, EP)

Companion pages — do not hand to student unless desired

Origin & context

Feb 2026: Worksheet 1 (condicional-worksheet-marvin.html) drilled the condicional for polite requests, teaching forms like “Poderias fechar a janela?” and expecting “quereria” as a valid drill item. While prescriptively correct, no native EP speaker uses these forms in conversation. Portuguese speakers say “Podias fechar a janela?” and “Queria um café” — using the imperfeito, not the condicional. This prompted a full pedagogy review and the addition of the Variant Tagging and Native Usage Filter rules to PEDAGOGY.md.

Student profile

Key pedagogical decision: imperfeito vs condicional in EP

In EP, the imperfeito has largely replaced the condicional for querer, poder, dever, gostar, preferir and similar verbs in three contexts: polite requests, desires/preferences, and advice. This is not colloquial or informal — it is standard spoken Portuguese at all registers. The condicional forms (quereria, poderia, deveria) sound overly formal or literary. Teaching them as the default misleads students into learning forms they’ll never hear.

When EP uses each form

Context Form in EP Example
Polite requests Imperfeito Podias ajudar-me? / Querias um café?
Desires & preferences Imperfeito Gostava de viver em Sintra. / Preferíamos ir a pé.
Advice Imperfeito Eu, no teu lugar, descansava uns dias.
Counterfactuals with “se” Condicional Se tivesse tempo, viajaria mais.
Future of the past Condicional Ele disse que viria amanhã.
Formal writing & news Condicional O ministro teria dito que…

The imperfeito’s double duty (teaching moment)

Because the imperfeito replaces the condicional for polite/hypothetical uses, it now carries two meanings in EP:

  • Past habitual: “Quando era criança, queria ser médico.” (When I was a child, I wanted to be a doctor.)
  • Polite/hypothetical: “Queria um café, por favor.” (I would like a coffee, please.)

Context disambiguates. Students need to learn to read context rather than relying on verb form alone. This is an explicit teaching goal of Section G.

Section-by-section design rationale

  • A & B (se-clauses): Condicional is correct here in EP. Se + imperfeito do conjuntivo triggers condicional in the result clause. These sections establish that the condicional does exist and has real uses — it’s not abolished, just narrower than textbooks suggest.
  • C (polite requests): The core correction. Students rewrite present-tense requests using imperfeito. Verbs tested: poder, querer, saber, ter, importar-se. Enclisis maintained throughout (EP pronoun placement).
  • D (advice): “Eu, no teu lugar, ...” + imperfeito. Open-ended answers develop production skills. Sample answers in the key use imperfeito consistently.
  • E (full hypothetical answers): Free-response se-clause answers using condicional. These are the hardest items — student must produce the full “se + conjuntivo, ... + condicional” structure.
  • F (future of the past): Mechanical transformation (futuro simples → condicional). This is the other context where condicional is unambiguously correct in EP.
  • G (choose the form): The pedagogically central section. Ten items mixing polite requests, se-clauses, desires, future-of-the-past, and advice. Student must identify the context and select the right form. Answer key labels each with (imp. — pedido/desejo/conselho) or (cond. — “se”/fut. passado). This is the exercise type recommended by PEDAGOGY.md for EP.
  • H (translation): Integrative exercise. English sentences require the student to (1) translate, (2) identify the context, and (3) choose the correct EP form. Five items use imperfeito, five use condicional.

EP-specific details in this worksheet

  • Tu forms throughout (standard in EP, not “você”)
  • Enclisis in affirmative contexts: “Podias ajudar-me”, “Podias passar-me”
  • EP vocabulary: comboio (not trem), telemóvel (not celular), autocarro (not ônibus), esplanada, pastel de nata, francesinha, bacalhau
  • “a + infinitivo” progressive: “estivesse a chover” (not “estivesse chovendo”)
  • “connosco” (EP spelling, not BP “conosco”)
  • Preposition usage: “até ao” (EP) rather than “até o” (BP)
  • Pequeno-almoço not used here (not relevant), but would be EP for “breakfast”
  • Morada for “address” (EP), not “endereço” (more BP-associated)
