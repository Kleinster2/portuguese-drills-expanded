## Beat 1 — Opening (~5 min)

- Greet in BP: *"Olá Ana! Tudo bem? Bem-vinda!"* — model the register from sentence one
- Set frame: BP throughout where possible; English only for explanations
- Quick ear-check: "What Portuguese do you already know from your dad / from listening growing up?" — gauges heritage exposure depth
- Sketch the arc verbally: four building blocks of *"Eu..."* → pronunciation → press-tour vocab → wrap

## Beat 2 — Eu sou (identity) (~20 min)

Target sentences:
- *Eu sou Ana.*
- *Eu sou americana.* (feminine adjective — flag the agreement)
- *Eu sou roteirista.* OR *Eu sou escritora.*

Drill: Ana fills the blank "Eu sou ___" with 4–5 things (name, nationality, profession, generation, etc.).

**Watch for:**
- Ser/estar confusion — *"Eu estou Ana"* (Spanish *estar* interference). Reframe: ser = identity, estar = temporary state. Don't go deep on estar yet (Part VII).
- Spanish *"Yo soy"* dropping into *"Eu sou"* — same shape, lucky.

**Pronunciation note (light touch, full drill in Beat 6):**
- *sou* = SOH (closed o), not SOO (don't let Spanish *soy* drag it long)

## Beat 3 — Eu moro em (location) (~15 min)

Target sentences:
- *Eu moro em Brooklyn.*
- *Eu moro em Nova York.*
- *Eu moro no Brasil* (expose the contraction em+o → no, don't drill yet)

**Watch for:**
- Spanish *"Yo vivo"* → *"Eu vivo"* — works grammatically but *moro* is the natural BP register for residence. Flag and substitute.
- *em / no / na* contractions — preview only, full unit later. Don't overload Beat 3.

**Pronunciation note:**
- *moro* has open o + closed o pattern → MÓ-roo. Both syllables matter; flag without belaboring.

## Beat 4 — Eu trabalho em/com (profession) (~15 min)

Target sentences:
- *Eu trabalho com cinema.*
- *Eu trabalho na DC Studios.* (model the contraction casually — *em + a*)
- Optional: *Eu trabalho em Hollywood.*

Conversational layer: ask about her work — what she writes, what's coming after Supergirl. Natural opening to practice the verb pattern with real content.

**Watch for:**
- Spanish *"trabajo"* (with /x/) bleeding into *"trabalho"* (with /ʎ/-like sound, palatalized). The *-lh-* is a key BP sound.

## Beat 5 — Eu falo + e (languages) (~15 min)

Target sentences:
- *Eu falo inglês.*
- *Eu falo inglês e espanhol.* — introduce conjunction *e*
- *Eu entendo um pouco de português.* — soft self-cushion for her actual level (sets her up to deflect with grace in Rio)

**Watch for:**
- *espanhol* — the *-nh-* sound; Spanish doesn't have this exactly.
- *inglês* — nasal ê. Natural setup for Beat 6.

## Beat 6 — Pronunciation: nasal vowels (~15 min)

The lesson's most distinctive PT-vs-Spanish payoff. Three nasal endings:

| Nasal | Examples | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| *ão* | não, mão, irmão, obrigação | She'll say *não / obrigada* constantly in Rio |
| *em* | bem, também, em | *Tudo bem?* / *Tudo bem.* — the universal opener-response |
| *om* | bom, com | *Bom dia.* — every morning greeting |

Minimal-pair drill (if she's solid by here):
- *pão* (bread) vs *pau* (stick)
- *são* (healthy/saint) vs *sou* (I am)
- *mão* (hand) vs *mal* (badly)

Rio-deployable mini-script for repetition:
- *"Bom dia. Tudo bem? Sim, obrigada. Não, obrigada."*

## Beat 7 — Press-tour vocab anchor (~5 min)

Verbal only, no handout. Vocab list to introduce (she repeats each, Gil corrects):

- *roteirista, escritora* — what she does
- *filme, cinema* — her domain
- *ator, atriz, diretor, diretora* — who she'll meet
- *entrevista, imprensa* — what she'll do

Anchor sentence: *"Eu sou roteirista. Eu escrevi um filme."* — past tense *"escrevi"* is technically A2, but introducing it as a single memorized form gives her a payoff sentence for the press tour. Flag it as "we'll learn this properly next time."

## Beat 8 — Wrap (~5 min)

- Set homework verbally: practice introducing yourself in BP to a mirror every day this week (5–6 sentences using *Eu sou / Eu moro / Eu trabalho / Eu falo*)
- Confirm logistics for lesson 2 (target Thu 5/28, same venue)
- Closure: *"Até a próxima quinta, Ana!"*

## Watch overall

- Don't overload. Principle 11 — one conceptual step. The step is "first-person self-introduction in BP." Parts I–IV are one move, not four.
- Every example sentence is Ana-anchored (Filadélfia, Carroll Gardens, DC Studios, Supergirl, cinema) — the grammar is generic, the lexicon is targeted.
- Pronunciation correction is gentle in lesson 1 — flag, model, move on. Mastery is lessons 2–4.
- If she asks about past tense ("How do I say 'I wrote'?") — answer with *escrevi* but don't unpack the conjugation; it's Lesson 2 material.
- If she asks about *você* / formal address — say "we'll get there in Phase 3; for now stick with *eu*."
