Formação do Condicional — Verbos Regulares
O condicional forma-se adicionando as terminações ao infinitivo completo do verbo:
Pessoafalarcomerabrir

Nota: A forma de nós leva acento agudo no i (falaríamos, comeríamos).

Verbos Irregulares no Condicional (apenas 4!)
Estes verbos usam um radical especial encurtado, mas as terminações são as mesmas:

Atenção: O radical de pôr é por- (sem acento circunflexo).
Usos do Condicional nesta ficha:

1. Situações hipotéticas (would): Expressa o que aconteceria numa situação imaginada.
  • Com mais dinheiro, eu compraria uma casa maior.
  • No teu lugar, ela falaria com o chefe.

2. Cláusulas com «se» (if-clauses): A cláusula com se usa o imperfeito do conjuntivo; a cláusula principal usa o condicional.
  • Se eu pudesse, compraria uma casa.
  • Se ela tivesse tempo, viajaria mais.

3. Futuro do passado (reported speech): Quando se relata o que alguém disse/pensou/prometeu que faria.
  • Ele disse que viria à festa.
  • Ela prometeu que traria os documentos.
Nota: Este é um dos poucos contextos em que o condicional é usado na fala em PE (não apenas na escrita).

Notas para o Professor — Teacher Notes

Student Profile

This worksheet is designed for a Level 1 learner of European Portuguese who is being introduced to the Condicional (conditional tense). The student is assumed to have prior knowledge of the present tense and basic verb conjugation patterns. No prior exposure to the condicional is required.

Scope & Pedagogical Focus

This worksheet covers three areas only:

  1. Conjugation — Regular formation (infinitive + -ia/-ias/-ia/-íamos/-iam) and the four irregular stems (far-, dir-, trar-, por-).
  2. Se-clause hypotheticals — The student fills in only the condicional form in the main clause; the imperfeito do conjuntivo in the se-clause is provided.
  3. Future of the past (reported speech) — Sentences with disse que, prometeu que, pensava que, sabia que, achava que, etc.
Important: This worksheet deliberately does NOT cover the use of the imperfeito to replace the condicional in spoken EP (e.g., Eu queria um café instead of Eu quereria um café). That is a separate topic. If students ask about this, acknowledge it briefly and note it will be covered in a future lesson on Conditional: Usage (EP).

Section-by-Section Notes

Section A (Items 1–8): Pure regular conjugation. Ensures students can form the condicional mechanically before applying it in context. Watch for missing accents on the nós form (íamos). Also note item 7 uses toda a gente, which takes 3rd person singular — a common source of errors.

Section B (Items 9–16): Focuses exclusively on the four irregular verbs. Emphasise that the endings are identical to regular verbs; only the stem changes. A common error is students writing *fazeria instead of faria.

Section C (Items 17–25): Introduces se-clause hypotheticals. The imperfeito do conjuntivo is pre-filled so students can focus on the condicional. This is a good moment to briefly review the se + imperfeito do conjuntivo pattern without deep-diving into conjuntivo conjugation.

Section D (Items 26–33): Future of the past / reported speech. Emphasise that this is one of the few contexts where EP speakers genuinely use the condicional in everyday speech (not just formal writing). The imperfeito would be incorrect here as it would change the meaning to past habitual.

Section E (Items 34–40): Mixed exercises requiring students to identify the context and apply the correct form. Useful for assessment of overall comprehension.

Section F (Items 41–44): Translation exercises for productive practice. Accept reasonable variations in vocabulary and word order, as long as the condicional form is correct.

Common Errors to Watch For

  • Missing accent on nós forms: falariamos instead of falaríamos.
  • Using full infinitive for irregulars: *fazeria, *dizeria, *trazeria.
  • Confusing pôr stem: Using *pôria instead of poria (no circumflex on the stem).
  • Subject-verb agreement with indefinite pronouns: toda a gente, alguém, ninguém all take 3rd person singular.
Spanish Speakers: If your student speaks Spanish, highlight the strong parallel between the two languages. Both form the conditional by adding -ía endings to the infinitive, and both have similar irregular stems (PT far- / ES har-; PT dir- / ES dir-; PT trar- / ES traer-*; PT por- / ES pondr-). The main difference is that Spanish has more irregular conditional verbs (e.g., sabr-, tendr-, vendr-), whereas in Portuguese saber, ter, and vir are regular in the conditional.

Extension Activities

  • Ask students to write 5 sentences about what they would do if they lived in Portugal.
  • Pair work: Student A makes a promise («Eu prometo que...»); Student B reports it using the condicional («Ele/Ela disse que...»).
  • After mastering this worksheet, proceed to the Conditional: Usage (EP) drill, which covers the imperfeito replacing the condicional in polite/spoken contexts.
