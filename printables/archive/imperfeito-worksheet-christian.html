-ER and -IR verbs have the same endings! Remove the infinitive ending and add: -ia, -ia, -íamos, -iam.
Even ir (to go) is regular: eu ia, nós íamos.

When to use the Imperfeito:
• Habitual actions: Eu sempre acordava cedo. (I always woke up early.)
• Descriptions: O dia estava bonito. (The day was beautiful.)
• Ongoing states: Ela morava em São Paulo. (She lived in São Paulo.)
• Age/time: Eu tinha dez anos. (I was ten years old.)

Part D: Past Continuous (37–44)

Form: estava/estavam + gerúndio (-ando, -endo, -indo). Fill in both blanks.

Past Continuous describes an action in progress when something else happened.
• Eu estava dormindo quando você ligou. (I was sleeping when you called.)
37. I was studying when you called. — Eu quando você ligou. (estar + estudar)
38. She was cooking dinner when the phone rang. — Ela o jantar quando o telefone tocou. (estar + cozinhar)
39. They were watching TV when I arrived. — Eles TV quando eu cheguei. (estar + assistir)
40. We were walking in the park when it started to rain. — Nós no parque quando começou a chover. (estar + caminhar)
41. He was sleeping when the alarm went off. — Ele quando o despertador tocou. (estar + dormir)
42. I was reading a book when you arrived. — Eu um livro quando você chegou. (estar + ler)
43. She was talking on the phone when the meeting started. — Ela no telefone quando a reunião começou. (estar + falar)
44. They were playing soccer in the rain. — Eles futebol na chuva. (estar + jogar)

Part E: Past Intent (45–50)

Form: ia/íamos/iam + infinitivo. “Was going to...” Fill in both blanks.

Past Intent describes something planned that didn’t happen.
• Eu ia comprar um carro, mas mudei de ideia. (I was going to buy a car, but I changed my mind.)
45. I was going to call you, but I forgot. — Eu para você, mas esqueci. (ir + ligar)
46. She was going to travel to Portugal, but cancelled the trip. — Ela para Portugal, mas cancelou a viagem. (ir + viajar)
47. We were going to eat at a restaurant, but stayed home. — Nós em um restaurante, mas ficamos em casa. (ir + comer)
48. He was going to buy a car, but changed his mind. — Ele um carro, mas mudou de ideia. (ir + comprar)
49. They were going to come to the party, but it rained. — Eles à festa, mas choveu. (ir + vir)
50. I was going to study for the test, but my friend called. — Eu para a prova, mas meu amigo ligou. (ir + estudar)
