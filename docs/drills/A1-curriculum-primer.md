# A1 Portuguese Curriculum - Survival Portuguese

## Overview

A1 is the beginner level where learners acquire survival Portuguese - the ability to handle basic everyday interactions. By the end of A1, learners can introduce themselves, ask and answer simple questions, and handle routine transactions.

All drills support both Brazilian Portuguese (BP) and European Portuguese (EP).

---

## Core Grammar

### 1. Personal Pronouns & Subject Omission
- Subject pronouns: eu, você, ele/ela, nós, vocês, eles/elas
- BP vs EP: você (BP) vs tu (EP) for informal "you"
- Subject omission: Portuguese drops unambiguous subjects (falo = I speak)

### 2. Ser vs Estar
- Ser: inherent qualities, professions, origin, time, events
- Estar: temporary states, location, moods, conditions
- Ambiguous adjectives that change meaning with each verb

### 3. Articles & Gender
- Definite articles: o, a, os, as
- Indefinite articles: um, uma, uns, umas
- Gender rules and common patterns
- When to omit articles

### 4. Noun Plurals
- Regular plurals (-s, -es, -is)
- Special endings (-ão → -ões/-ães/-ãos, -m → -ns)

### 5. Basic Adjective Agreement
- Gender agreement (novo/nova)
- Number agreement (novos/novas)
- Invariable adjectives (-e, -l, -z endings)

### 6. Possessives
- Meu/minha, seu/sua, nosso/nossa, dele/dela
- BP vs EP usage differences
- Article + possessive patterns

### 7. Demonstratives
- Este/esta (near speaker)
- Esse/essa (near listener)
- Aquele/aquela (far from both)
- Contractions: deste, nesse, daquele, etc.

### 8. Basic Prepositions
- De (from, of), em (in, on, at), para (to, for), com (with)
- A (to), por (by, through), sem (without)

### 9. Contractions
- De + articles: do, da, dos, das
- Em + articles: no, na, nos, nas
- A + articles: ao, à, aos, às
- Por + articles: pelo, pela, pelos, pelas

### 10. Question Words
- Quem (who), o que (what), qual (which)
- Onde (where), quando (when), como (how)
- Por que/porque (why/because), quanto (how much/many)

### 11. É que Questions
- Forming questions with "é que": O que é que você quer?
- Optional but very common in spoken Portuguese
- Adds emphasis or naturalness to questions

---

### 12. Subject Identification
- Recognizing which subjects match a conjugated verb form
- Unambiguous forms (eu/-o, nós/-mos) vs ambiguous forms
- When subjects can be omitted in natural speech

---

## Verb Tenses

### 13. Present Tense - Regular Verbs
- -AR verbs: falar → falo, fala, falamos, falam
- -ER verbs: comer → como, come, comemos, comem
- -IR verbs: partir → parto, parte, partimos, partem

### 14. Present Tense - Essential Irregular Verbs
- Ser: sou, é, somos, são
- Estar: estou, está, estamos, estão
- Ir: vou, vai, vamos, vão
- Ter: tenho, tem, temos, têm
- Fazer: faço, faz, fazemos, fazem
- Poder: posso, pode, podemos, podem
- Querer: quero, quer, queremos, querem
- Ver: vejo, vê, vemos, veem
- Vir: venho, vem, vimos, vêm
- Dar: dou, dá, damos, dão
- Saber: sei, sabe, sabemos, sabem
- Pôr: ponho, põe, pomos, põem

### 15. Immediate Future
- Ir (conjugated) + infinitive
- "Vou falar" = I'm going to speak
- Most common future expression in spoken Portuguese
