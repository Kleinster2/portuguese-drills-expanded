# A2 Portuguese Curriculum - Elementary

## Overview

A2 builds on A1 foundations to enable learners to handle routine tasks and describe their background, immediate environment, and matters of immediate need. Learners can participate in short social exchanges and understand frequently used expressions.

All drills support both Brazilian Portuguese (BP) and European Portuguese (EP).

---

## Past Tenses

### 1. Preterite Tense - Regular Verbs
- -AR verbs: falei, falou, falamos, falaram
- -ER verbs: comi, comeu, comemos, comeram
- -IR verbs: parti, partiu, partimos, partiram
- Completed actions in the past

### 2. Preterite Tense - Irregular Verbs
- Ser/Ir: fui, foi, fomos, foram (identical forms!)
- Ter: tive, teve, tivemos, tiveram
- Estar: estive, esteve, estivemos, estiveram
- Fazer: fiz, fez, fizemos, fizeram
- Poder: pude, pôde, pudemos, puderam
- Querer: quis, quis, quisemos, quiseram
- Ver: vi, viu, vimos, viram
- Vir: vim, veio, viemos, vieram
- Dar: dei, deu, demos, deram
- Dizer: disse, disse, dissemos, disseram
- Trazer: trouxe, trouxe, trouxemos, trouxeram
- Pôr: pus, pôs, pusemos, puseram

### 3. Imperfect Tense
- -AR verbs: falava, falava, falávamos, falavam
- -ER/-IR verbs: comia, comia, comíamos, comiam
- Irregular: ser (era), ter (tinha), vir (vinha), pôr (punha)
- Ongoing/habitual actions in the past

### 4. Preterite vs Imperfect
- Preterite: completed, punctual actions
- Imperfect: ongoing, habitual, descriptive
- Using both in narrative (background vs foreground)
- Time markers for each tense

## Verb Constructions

### 5. Reflexive Verbs
- Reflexive pronouns: me, se, nos
- Common reflexive verbs: levantar-se, sentar-se, chamar-se
- Placement of reflexive pronouns
- Reciprocal meaning (eles se conhecem)

### 6. Present Continuous
- Estar + gerund (-ando, -endo, -indo)
- "Estou falando" = I am speaking
- BP vs EP differences (BP prefers continuous more)

### 7. Imperative Mood
- Affirmative commands (fale, coma, abra)
- Negative commands (não fale, não coma)
- Irregular imperatives (ser, ir, estar)
- Formal vs informal commands

---

## Pronouns

### 8. Direct Object Pronouns
- Me, te/o/a, nos, os/as
- Placement rules (before vs after verb)
- Contractions with verb endings

### 9. Indirect Object Pronouns
- Me, te/lhe, nos, lhes
- Verbs that take indirect objects
- Combined pronouns (mo, lho)

### 10. Pronoun Placement
- Proclisis (before): negative, question words, certain adverbs
- Enclisis (after): beginning of sentence, affirmative commands
- Mesoclisis (middle): future/conditional (formal, mostly EP)

---

## Comparisons

### 11. Comparatives
- Mais/menos + adjective + (do) que
- Tão + adjective + quanto/como
- Irregular comparatives: melhor, pior, maior, menor

### 12. Superlatives
- O/A mais + adjective
- Absolute superlative (-íssimo)
- Irregular superlatives: o melhor, o pior

---

## Prepositions & Usage

### 13. Por vs Para
- Por: cause, exchange, duration, means, through
- Para: purpose, destination, recipient, deadline
- Common expressions with each

### 14. Saber vs Conhecer
- Saber: facts, information, skills (saber + infinitive)
- Conhecer: people, places, familiarity
- "Sei a resposta" vs "Conheço Maria"

### 15. More Preposition Usage
- A: direction, time expressions
- Com: accompaniment, instrument
- Sem: without
- Sobre: about, on top of
- Entre: between, among

---

## Adverbs & Modifiers

### 16. Frequency Adverbs
- Sempre, nunca, às vezes, frequentemente
- Placement in sentences

### 17. Manner Adverbs
- Formation: adjective + -mente
- Common irregular adverbs: bem, mal, devagar

### 18. Muito, Pouco, Tanto, Demais
- As adverbs (muito bem) vs adjectives (muitos livros)
- Agreement rules

### 19. Todo vs Tudo
- Todo/toda/todos/todas (adjective)
- Tudo (pronoun = everything)

## Connectors

### 20. Basic Conjunctions
- E (and), ou (or), mas (but)
- Porque (because), quando (when)
- Se (if), como (as/like)

### 21. Time Expressions
- Antes de, depois de
- Enquanto, durante
- Desde, até

### 22. É que Questions
- Forming questions with "é que": O que é que você quer?
- Common in spoken Portuguese
- Adds emphasis or naturalness to questions

## Summary

A2 covers approximately 31 topic areas focusing on:
- Past tenses (preterite, imperfect) and their distinction
- Reflexive verbs and present continuous
- Object pronouns and placement
- Comparatives and superlatives
- Key vocabulary distinctions (por/para, saber/conhecer)
- Expanded vocabulary for daily life
