# A1 Curriculum Structure

## Pedagogical Principles

1. **ATOMIC UNITS** - One topic per unit. Split when content covers multiple concepts.

2. **SINGULAR BEFORE PLURAL** - Students master production tools in singular (eu, ele/ela, você) before introducing plural forms (nós, eles/elas, vocês).

3. **PRODUCTION FIRST** - Introduce useful vocabulary words before formal grammar patterns. Students produce as soon as possible.

4. **CONCRETE BEFORE ABSTRACT** - Location (where you are) before feelings (how you feel).

5. **PRONOUN PROGRESSION** - eu → (drop eu) → ele/ela → você → (later: nós, eles/elas, vocês)

6. **VOCABULARY SERVES PRODUCTION** - No vocabulary for vocabulary's sake. Every word introduced enables a specific production goal.

7. **CULTURAL RELEVANCE**
   - Age: People rarely state their age in conversation. Low priority.
   - Hunger/thirst: Use "estou com fome/sede" not "tenho fome/sede"

  6   │ Contractions em      │ em/no/na/nos/nas
  7   │ Where You Live (City)│ eu moro em + city
  8   │ Where You Live (Street)│ eu moro na rua/avenida
  9   │ What You Like        │ eu gosto de

 12   │ What You Have        │ eu tenho

## Section Summary

| Section | Focus | Units |
|---------|-------|-------|
| 1 | EU - First person with explicit pronoun | 1-14 |
| 2 | DROP EU - First person without pronoun | 15-26 |
| 3 | LONG SENTENCES - Combining patterns | 27-32 |
| 4 | ELE/ELA - Third person singular | 33-45 |
| 5 | ELE/ELA LONG SENTENCES - Combining third person patterns | 46-51 |
| 6 | QUESTIONS - Question words | 52 |
| 7 | VOCÊ - Second person questions | 53-60 |

---

## Progression Flow

```
eu (explicit) → eu (dropped) → long sentences → ele/ela → ele/ela long → questions → você
```
