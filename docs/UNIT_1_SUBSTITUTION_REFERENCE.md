# Unit 1: Identity Statements - Substitution Mode Reference

This document shows all Unit 1 sentences in **Substitution Mode**, demonstrating how they sound when pronounced.

## Pattern 1.1 - Names (Eu sou [o/a] [name])

| Original | Annotated | Substituted |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Eu sou o Daniel. | Eu sou o/u/ Daniel/u/. | Eu sou u Danielu. |
| Eu sou a Sofia. | Eu sou a Sofia. | Eu sou a Sofia. |

**Key change:** `o` → `u` (article and name ending)

---

## Pattern 1.2 - Professions (Eu sou [profession])

| Original | Annotated | Substituted |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Eu sou analista. | Eu sou analista. | Eu sou analista. |
| Eu sou professor. | Eu sou professor/u/. | Eu sou professu. |
| Eu sou professora. | Eu sou professora. | Eu sou professora. |

**Key changes:**
- `-o` → `u` (masculine profession)
- Feminine forms ending in `-a` stay the same
- `-ista` (gender-neutral) stays the same

## Pattern 1.4 - Cities (Eu sou de [city])

| Original | Annotated | Substituted |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Eu sou de Miami. | Eu sou de/dji/ Miami. | Eu sou dji Miami. |
| Eu sou de São Paulo. | Eu sou de/dji/ São Paulo/u/. | Eu sou dji São Paulu. |

**Key changes:**
- `de` → `dji` (palatalization)
- Final `-o` → `u` (Paulo → Paulu)

## Pattern 1.5 - Countries (Eu sou [do/da] [place])

| Original | Annotated | Substituted |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Eu sou do Brasil. | Eu sou do/u/ Brasil/u/. | Eu sou du Brasilu. |
| Eu sou da França. | Eu sou da França. | Eu sou da França. |

**Key changes:**
- `do` → `du` (contraction: de + o)
- Final `-il` → `-ilu` (Brasil → Brasilu)
- `da` stays the same (feminine article)

## Full Example

| Original | Annotated | Substituted |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Eu sou casado com a Sofia. | Eu sou casado/u/ com/coun/ a Sofia. | Eu sou casadu coun a Sofia. |

**Key changes:**
- `-ado` → `adu`
- `com` → `coun` (nasal vowel)

---

## Pronunciation Key

### Changes that occur:
- **o → u**: Daniel → Danielu, do → du, casado → casadu, alto → altu, professor → professu
- **de → dji**: de → dji (standalone word)
- **com → coun**: com → coun (nasal vowel)

### No changes (feminine forms):
- Sofia, professora, americana, brasileira, casada, alta
- Feminine forms ending in **-a** remain unchanged

## Why This Matters

Understanding substitution mode helps learners:
1. **Hear** the actual pronunciation vs. what's written
2. **Recognize** the systematic patterns (masculine -o always → u)
3. **Predict** how new words will sound
4. **Practice** pronunciation with confidence

The phonetic realization shows that Portuguese pronunciation follows predictable rules, making it easier to learn once you understand the patterns.
