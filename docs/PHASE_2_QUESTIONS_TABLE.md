## Key Phase 2 Characteristics

### 1. **Introduction of Indefinite Pronouns (Units 26-30)**
Phase 2 introduces 12 questions with indefinite pronouns:
- Questions 287-295 (9 new questions added in Phase 3 development)
- Questions 49-51 (original questions converted)

**Purpose:** Teach that alguém, ninguém, and todo mundo conjugate as 3rd person singular, preparing students for singular mastery before plural introduction.

### 2. **Heavy Preterite Focus (31 questions - 39.2%)**
Phase 2 dedicates significant attention to past tense:
- Regular preterite: 6 questions (Units 34-35, 42-43)
- Irregular preterite: 25 questions (Units 36-40, 44-46)

**Purpose:** Master past tense before moving to imperfect and other advanced tenses.

### 3. **"A gente" Introduction (8 questions - 10.1%)**
First appearance of "a gente" (informal "we"):
- Unit 35: Q63 (A gente abriu a porta)
- Units 38-40: Q74, 77, 78, 80, 81
- Unit 42: Q87
- Unit 44: Q145

**Purpose:** Establish "a gente" as a 3rd person singular conjugation before introducing plural forms.

### 4. **Object Pronouns (11 questions - 13.9%)**
Introduction of pronoun systems:
- Reflexive pronouns (Unit 43): 5 questions
- Direct object pronouns (Unit 44): 6 questions

**Purpose:** Build foundation for more complex pronoun placement rules in Phase 3.

### 5. **Approaching Singular Checkpoint (Unit 40)**
By Unit 40, students have learned ALL singular-conjugating subjects:
- ✅ eu
- ✅ você
- ✅ ele/ela
- ✅ a gente
- ✅ alguém/ninguém/todo mundo

**Next:** Units 41+ will introduce plural conjugations (nós, vocês, eles/elas).

## Pattern Analysis

### Comprehension Questions (6 total)
Only 7.6% comprehension (vs 21.9% in Phase 1). Students are now expected to **produce** more than passively **understand**:
- Q33: Eu como pão todos os dias
- Q46: Eu vou viajar
- Q65: Eu fui ao Brasil
- Q66: Eu fui professor
- Q143: Eu o vejo
- Q158: Eu me levanto cedo

### Production Questions (73 total)
92.4% of Phase 2 is active production, focusing on:
- Verb conjugations (especially preterite)
- Preposition selection and contractions
- Pronoun placement
- Agreement patterns
