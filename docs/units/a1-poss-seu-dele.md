## Outcomes

- Use seu/sua/seus/suas possessives (but understand the ambiguity)
- **Use dele/dela/deles/delas construction for clarity** (PREFERRED in BP!)
- Distinguish "your" from "his/her" using context or dele/dela
- Understand why Brazilians prefer the dele/dela construction
- Express third-person possession clearly

## Vocabulary

- **seu/sua/seus/suas** (your/his/her - masculine/feminine, singular/plural) - **AMBIGUOUS!**
- **dele** (his - literally "of-him")
- **dela** (her - literally "of-her")
- **deles** (their - masculine/mixed group)
- **delas** (their - feminine group only)

## Grammar

- **Seu/sua/seus/suas possessives** - can mean YOUR, HIS, HER, or THEIR!
  - Four forms: seu (masc sing), sua (fem sing), seus (masc pl), suas (fem pl)
  - **CRITICAL PROBLEM:** Extremely ambiguous in Brazilian Portuguese
- **SOLUTION: Dele/Dela construction** (PREFERRED in colloquial BP!)
  - Structure: **article + noun + dele/dela/deles/delas**
  - o irmão dele (his brother), a casa dela (her house)
  - **REQUIRES article:** Cannot say just "irmão dele"
- Position:
  - Seu/sua: possessive + noun (like meu/minha)
  - Dele/dela: article + noun + dele/dela
