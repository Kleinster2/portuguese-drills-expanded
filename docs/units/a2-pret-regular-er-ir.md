## Outcomes

- Talk about completed past actions with regular -ER and -IR verbs
- Use all -ER verbs from Unit 20 in past tense (comer, beber, correr)
- Use all -IR verbs from Unit 26 in past tense (abrir, partir, assistir)
- Recognize that -ER and -IR share past tense endings
- Notice that nós form is SAME as present (like -AR verbs)
- Combine with -AR past tense from Unit 29

## Vocabulary

- Time expressions (review from Unit 29): ontem, na semana passada, no mês passado
- Additional: de manhã (in the morning), à tarde (in the afternoon), à noite (at night)
- já (already), ainda não (not yet)

## Grammar

- **Preterite tense for regular -ER and -IR verbs**
- **KEY INSIGHT: -ER and -IR verbs share the SAME past tense endings!**
- Past tense endings for -ER/-IR verbs:
  - eu: -**i** (comi, bebi, corri, abri, parti, assisti)
  - você/ele/ela: -**eu/-iu** (comeu, bebeu, correu, abriu, partiu, assistiu)
  - nós: -**emos/-imos** (comemos/abrimos - same as present!)
  - vocês/eles/elas: -**eram/-iram** (comeram/abriram)
- Used for completed actions in the past (same as -AR verbs in Unit 29)

### Exemplar sentences

- Comi arroz e feijão ontem (I ate rice and beans yesterday)
- Bebi café de manhã (I drank coffee in the morning)
- Corri no parque na semana passada (I ran in the park last week)
- Abri a porta (I opened the door)
- Ele partiu cedo (He left early)
- Assistimos um filme (We watched a movie)
- Vocês comeram? (Did you all eat?)
- Não bebi cerveja (I didn't drink beer)
