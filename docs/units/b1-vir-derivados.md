## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Conjugate any verb derived from **vir** by inheriting the parent's irregular paradigm
- Apply the **3sg / 3pl accent rule** that affects vir-derivatives in the present indicative (acute on 3sg, circumflex on 3pl) — same pattern as ter-derivatives
- Use *intervir* and *convir* (the two productively used derivatives) in context
- Recognize *advir, provir, sobrevir* (formal/literary) without forcing production

## Vocabulary

The vir family is the smallest of the three derivative families. Most members are formal or restricted-context.

| Verb | Meaning | Frequency / register |
|---|---|---|
| **vir** | to come | foundational |
| **intervir** | to intervene | medium — daily + news |
| **convir** | to be appropriate, suit, be fitting | medium — mostly impersonal *convém que* |
| **provir** | to come from, originate from | low — formal/written |
| **advir** | to come from, derive, ensue | low — formal/literary |
| **sobrevir** | to come over, befall, occur unexpectedly | low — literary |

## Grammar

The inheritance rule is total: every derivative conjugates exactly like vir, with **the same 3sg-acute / 3pl-circumflex pattern** seen in the ter family.

### Vir paradigm (parent — must know cold)

| Tense | eu | tu | você/ele/ela | nós | vocês/eles/elas |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Presente | venho | vens | vem | vimos | vêm |
| Pretérito | vim | vieste | veio | viemos | vieram |
| Imperfeito | vinha | vinhas | vinha | vínhamos | vinham |
| Futuro | virei | virás | virá | viremos | virão |
| Condicional | viria | virias | viria | viríamos | viriam |
| Pres. subj. | venha | venhas | venha | venhamos | venham |
| Imp. subj. | viesse | viesses | viesse | viéssemos | viessem |
| Particípio | vindo | | | | |

### The 3sg / 3pl accent shift in present

When vir takes a prefix, the same accent rule from the ter family applies:

| Verb | 3sg present (acute) | 3pl present (circumflex) |
|---|---|---|
| vir | vem (no accent — monosyllabic) | vêm (circumflex) |
| convir | **convém** | **convêm** |
| intervir | **intervém** | **intervêm** |
| provir | **provém** | **provêm** |
| advir | **advém** | **advêm** |
| sobrevir | **sobrevém** | **sobrevêm** |

### Derivative inheritance — example: intervir

| Tense | eu form | 3sg form |
|---|---|---|
| Presente | intervenho | **intervém** |
| Pretérito | intervim | interveio |
| Imperfeito | intervinha | intervinha |
| Futuro | intervirei | intervirá |
| Pres. subj. | intervenha | intervenha |
| Particípio | intervindo | |

### Convir is mostly impersonal

The most common form of convir in modern Portuguese is the impersonal *convém que* + subjuntivo:

- *Convém que tu **venhas** cedo.* — It's advisable for you to come early.
- *Não convém que ele **saiba** disso.* — He shouldn't know about this.
- *Convém-lhe ficar em casa.* — It suits him to stay home. (clitic + impersonal)

Other personal forms (*convenho, convéns, convimos*) are rare in conversation.

### Exemplar sentences

- *O jornalista **interveio** na discussão.* — The journalist intervened in the discussion.
- *O dinheiro **provém** do governo.* — The money comes from the government.
- *Esse problema **adveio** de um mal-entendido.* — This problem arose from a misunderstanding. (formal)
- *Sobreveio uma tempestade durante a viagem.* — A storm came over during the trip. (literary)
- *Convém que vocês **cheguem** cedo.* — It's advisable that you arrive early.
- *O presidente **interveio** para resolver o impasse.* — The president intervened to resolve the deadlock.

## Traps

- **Forgetting the 3sg acute / 3pl circumflex accent.** *Convem, intervem, provem* without the accent are wrong. Same rule as ter family — drill them together.
- **Convir is mostly used impersonally in modern Portuguese.** Don't drill the full personal paradigm as primary content; the impersonal *convém que* + subjuntivo is the productive form.
- **Sobrevir, advir are formal/literary.** Recognize when reading; don't force into production. *Sobreveio* in a casual conversation sounds bookish.
- **Particípio is *vindo*, not *vido*.** Compounds: *intervindo, convindo, provindo, advindo, sobrevindo*. Note that *vindo* doubles as both particípio of vir AND gerúndio of vir — context disambiguates.
- **Don't confuse with vir-itself in compound tenses.** *Tenho vindo* (I've been coming) uses ter as auxiliary + vir's particípio (*vindo*). Vir-derivatives in compound tenses use the same pattern: *Tenho intervindo, tinha convindo*.
- **Vir family vs ver family.** Both look similar: *vir* (to come) vs *ver* (to see). Conjugations diverge entirely (vens vs vês, vim vs vi, vinha vs via, venha vs veja). The vir family takes derivatives; the ver family doesn't form a parallel derivative system.
