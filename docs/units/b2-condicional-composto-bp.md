## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the condicional composto (teria/terias/teria/teríamos/teriam + particípio) in BP
- Use it as the main-clause verb in counterfactual past conditionals (Type 3 — see `b2-cond-contra-bp`)
- Express past hypothetical consequences ("would have done") and future-in-the-past in reported speech
- Recognize that EP often substitutes mais-que-perfeito do indicativo (tinha falado) for the same meaning — a real BP/EP divergence

## Vocabulary

- **teria / terias / teria / teríamos / teriam + particípio** — auxiliary + past participle
- **haveria + particípio** — exists but essentially literary; use *teria* in BP

## Grammar

Form: condicional simples of *ter* + particípio passado.

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | teria | falado |
| você / ele / ela | teria | falado |
| nós | teríamos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | teriam | falado |

The form expresses a hypothetical past consequence — what *would have* happened *if* something had been different. It also appears in reported speech as the future-in-the-past.

### Three primary uses

**1. Main clause of Type 3 conditional — see `b2-cond-contra-bp`**

- *Se eu tivesse estudado mais, **teria passado**.* — If I had studied more, I would have passed.
- *Se ela tivesse vindo, **teríamos comemorado** juntos.* — If she had come, we would have celebrated together.

**2. Past hypothetical consequence (no explicit *se*-clause)**

- *Eu **teria ido** à festa, mas estava doente.* — I would have gone to the party, but I was sick.
- *Você **teria feito** o mesmo no meu lugar?* — Would you have done the same in my place?
- *Nós **teríamos comprado** a casa, mas o preço subiu.* — We would have bought the house, but the price went up.

**3. Future-in-the-past in reported speech**

- *Ele disse que **teria terminado** até hoje.* — He said he would have finished by today.
- *Ela prometeu que **teria respondido** logo.* — She promised she would have answered soon.

### BP/EP divergence

This is one of the sharpest BP/EP variant points covered in the curriculum:

| Variant | Form for "I would have gone" |
|---|---|
| BP | *Eu **teria ido**.* (condicional composto) |
| EP (spoken) | *Eu **tinha ido**.* (mais-que-perfeito do indicativo as substitute) |
| EP (formal) | *Eu **teria ido**.* (also acceptable) |

EP students learn this divergence in their twin unit (Cluster 5 backlog). BP students should produce *teria* exclusively and recognize the EP *tinha* substitution when listening or reading.

## Traps

- **Don't substitute *tinha* for *teria* in BP main clauses.** *Se eu tivesse estudado, tinha passado* sounds EP-flavored to BP ears. BP keeps *teria passado*.
- **Distinguish from condicional simples (faria vs teria feito).** *Eu faria* = I would do (hypothetical present/future). *Eu teria feito* = I would have done (counterfactual past). Different time anchors.
- **Don't use it for unrealized future plans.** *Vou ter feito* (future perfect with *ir*) or *terei feito* (futuro composto, see `b2-futuro-composto-ind`) — those are forward-looking. *Teria feito* is backward-looking, counterfactual.
- **Particípios irregulares.** *Teria dito, teria feito, teria posto, teria visto* — same set as the other compounds.
- **Stays variant: bp.** The form *teria* is grammatically available in EP but practically substituted by *tinha* in spoken EP — the EP twin (Cluster 5 backlog) will document the substitution pattern.
