## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can recognize and form the **condicional composto** (compound conditional) and use it as the main-clause verb in counterfactual past conditionals.

## Vocabulary

- **teria + particípio** — conditional auxiliary + past participle
- *haveria + particípio* exists but is essentially literary; use *teria* in BP

## Grammar

Formed with condicional simples of *ter* + past participle:

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | teria | falado |
| você / ele / ela | teria | falado |
| nós | teríamos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | teriam | falado |

### Minimal example

- *Se eu tivesse estudado mais, **teria passado**.* — If I had studied more, I would have passed.

The form's primary use is in Type 3 (counterfactual past) conditionals — see `b2-cond-contra-bp`. It also pairs with reported speech and unrealized past expectations.
