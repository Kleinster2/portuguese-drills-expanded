## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the mais-que-perfeito do subjuntivo (tivesse/tivesses/tivesse/tivéssemos/tivessem + particípio)
- Use it as the *se*-clause in counterfactual past conditionals (Type 3 — see `b2-cond-contra-bp`)
- Use it after subjuntivo triggers in past time (e.g., *queria que ele tivesse vindo*)
- Use it after concessive/conditional conjunctions in past contexts (*embora tivesse, mesmo que tivesse*)

## Vocabulary

- **tivesse / tivesses / tivesse / tivéssemos / tivessem + particípio** — auxiliary + past participle
- **houvesse + particípio** — formal/literary alternative; rare in spoken use, common in written prose

## Grammar

Form: imperfeito do subjuntivo of *ter* (or *haver*) + particípio passado.

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | tivesse | falado |
| você / ele / ela | tivesse | falado |
| nós | tivéssemos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | tivessem | falado |

The form expresses a hypothetical, counterfactual, or wished-for past event. It marks the *se*-clause of Type 3 conditionals and appears under any subjuntivo trigger when the action is anchored in the past.

### Three primary uses

**1. Counterfactual past conditionals (Type 3) — see `b2-cond-contra-bp`**

- *Se eu **tivesse estudado** mais, teria passado.* — If I had studied more, I would have passed.
- *Se ela **tivesse vindo** ontem, teríamos comemorado.* — If she had come yesterday, we would have celebrated.

**2. After subjuntivo triggers in past contexts**

- *Eu queria que ele **tivesse vindo**.* — I wished he had come.
- *Era importante que vocês **tivessem chegado** a tempo.* — It was important you had arrived on time.
- *Duvidava que ele **tivesse feito** isso.* — I doubted he had done that.

**3. After concessive / conditional conjunctions in past time**

- *Embora **tivesse chovido**, saímos.* — Even though it had rained, we went out.
- *Mesmo que **tivessem avisado**, eu não teria ido.* — Even if they had warned me, I wouldn't have gone.

### Houvesse vs tivesse

Both forms are grammatical and interchangeable. *Tivesse* dominates in spoken usage and most modern writing. *Houvesse* appears in formal/literary register and legal/bureaucratic text:

- *Se eu **tivesse** sabido…* — natural in any register
- *Se eu **houvesse** sabido…* — formal, literary

## Traps

- **Don't substitute mais-que-perfeito do indicativo (tinha falado) in subjuntivo contexts.** *Se eu tinha estudado, teria passado* is wrong — the *se*-clause requires subjuntivo. (EP allows *tinha* in spoken usage as a substitute, but the prescriptive form and the BP norm is *tivesse*.)
- **Don't use simple imperfeito do subjuntivo where a past anchor exists.** *Se eu estudasse, teria passado* mixes Type 2's *se*-clause with Type 3's main clause — confusing. For counterfactual past, use *tivesse estudado*; for irreal present/future, use *estudasse*.
- **Particípios irregulares.** *Tivesse dito, tivesse feito, tivesse posto, tivesse visto, tivesse aberto* — review the irregular set before drilling.
