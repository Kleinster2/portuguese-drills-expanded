## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Recognize the conjunctions that always take subjuntivo, regardless of the main clause
- Use these to construct purpose clauses (*para que*), concessive clauses (*embora*), conditional clauses (*a menos que*, *contanto que*), and time clauses (*antes que*, *até que*)
- Distinguish always-subjuntivo conjunctions from conjunctions that take indicative or both

## Vocabulary

Conjunctions that **always** take subjuntivo:

- **para que** — so that, in order that
- **a fim de que** — so that (more formal)
- **antes que** — before (in time)
- **sem que** — without (someone doing something)
- **embora** — although, even though
- **ainda que** — even if
- **mesmo que** — even if
- **a menos que** — unless
- **a não ser que** — unless
- **contanto que** — provided that
- **caso** — in case (alternative to *se* + subj)
- **por mais que** — no matter how much

## Grammar

Pattern: **[conjunction] + sujeito + verbo no subjuntivo**.

These conjunctions trigger subjuntivo categorically — there is no contrast with indicative. The subjuntivo expresses the projected, hypothetical, or contrary-to-fact nature of the subordinate clause.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Vou estudar **para que eu passe** no exame.* — I'll study so I pass the exam.
- *Saiu **antes que pudéssemos** despedir-nos.* — He left before we could say goodbye. (imperfeito subj because main is preterite)
- *Ela ajudou **sem que eu pedisse**.* — She helped without my asking.
- *Embora **chova**, vou sair.* — Even though it's raining, I'm going out.
- *Mesmo que ele **venha**, não vou ficar.* — Even if he comes, I won't stay.
- *A menos que você **mude** de ideia, fica decidido.* — Unless you change your mind, it's decided.
- *Contanto que vocês **avisem**, podemos remarcar.* — Provided you let us know, we can reschedule.
- *Caso **chova**, levo guarda-chuva.* — In case it rains, I'll take an umbrella.

### Tense agreement

When the main clause is in the past, the subordinate uses imperfeito subjuntivo:

- *Eu estudei **para que passasse** no exame.* — I studied so I would pass the exam.
- *Ela ajudou **sem que eu pedisse**.* — She helped without my asking.

## Traps

- **Para que vs para + infinitivo.** Same subject across both clauses → infinitive (*estudei **para passar** no exame*). Different subjects → *para que* + subjuntivo (*ele estudou **para que eu passasse**, dando o exemplo*). The infinitive form is far more common in BP for same-subject cases.
- **Embora ≠ porque.** *Embora chova* (subjuntivo, even though). *Porque chove* (indicative, because). Easy to confuse the structures.
- *Antes que* triggers subjuntivo; *depois que* triggers indicative (factual sequence): *antes que ele chegue, vou sair* vs *depois que ele chegou, saí*.
- **BP register.** Several of these conjunctions (*a fim de que*, *ainda que*) sound formal/written. Spoken BP often substitutes *para*, *mesmo se*, etc. Teach the full set; flag register.
