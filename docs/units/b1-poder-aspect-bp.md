## Outcomes

- Distinguish **pude** (managed to, succeeded in doing on a specific occasion) from **podia** (was able to, had the general ability or context)
- Express past attempts and accomplishments precisely
- Recognize that English "I could" is ambiguous and forces a context-driven choice

## Vocabulary

Triggering markers for **pude** (specific accomplishment):
- ontem, finalmente, depois de muito esforço
- só / apenas (only then)
- conseguir + infinitivo (often paraphrasable as *consegui* — same aspect)
- Negative: *não pude* = didn't manage to / wasn't able to (this time)

Triggering markers for **podia** (general ability or background context):
- sempre, quando era jovem, naquela época
- antigamente, no meu tempo
- background descriptions of what was possible (often paired with another past tense)

## Grammar

- **pude** = managed to / succeeded in → completed action, specific moment, often after effort
- **podia** = was able to / had the ability → ongoing capacity, context, no specific outcome in focus

### Exemplar pairs

- *Finalmente **pude** terminar o relatório.* — I finally managed to finish the report.
- *Quando eu era jovem, **podia** correr 10 km sem parar.* — When I was young, I could run 10 km without stopping.

- *Não **pude** ir à festa, tive que trabalhar.* — I couldn't make it to the party, I had to work. (specific occasion)
- *Naquela época, eu não **podia** sair sozinho.* — Back then, I wasn't allowed to go out alone. (ongoing context)

- *Só ontem **pude** falar com ele.* — Only yesterday did I manage to talk to him.
- *Eu **podia** falar com ele sempre que quisesse.* — I could talk to him whenever I wanted. (general access)

- *Você **pôde** ver o jogo?* — Were you able to catch the game? (this time)
- *No domingo eu sempre **podia** ver o jogo.* — On Sundays I could always watch the game. (habitual)

## Traps

- **"I could" is the trap.** English collapses both readings. Default-translating to *podia* often loses the "managed to" reading. If the sentence implies effort, completion, or a specific occasion, switch to *pude*.
- **Negative is especially tricky.** *Não pude* = didn't manage to (specific). *Não podia* = wasn't allowed to / wasn't able to (general). "I couldn't come yesterday" → *Não pude vir ontem*, not *não podia vir ontem*.
- *Pude* and *consegui* (preterite of *conseguir*) are near-synonyms in this aspect. Many native speakers use *consegui* even where *pude* would work.
- *Pôde* (third person, with the circumflex) is often misspelled or pronounced like *pode* (present); the accent is the only marker.
