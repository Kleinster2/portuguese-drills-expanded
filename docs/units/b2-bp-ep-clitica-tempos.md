## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Map the five major BP/EP grammatical-divergence dimensions side-by-side: clitic placement, progressive aspect, *tenho falado* register, Type-2 conditional substitution, Type-3 conditional substitution
- Read a passage and identify the variant from clitic placement and tense choice alone
- Switch production register between variants without carrying over wrong-variant patterns

## Vocabulary

The five divergence dimensions, with the canonical contrast pair for each:

| Dimension | BP form | EP form |
|---|---|---|
| Clitic placement (default) | proclisis (*me chamo*) | enclisis (*chamo-me*) |
| Progressive aspect | *estar + gerúndio* (*estou falando*) | *estar a + infinitivo* (*estou a falar*) |
| *Tenho falado* | requires recency anchor (*tenho ido ultimamente*) | free-standing (*tenho ido*) |
| Type-2 conditional | condicional simples (*viajaria*) | imperfeito do indicativo (*viajava*) — colloquial |
| Type-3 conditional | condicional composto (*teria passado*) | mais-que-perfeito do indicativo (*tinha passado*) — colloquial |

## Grammar

### Dimension 1 — Clitic placement

The single sharpest BP/EP divergence in syntax. **EP defaults to enclisis; BP defaults to proclisis** in spoken language. The trigger words that force proclisis (negação, conjunções subordinativas, advérbios de inclusão, pronomes interrogativos / relativos / indefinidos) are the same in both variants — but the unmarked default differs:

- BP: *Eu **me chamo** João.* (proclisis even with no trigger)
- EP: *Chamo-me João.* (enclisis is default)
- Both: *Não **me chamo** João.* (proclisis triggered by *não* in both variants)

Mesoclisis (*chamar-me-ei*) survives in formal EP, dead in BP.

See [[a2-reflexivos-bp]], [[a2-reflexivos-ep]], [[a2-dop-bp]], [[a2-dop-ep]], [[a2-iop-bp]], [[a2-iop-ep]] for full per-variant detail.

### Dimension 2 — Progressive aspect

Same meaning, two structurally different constructions:

- BP: *Estou **falando** com ela.* — I'm speaking with her.
- EP: *Estou **a falar** com ela.* — Same meaning.

The construction is identical across all tenses (estava + gerúndio / estava a + infinitivo, estarei + gerúndio / estarei a + infinitivo, etc.).

Geographic note: some EP regions (Alentejo, Azores) historically used the gerund; standard EP (Lisbon norm) uses *estar a + infinitivo*. BP universally uses the gerund.

See [[a2-estar-gerundio-bp]] and [[a2-estar-a-infinitivo-ep]].

### Dimension 3 — Pretérito perfeito composto register

Same form (*tenho + particípio*), divergent natural-usage pattern:

| Form | BP reading | EP reading |
|---|---|---|
| *Tenho falado.* | sounds incomplete; needs *ultimamente* | natural — "I've been speaking [recently]" |
| *Tenho falado ultimamente.* | habitual recent action | same meaning, adverb optional |

A BP speaker hearing free-standing *tenho falado* parses it as somewhat formal/EP-flavored. An EP speaker hearing *tenho falado ultimamente* finds the adverb redundant.

See [[b1-pret-perfeito-composto-bp]] and [[b1-pret-perfeito-composto-ep]].

### Dimension 4 — Type-2 conditional substitution

Type 2 (irreal present/future) main clause:

| Variant | Standard form | Substitute |
|---|---|---|
| BP | *Se eu fosse rico, **viajaria**.* | (no substitute) |
| EP formal | *Se eu fosse rico, **viajaria**.* | — |
| EP colloquial | *Se eu fosse rico, **viajava**.* | imperfeito do indicativo |

Both EP forms are productive native usage. The substitution is broad (applies across most verbs in colloquial EP) but specifically excludes the *se*-clause (which stays imperfeito subjuntivo: *se fosse*, never *se era*).

See [[b2-cond-irreal-bp]] and [[b2-cond-irreal-ep]].

### Dimension 5 — Type-3 conditional substitution

Same register split, different verb forms:

| Variant | Standard form | Substitute |
|---|---|---|
| BP | *Se eu tivesse estudado, **teria passado**.* | (no substitute) |
| EP formal | *Se eu tivesse estudado, **teria passado**.* | — |
| EP colloquial | *Se eu tivesse estudado, **tinha passado**.* | mais-que-perfeito do indicativo |

A BP speaker hearing *tinha passado* in this context parses it as past pluperfect (he had passed — strange) and may not recognize it as a conditional substitute. An EP speaker hears it as natural Type-3 result clause.

See [[b2-cond-contra-bp]] and [[b2-cond-contra-ep]].

### Dimension 6 (auxiliary) — Polite-request register

Smaller divergence, but real: EP uses imperfeito-de-cortesia substitution more pervasively than BP, especially for the high-frequency request set (*queria, gostava, podia, devia*).

- BP polite: *Eu **queria** um café.* (also *Eu **gostaria** de um café*)
- EP polite: *Eu **queria** um café.* (essentially mandatory; *quereria* is prescriptively correct but practically dead)

See [[b1-condicional-bp]] and [[b1-condicional-ep]].

### Identifying variant from a passage

Quick diagnostic: look for clitic placement first.

- *Eu te ligo amanhã.* → BP (proclisis with no trigger)
- *Ligo-te amanhã.* → EP (enclisis as default)
- *Estou trabalhando muito.* → BP (gerund)
- *Estou a trabalhar muito.* → EP (a + infinitive)
- *Se pudesse, ia.* → likely EP (Type-2 imperfeito substitution)
- *Se pudesse, iria.* → BP, or formal EP
- *Tenho lido este livro.* → likely EP (no recency anchor)
- *Tenho lido este livro ultimamente.* → could be either

## Traps

- **Don't import wrong-variant clitic placement.** A BP-trained student writing EP and forgetting to switch to enclisis sounds Brazilian-on-paper. An EP-trained student speaking BP and using enclisis sounds bookish. Both errors are register-marked.
- **Gerund vs *a +* infinitive isn't optional.** *Estou falando* in EP sounds Brazilian; *Estou a falar* in BP sounds Portuguese. The forms aren't interchangeable for naturalness, only for grammar.
- **The Type 2/3 substitution applies ONLY to the main clause.** The *se*-clause stays imperfeito subjuntivo (Type 2) or mais-que-perfeito subjuntivo (Type 3) in both variants. *Se eu era rico* is wrong in any variant — substitution doesn't enter the *se*-clause.
- **The *tenho falado* register error.** Brazilian students writing EP-style *tenho falado* without an adverb sound stiff. EP students writing BP-style *tenho falado ultimamente* are correct but the adverb redundant.
- **Polite-request *queria* is shared, but EP relies on it more.** *Quereria* is dead in EP (real-session trap documented). *Gostaria* is sometimes substituted by *gostava* in EP. BP keeps both *queria* and *gostaria* alive in equivalent register.
- **Variant-identification is probabilistic, not deterministic.** A formal EP passage may use condicional simples (no Type-2 substitution) and look BP-like by tense. Use multiple cues — clitic placement is the most reliable single cue.
