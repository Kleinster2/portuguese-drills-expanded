## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the futuro composto do subjuntivo (tiver/tiveres/tiver/tivermos/tiverem + particípio)
- Use it after temporal and conditional conjunctions (*quando, assim que, depois que, se*) to mark **a completed action in the future**
- Distinguish it from the simple futuro do subjuntivo (which marks a future action without completion focus)

## Vocabulary

- **tiver / tiveres / tiver / tivermos / tiverem + particípio** — auxiliary + past participle
- Triggering conjunctions:
  - *quando* — when
  - *assim que / logo que* — as soon as
  - *depois que* — after
  - *se* — if (real conditional with completion reading)

## Grammar

Form: futuro do subjuntivo of *ter* + particípio passado.

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | tiver | falado |
| você / ele / ela | tiver | falado |
| nós | tivermos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | tiverem | falado |

The form is triggered by certain conjunctions that take futuro do subjuntivo (covered in `b2-subj-futuro`). The compound version adds the meaning of **completion** — the subordinate action will have been finished by the time of the main action.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Quando você **tiver acabado**, me chame.* — When you've finished, call me.
- *Assim que ele **tiver chegado**, começamos a reunião.* — As soon as he's arrived, we start the meeting.
- *Depois que vocês **tiverem terminado**, podemos ir.* — After you've finished, we can go.
- *Se você **tiver lido** o livro, podemos discutir.* — If you've read the book, we can discuss it.
- *Logo que eu **tiver feito** as compras, te ligo.* — As soon as I've done the shopping, I'll call you.
- *Quando ela **tiver respondido** o e-mail, te aviso.* — When she's answered the email, I'll let you know.

### Simple vs compound future subjunctive

| Form | Reading |
|---|---|
| *Quando você **chegar**, te ligo.* | When you arrive — single future action |
| *Quando você **tiver chegado**, te ligo.* | When you've arrived — completion focus |

Both are correct after *quando*; the compound emphasizes completion before the main action. In casual speech, the simple form often suffices; the compound is precision-marking.

## Traps

- **Don't use indicativo after *quando, assim que, depois que* in future contexts.** *Quando eu terei terminado* — wrong; subjuntivo required after these conjunctions when pointing to a future event. *Quando eu tiver terminado* — correct.
- **Distinguish from simple futuro do subjuntivo.** *Tiver* (compound auxiliary) + particípio is the future perfect subjunctive. *Tiver* alone (1st/3rd person sg of futuro do subj of *ter*) means simply "will have." Both forms use *tiver*, but only the compound has a particípio attached.
- **The compound is more common than the indicative future-perfect (terei feito).** Speakers reach for *quando eu tiver feito* far more often than for *terei feito X amanhã*. The subjunctive form has a clear conjunction trigger; the indicative form depends on context.
- **EP and BP usage are essentially identical** for this form — variant: shared.
