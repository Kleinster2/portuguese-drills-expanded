## Outcomes

- Ask what time it is
- Tell time on the hour
- Tell time with minutes (past and to the hour)
- Use special expressions (meio-dia, meia-noite)
- Distinguish AM from PM (da manhã/da tarde/da noite)
- Schedule appointments and meetings

## Vocabulary

- **hora/horas** (hour/hours, o'clock)
- **meio-dia** (noon - literally "half-day")
- **meia-noite** (midnight - literally "half-night")
- **meio/meia** (half)
- **quinze** (quarter - 15 minutes)
- **menos** (minus, to - for "to the hour")
- **para** (to - alternate for "to the hour")
- **em ponto** (on the dot, exactly)
- **da manhã** (in the morning - AM)
- **da tarde** (in the afternoon - PM)
- **da noite** (at night - PM)
- **madrugada** (early morning hours - 1-5 AM)

## Grammar

- **Question:** Que horas são? (What time is it? - literally "What hours are they?")
  - Note: PLURAL "são" because time is plural concept
- **Exception:** É uma hora (It's 1 o'clock - SINGULAR for 1:00 only!)
- **Structure patterns:**
  - **On the hour:** São [number] horas
  - **Past the hour:** São [hour] e [minutes]
  - **Half past:** São [hour] e meia (literally "and half")
  - **Quarter past:** São [hour] e quinze
  - **To the hour:** São [minutes] para as [next hour] OR Faltam [minutes] para as [next hour]
- **12-hour system** (Brazil uses 12-hour more commonly than 24-hour in speech)
