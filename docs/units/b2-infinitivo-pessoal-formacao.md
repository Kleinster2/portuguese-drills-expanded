## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the **personal infinitive** for any verb — regular and "irregular" alike (the personal-infinitive paradigm is highly regular)
- Recognize the difference between the bare infinitive (*falar*) and the inflected forms (*falares, falarmos, falarem*)
- Distinguish the personal-infinitive forms from the present-subjuntivo forms in same-form cells (1st-person sing and 3rd-person sing collide on form for many verbs)

## Vocabulary

The personal infinitive is a finite-like form of the infinitive that inflects for person and number. The endings:

- **eu** → no ending (bare infinitive)
- **tu** → -es
- **você / ele / ela** → no ending (bare infinitive)
- **nós** → -mos
- **vocês / eles / elas** → -em

(*vós* form *-des* is archaic and excluded from modern usage.)

## Grammar

### Regular paradigm

The personal infinitive of any -ar / -er / -ir verb is built by appending the personal endings to the **bare infinitive**. No stem change — just additions.

| Pessoa | falar (-AR) | comer (-ER) | partir (-IR) |
|---|---|---|---|
| eu | falar | comer | partir |
| tu | falares | comeres | partires |
| você / ele / ela | falar | comer | partir |
| nós | falarmos | comermos | partirmos |
| vocês / eles / elas | falarem | comerem | partirem |

### "Irregular" verbs follow the same pattern

Verbs that are highly irregular in other tenses (ser, ter, ir, vir, fazer, pôr) **become regular in personal infinitive** — just append the endings to the bare infinitive:

| Pessoa | ser | ter | ir | vir | fazer | pôr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| eu | ser | ter | ir | vir | fazer | pôr |
| tu | seres | teres | ires | vires | fazeres | pores |
| você / ele / ela | ser | ter | ir | vir | fazer | pôr |
| nós | sermos | termos | irmos | virmos | fazermos | pormos |
| vocês / eles / elas | serem | terem | irem | virem | fazerem | porem |

Note: the circumflex on the bare *pôr* form drops in the inflected forms (*pores, pormos, porem*) because those forms are no longer monosyllabic.

### Same pattern for derivatives

Derivatives of pôr / ter / vir inherit:

- compor → compores, compormos, comporem
- manter → manteres, mantermos, manterem
- intervir → intervires, intervirmos, intervirem

### Distinguishing from subjuntivo presente

Several personal-infinitive forms are identical to subjuntivo presente forms — disambiguation is contextual, not formal.

For **-AR verbs**, the forms diverge cleanly:

| Pessoa | personal infinitive (falar) | subjuntivo presente (falar) |
|---|---|---|
| eu | falar | fale |
| tu | falares | fales |
| ele / ela | falar | fale |
| nós | falarmos | falemos |
| eles / elas | falarem | falem |

For **-ER / -IR verbs**, the eu and 3sg forms collide with subjuntivo presente — distinguished only by trigger context:

| Pessoa | personal infinitive (comer) | subjuntivo presente (comer) |
|---|---|---|
| eu | comer | coma |
| tu | comeres | comas |
| ele / ela | comer | coma |
| nós | comermos | comamos |
| eles / elas | comerem | comam |

Wait — these *don't* collide for -ER/-IR because subj-pres has its own endings. Let me re-check.

Actually the collision is at the **futuro do subjuntivo** for regular -er/-ir verbs:

| Pessoa | personal infinitive (comer) | futuro do subjuntivo (comer) |
|---|---|---|
| eu | comer | comer |
| tu | comeres | comeres |
| ele / ela | comer | comer |
| nós | comermos | comermos |
| eles / elas | comerem | comerem |

The forms are **identical** — only context (the trigger) tells them apart:

- *Quando comermos…* — futuro do subjuntivo (after temporal conjunction *quando*)
- *Para comermos…* — personal infinitive (after preposition *para* with subject)

This is the most common confusion point and is covered in the use unit (`b2-infinitivo-pessoal-uso`).

## Traps

- **Don't try to make the "irregular" verbs irregular here.** Personal infinitive of *ser* is *ser/seres/ser/sermos/serem* — no *seja-/foss-* stems. The verb's other-tense irregularity doesn't carry into personal infinitive.
- **The personal infinitive of pôr drops the circumflex in inflected forms.** *Pôr* (bare) but *pores, pormos, porem* (inflected). The circumflex marks contrastive stress on the monosyllable; once the form has additional syllables, the accent falls naturally without needing the diacritic.
- **Form collision with futuro do subjuntivo for -ER/-IR verbs is real.** *Comermos* is both personal infinitive and futuro do subjuntivo for *comer*. Disambiguation is by trigger: temporal/conditional conjunctions (*quando, se, assim que*) signal futuro do subjuntivo; prepositions or impersonal expressions signal personal infinitive. Covered in `b2-infinitivo-pessoal-uso`.
- **For -AR verbs, the form is unique** — no collision with subjuntivo or futuro do subjuntivo. *Falarmos* unambiguously means personal infinitive (futuro do subj would be *falarmos* — actually wait, futuro do subj of *falar* is also *falarmos*. Same collision!). Recheck: yes, the collision applies across all conjugations. Disambiguation is always by trigger context.
- **The personal-infinitive form of vir is *virmos*, NOT *vimos*.** *Vimos* is the present-indicative 1pl of *vir*. *Virmos* is the personal infinitive 1pl. The extra *r* matters.
