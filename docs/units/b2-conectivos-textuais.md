## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use higher-register textual connectives across six categories — additive, contrastive, causal, consecutive, enumerative, conclusive
- Choose connectives appropriate to the register (academic / journalistic / business / informal essay)
- Avoid repetition fatigue by varying connectives within a single text

## Vocabulary

The connective inventory, organized by category. Forms in **bold** are the most-used in formal writing; the rest are register/style alternatives.

### Additives — adding information

- **além disso** — in addition / moreover
- **ademais** — furthermore (more formal)
- **mais ainda** — even more so
- **igualmente** — likewise
- **do mesmo modo / da mesma forma** — in the same way
- **paralelamente** — in parallel
- **bem como** — as well as

### Contrastives — opposing information

- **contudo** — however
- **no entanto** — nevertheless
- **todavia** — yet
- **por outro lado** — on the other hand
- **em contrapartida** — in counterpart / on the other hand
- **ao invés de** — instead of
- **pelo contrário** — on the contrary

### Causals — introducing cause

- **visto que** — given that
- **dado que** — given that (slightly more formal)
- **uma vez que** — once / given that
- **na medida em que** — to the extent that
- **devido a / em virtude de** — due to / by virtue of (with noun phrase)
- **graças a** — thanks to

### Consecutives — introducing consequence

- **por conseguinte** — consequently
- **logo** — therefore (also temporal)
- **portanto** — therefore
- **assim sendo / assim** — thus
- **dessa forma / desse modo** — in this way
- **em consequência (disso)** — as a consequence

### Enumeratives — ordering points

- **em primeiro lugar / primeiramente** — firstly
- **em segundo lugar** — secondly
- **a seguir / em seguida** — next
- **por último / finalmente** — finally / lastly
- **por fim** — at the end / lastly

### Conclusives — summarizing

- **em suma** — in sum
- **em síntese** — in synthesis
- **em conclusão** — in conclusion
- **em resumo** — in short
- **enfim** — in short / finally (mid-discourse marker)
- **resumindo** — to summarize

## Grammar

These connectives operate at the **text level** — they organize multi-sentence or multi-paragraph flow rather than connecting single clauses. Most function as parenthetical adverbials and require comma punctuation.

### Standard punctuation pattern

A connective opening a sentence usually takes a comma after:

- *Além disso, é necessário considerar a opinião dos especialistas.*
- *Contudo, a evidência aponta noutra direção.*
- *Em primeiro lugar, devemos definir o problema.*

A connective inserted mid-sentence typically takes commas around:

- *A evidência, contudo, aponta noutra direção.*
- *Devemos, em primeiro lugar, definir o problema.*

### Register match

Connectives carry register weight. Mixing high-formal connectives with informal style sounds incongruous:

| Register | Typical connective set |
|---|---|
| Casual essay | mas, então, e, depois, no fim |
| Academic / formal | além disso, contudo, por conseguinte, em síntese |
| Journalistic | no entanto, por outro lado, em consequência |
| Business correspondence | adicionalmente, em contrapartida, por fim |

A formal essay reaching for *então* repeatedly sounds underdressed; a casual blog post over-using *por conseguinte* sounds pretentious.

### Avoiding repetition fatigue

Within a single text, vary connectives across categories. Don't stack three *contudo*s in three sentences — use *no entanto*, *por outro lado*, or restructure.

### Exemplar paragraph (mid-formal)

> *A inovação tecnológica trouxe enormes ganhos de produtividade. **Além disso**, criou novos mercados de trabalho que não existiam há uma década. **Contudo**, a transição também produziu perdas significativas em setores tradicionais. **Por conseguinte**, qualquer política pública nesta área deve equilibrar inovação e proteção social. **Em suma**, o desafio é económico e ético ao mesmo tempo.*

The five connectives mark text structure cleanly: addition (além disso) → contrast (contudo) → consequence (por conseguinte) → conclusion (em suma).

## Traps

- **Don't mix register.** *Então, podemos concluir que…* sounds informal in academic writing — use *Por conseguinte* or *Portanto*. Conversely, *por conseguinte* in a personal email sounds stiff.
- **Don't over-use one connective.** Three *contudo*s in three sentences signals a mechanical writer. Vary across category-mates (*no entanto, por outro lado, todavia*) or restructure to remove some.
- **Punctuation matters.** Connectives are parenthetical — comma after when sentence-initial, commas around when mid-sentence. *Além disso* (with the comma) signals "moving to a related point"; without the comma the reader stumbles.
- **Logo has two meanings — temporal and consequential.** *Logo depois* (right after — temporal). *Logo, podemos concluir* (therefore — consequential). Disambiguation by context.
- **Visto que / dado que / uma vez que are causal, NOT temporal.** All three mean "given that" or "since [as cause]" — they don't mark time. Don't substitute for *desde que* (temporal — covered in [[b2-conj-condicionais]]).
- **Bem como is not the same as e (and).** *Bem como* implies "as well as" with a parallel-listing flavor; *e* is the simple coordinator. *X bem como Y* sounds more formal and emphasizes the equivalent status of X and Y. Don't substitute *bem como* for *e* in casual lists.
