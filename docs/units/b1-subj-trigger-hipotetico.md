## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form hypothetical conditions of the *Type 2* shape: **se + imperfeito subjuntivo + condicional**
- Distinguish hypothetical *se* (imagined situation → subjuntivo) from real-conditional *se* (factual → indicative or futuro do subjuntivo)
- Use this construction to express imagined preferences, plans, and unrealized situations

## Vocabulary

Trigger: **se** + imperfeito subjuntivo, paired with the conditional in the main clause.

Common imperfeito subjuntivo forms (review from `b1-subj-imperfeito`):

- ser → fosse, fosses, fosse, fôssemos, fossem
- ter → tivesse
- estar → estivesse
- fazer → fizesse
- poder → pudesse
- saber → soubesse

## Grammar

Pattern: **se + imperfeito subjuntivo, [main clause em condicional]**.

The hypothetical is unreal or contrary-to-fact at the moment of speaking. *Se eu fosse rico* doesn't claim wealth — it imagines an alternative state.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Se eu **fosse** rico, **viajaria** pelo mundo.* — If I were rich, I'd travel the world.
- *Se ela **soubesse** a verdade, **me contaria**.* — If she knew the truth, she'd tell me.
- *Se **chovesse** amanhã, eu **ficaria** em casa.* — If it rained tomorrow, I'd stay home.
- *Se você **estivesse** aqui, **veria** com seus próprios olhos.* — If you were here, you'd see with your own eyes.
- *O que você **faria** se **ganhasse** na loteria?* — What would you do if you won the lottery?

### Real conditional contrast

When the *se*-clause describes a real or future-likely event, **not** a hypothetical, BP uses **futuro do subjuntivo** (covered in `b2-subj-futuro`) — not imperfeito subjuntivo:

- *Se **chover** amanhã, fico em casa.* — If it rains tomorrow, I'll stay home. (real future condition: futuro do subjuntivo)
- *Se **chovesse** amanhã, ficaria em casa.* — If it rained tomorrow, I'd stay home. (hypothetical: imperfeito subjuntivo)

## Traps

- **Don't use indicative after hypothetical *se*.** *Se eu **era** rico, viajaria* is wrong. Hypothetical = subjuntivo always.
- **The two conditional types collapse for English speakers.** English "if I were rich" and "if it rains" both use "if" + present, leaving the modality to the main clause. Portuguese splits them: hypothetical → imperfeito subj, real → futuro do subj. Drill both side-by-side.
- **EP polite-request overlap.** EP often uses imperfeito subjuntivo where BP uses imperfeito (*se pudesses, vinhas comigo* in EP vs *se você pudesse, vinha comigo* in BP). Phase 5 EP twin will revisit.
- **The main-clause verb defaults to condicional**, not future. *Se eu fosse rico, vou viajar* is wrong; the hypothetical chains to *viajaria*.
