## Outcomes

- Distinguish **soube** (found out, learned at a specific moment) from **sabia** (knew, was already aware)
- Use temporal markers (ontem, sempre, quando, há anos) to signal which reading is intended
- Recognize that English "I knew" maps to either form depending on context

## Vocabulary

Triggering markers for **soube** (inception of knowledge):
- ontem, na semana passada, no mês passado
- quando + [event] (at the moment of...)
- só / finalmente (only then / finally)
- pelos jornais, pela Maria (through, from)

Triggering markers for **sabia** (state of knowledge):
- sempre, desde criança, desde sempre
- naquela época, quando era criança
- já (already, in the sense of "had known for a while")

## Grammar

The same verb shifts meaning by tense:

- **soube** = found out, learned, discovered → marks the moment knowledge was acquired
- **sabia** = knew → describes ongoing knowledge, with no inception event in focus

### Exemplar pairs

- *Eu **soube** ontem que ela está grávida.* — I found out yesterday that she's pregnant.
- *Eu **sabia** que ela estava grávida.* — I knew she was pregnant. (already knew)

- *Só **soube** do problema quando você me contou.* — I only found out about the problem when you told me.
- *Eu **sabia** do problema, mas não falei nada.* — I knew about the problem, but didn't say anything.

- *Quando você **soube** da notícia?* — When did you find out about the news?
- *Você **sabia** que ele mora aqui?* — Did you know he lives here?

- *Quando eu era criança, eu já **sabia** ler.* — When I was a child, I already knew how to read.

## Traps

- **English "I knew" is ambiguous.** Default-translating it to *eu sabia* is usually safe, but if the context is "I found out" (a moment of discovery), the correct form is *soube*. Listen for "when did you find out" → *quando você soube?*, not *quando você sabia?*
- **Time markers are the strongest disambiguator.** *Ontem*, *no mês passado*, *quando me contou* all push toward *soube*. *Sempre*, *desde*, *já* push toward *sabia*.
- The negation flips: *não sabia* = didn't know (state). *Não soube* = didn't find out, never learned about it.
