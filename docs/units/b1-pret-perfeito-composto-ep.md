## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the pretérito perfeito composto (tenho/tens/tem/temos/têm + particípio passado) in EP
- Use it freely for **action that has been happening up to and including the present** — the EP equivalent of English "have been doing"
- Recognize that EP often omits the recency adverb that BP requires; the compound form alone carries the recent-ongoing meaning

## Vocabulary

- **tenho / tens / tem / temos / têm + particípio** — auxiliary + past participle
- Recency anchors are *optional* in EP (unlike BP):
  - *ultimamente, recentemente, nestes últimos dias*
  - May appear, but the compound form alone implies "recently / lately"

## Grammar

Form: presente do indicativo of *ter* + particípio passado.

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | tenho | falado |
| tu | tens | falado |
| você / ele / ela | tem | falado |
| nós | temos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | têm | falado |

The EP reading is **continuous up to the present** — the action started in the past and either continues now or its effects are recent. EP uses the compound form much more freely than BP and doesn't need an obligatory adverb anchor.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Tenho trabalhado muito.* — I've been working a lot. (no adverb needed)
- *Tens estudado para o exame?* — Have you been studying for the exam?
- *Ela tem-me telefonado todos os dias.* — She's been calling me every day.
- *Temos visitado os avós.* — We've been visiting our grandparents.
- *Eles têm passado pouco tempo em casa.* — They've been spending little time at home.
- *Não tenho dormido bem.* — I haven't been sleeping well.

### Compare with BP usage

| Form | EP reading | BP reading |
|---|---|---|
| *Tenho trabalhado.* | I've been working (lately). | (sounds incomplete; needs *ultimamente*) |
| *Tenho lido este livro.* | I've been reading this book. | (incomplete; needs anchor) |
| *Tenho falado com ela.* | I've been talking to her recently. | (uncommon in BP; would prefer *Tenho conversado com ela ultimamente*) |

## Traps

- **The EP form is NOT the equivalent of English present perfect for one-time events.** *I have eaten the cake* (one-time, completed) → EP pretérito perfeito simples (*comi o bolo*), NOT *tenho comido o bolo*. The compound is specifically continuous/iterative.
- **Don't impose BP's obligatory adverb in EP production.** EP speakers say *Tenho trabalhado muito* without needing *ultimamente* — the recency reading is built into the form.
- **Clitic placement.** EP places clitics enclitically with the auxiliary in affirmative clauses: *Tenho-me sentido bem* (I've been feeling well), not *Eu me tenho sentido bem*. (Clitic-placement details belong to the EP-twin reflexive/object-pronoun units, when those land.)
- **Particípios irregulares.** Same set as BP — *dito, feito, posto, visto, escrito, aberto, ganho, gasto* — review before drilling.
