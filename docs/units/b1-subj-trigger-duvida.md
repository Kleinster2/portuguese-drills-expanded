## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use verbs of doubt and uncertainty (*duvidar, não acreditar, não pensar*) with subjuntivo presente
- Apply the **negation flip**: *acho que* (indicative, asserts a belief) → *não acho que* (subjuntivo, asserts the absence of belief)
- Use *talvez*, *é possível que*, *é provável que* and similar uncertainty markers correctly

## Vocabulary

Trigger expressions:

- **duvidar que** — to doubt that
- **não acreditar que** — to not believe that
- **não pensar / não achar que** — to not think that (negation flips trigger)
- **negar que** — to deny that
- **talvez** — maybe (no `que`)
- **é possível que** — it's possible that
- **é provável que** — it's likely that
- **pode ser que** — it might be that

## Grammar

Pattern: **[doubt expression] + (que) + sujeito + verbo no subjuntivo**.

The subjunctive marks that the speaker is *not committed* to the truth of the proposition. With *talvez*, no `que` appears — the subjunctive sits directly after the adverb.

### The negation flip

*Achar que* and *pensar que* are the most subtle case. In the affirmative, they assert a belief, so they take indicative. Negating them removes the assertion — the speaker is now denying belief, so subjuntivo:

- *Acho que ele **vem** amanhã.* — I think he's coming tomorrow. (assertion → indicative)
- *Não acho que ele **venha** amanhã.* — I don't think he's coming tomorrow. (denied assertion → subjuntivo)

The same flip applies to *acreditar que*, *crer que*, *parecer que* in the literal-perception sense.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Duvido que ele **chegue** a tempo.* — I doubt he'll arrive on time.
- *Não acredito que isso **seja** verdade.* — I don't believe that's true.
- *Talvez **chova** amanhã.* — Maybe it'll rain tomorrow.
- *É provável que ele **esteja** dormindo.* — He's probably sleeping.
- *Pode ser que **dê** tudo certo.* — Everything might work out.
- *O réu negou que **tivesse** participado.* — The defendant denied having taken part. (imperfeito subj because triggering verb is past)

## Traps

- **The negation flip is the single highest-frequency error.** Many students learn *acho que ele vem* (correct, indicative) and then say *não acho que ele vem* — wrong; it should be *não acho que ele venha*. Drill this contrast explicitly.
- *Talvez* takes subjuntivo when it precedes the verb. After the verb, indicative is fine: *Ele vem talvez amanhã* (indicative, *talvez* is just a side adverb).
- **BP indicative drift.** Casual BP sometimes uses indicative after *talvez*: *talvez ele vem*. Teach subjuntivo as the target.
- *Duvidar que* always takes subjuntivo, even when negated. *Não duvido que ele venha* — still subjuntivo, because the lexical content is "doubt." (Some grammars accept indicative for *não duvido*, but subjuntivo is safer.)
