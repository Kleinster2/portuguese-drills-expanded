## Outcomes

- Name 17 parts of a house/apartment
- Describe how many rooms their house has
- Say where they are in the house: "Estou no quarto"
- Use MORAR to say where they live
- Combine with numbers from Units 44-45

## Vocabulary

- **Parts of a house:**
  - a casa (house)
  - o apartamento (apartment)
  - o quarto (bedroom)
  - a sala (living room)
  - a sala de estar (living room - formal)
  - a sala de jantar (dining room)
  - a cozinha (kitchen)
  - o banheiro (bathroom)
  - o quarto de banho (bathroom - European Portuguese)
  - a garagem (garage)
  - o jardim (garden/yard)
  - a varanda (balcony/porch)
  - o corredor (hallway)
  - a escada (staircase)
  - o telhado (roof)
  - a porta (door)
  - a janela (window)

## Grammar

- **Using rooms with prepositions:**
  - **no** quarto (in the bedroom - masculine)
  - **na** cozinha (in the kitchen - feminine)
  - **na** sala (in the living room - feminine)
  - Remember: em + o = no, em + a = na

- **Expressing possession of rooms:**
  - Minha casa tem três quartos. (My house has three bedrooms.)
  - Meu apartamento tem dois banheiros. (My apartment has two bathrooms.)
  - Use TER (to have) to describe what's in the house

- **MORAR vs VIVER (to live):**
  - Both mean "to live" but slightly different usage:
  - **MORAR** = to reside, live in (more common for address)
    - "Eu moro em uma casa" (I live in a house)
    - "Eu moro no apartamento" (I live in the apartment)
  - **VIVER** = to live, exist (more general/philosophical)
    - "Eu vivo no Brasil" (I live in Brazil)

### Exemplar sentences

- Eu moro em uma casa. (I live in a house.)
- Minha casa tem três quartos. (My house has three bedrooms.)
- Eu estou na cozinha. (I'm in the kitchen.)
- O banheiro fica ao lado do quarto. (The bathroom is next to the bedroom.)
- Meu apartamento tem uma varanda. (My apartment has a balcony.)
- A sala é grande. (The living room is big.)
- Onde está o banheiro? - No corredor, à direita. (Where's the bathroom? - In the hallway, on the right.)
