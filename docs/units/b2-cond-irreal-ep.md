## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Construct **Type 2 (irreal present/future)** conditional sentences in EP using `se + imperfeito subjuntivo + [condicional simples OR imperfeito do indicativo]`
- Apply the **register split** that defines EP Type 2: formal/written EP uses condicional simples in the main clause; spoken/informal EP commonly substitutes imperfeito do indicativo
- Choose between *viajaria* and *viajava* fluently based on register, recognizing both as productive native usage

## Vocabulary

- **se** — if (hypothetical reading)
- Imperfeito subjuntivo forms (review from `b1-subj-imperfeito`): *fosse, tivesse, estivesse, fizesse, pudesse, soubesse, viesse*
- Main-clause forms — TWO productive paradigms:
  - **Condicional simples**: *seria, teria, faria, viajaria, gostaria* (formal register)
  - **Imperfeito do indicativo**: *era, tinha, fazia, viajava, gostava* (colloquial register)

## Grammar

Type 2 covers situations the speaker treats as **unreal at the moment of speaking** — imagined, hypothetical, or contrary to current circumstance.

The *se*-clause is fixed: **imperfeito subjuntivo**. The main clause has **two productive forms** in EP, distributed by register.

### Pattern A — Formal register

`se + imperfeito subjuntivo + condicional simples`

- *Se eu **fosse** rico, **viajaria** pelo mundo.* — If I were rich, I'd travel the world.
- *Se ela **soubesse** a verdade, **contar-me-ia**.* — If she knew the truth, she'd tell me. (mesoclisis in formal register)
- *Se nós **tivéssemos** mais tempo, **terminaríamos** hoje.* — If we had more time, we'd finish today.

### Pattern B — Colloquial register

`se + imperfeito subjuntivo + imperfeito do indicativo`

- *Se eu **fosse** rico, **viajava** pelo mundo.* — Same meaning, conversational.
- *Se ela **soubesse** a verdade, **dizia-me**.* — Same, with normal enclitic placement.
- *Se nós **tivéssemos** mais tempo, **terminávamos** hoje.* — Same, conversational.

### Both patterns are correct EP

The substitution is **not** a learner error to correct. PEDAGOGY's Native Usage Filter applies: native EP speakers fluently use both forms and code-switch by register. Production at B2 should match the register; recognition needs to handle both.

| Register | Main-clause form | Where you'll see it |
|---|---|---|
| Formal / written | Condicional simples (*viajaria, dizia, faria*) | Newspapers, academic essays, formal correspondence, prepared speeches |
| Spoken / informal | Imperfeito do indicativo (*viajava, dizia, fazia*) | Conversation, WhatsApp, informal email, spontaneous interviews |

### Polite-request overlap

The Type 2 condicional often appears in polite-request frames where EP also commonly substitutes imperfeito (this is the *imperfeito de cortesia* pattern from `b1-condicional-ep`):

- Formal: *Eu **gostaria** de um café, por favor.*
- Colloquial: *Eu **gostava** de um café, por favor.* (more natural in EP cafés/restaurants)

The substitution set for polite requests is small (*queria, gostava, podia, devia*); for full Type 2 hypotheticals, the substitution applies broadly across verbs.

### Exemplar pairs

- *Se eu **pudesse**, **comprava** uma casa em Cascais.* (colloquial) — If I could, I'd buy a house in Cascais.
- *Se eu **pudesse**, **compraria** uma casa em Cascais.* (formal) — Same meaning.
- *Se a Marta **viesse**, **ficávamos** mais tempo.* (colloquial)
- *Se a Marta **viesse**, **ficaríamos** mais tempo.* (formal)
- *O que **fazias** se **ganhasses** na lotaria?* (colloquial)
- *O que **farias** se **ganhasses** na lotaria?* (formal)

## Traps

- **Don't treat the imperfeito substitute as an error.** *Se eu fosse rico, viajava pelo mundo* is correct, productive EP. A student who has internalized BP would call this wrong; the EP teacher must explicitly frame both forms as legitimate.
- **The substitution is broad in EP, narrow in BP.** EP applies imperfeito-as-condicional to most Type 2 main clauses in spoken usage. BP substitutes only for the polite-request set (*queria, podia, gostava* — and even those are a marked register choice, not the default).
- **The *se*-clause stays imperfeito subjuntivo.** Don't substitute imperfeito do indicativo there: *Se eu era rico, viajava* is wrong (the *se*-clause needs subjuntivo). The substitution is main-clause only.
- **Mesoclisis in formal main clauses.** Formal EP can produce *viajá-lo-ia*, *contar-lhe-ia* — recognize but don't drill at B2. Colloquial EP uses normal enclisis: *contava-lhe*.
- **The imperfeito-substitute's form is the mais-que-perfeito's form for past pluperfect.** *Tinha estudado* in Type 2: substitute for *teria estudado*? No — that's Type 3 (counterfactual past, see `b2-cond-contra-ep`). Type 2 substitution is *imperfeito do indicativo*, not mais-que-perfeito do indicativo. Don't conflate the two register-shift patterns.
