## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use **o qual / a qual / os quais / as quais** as a formal alternative to *que*, especially after prepositions and in disambiguation contexts
- Use **onde** as the locative relative pronoun for static location (and *aonde / de onde / por onde* with motion verbs)
- Recognize that o qual is register-marked (formal/written) while onde is unmarked
- Avoid the dialectal extension of onde to temporal contexts (*o dia onde* — nonstandard)

## Vocabulary

**O qual family** (formal alternative to *que*):

- **o qual** (m sg) — which
- **a qual** (f sg) — which
- **os quais** (m pl) — which (plural)
- **as quais** (f pl) — which (plural)

These agree with their antecedent (unlike cujo, which agrees with the possessed thing).

**Onde** — locative:

- **onde** — where (static location)
- **aonde** — to where (with motion verbs: ir, chegar)
- **de onde** — from where
- **por onde** — through where / by which way

## Grammar

### O qual / a qual — when to choose over *que*

Three contexts where *o qual* is preferred or required:

**1. After a preposition, for clarification.** When the relative clause has a complex preposition or when *que* could be ambiguous:

- *Trabalho num projeto **sobre o qual** escrevi um artigo.* (formal) — I work on a project I wrote an article about.
- Less formal alternative: *Trabalho num projeto **sobre que** escrevi um artigo.* (acceptable but heavier)
- *A casa **dentro da qual** vivemos está velha.* — The house we live inside of is old. (with *dentro de* + relative)

**2. To disambiguate when *que* could refer to multiple antecedents.**

- *A irmã do João, **a qual** mora em Faro, vai casar-se.* — João's sister, who lives in Faro, is getting married. (*a qual* = the sister, not João — disambiguation)
- *A irmã do João, **que mora em Faro**, vai casar-se.* — Same sentence with *que*: ambiguous, could be sister or João.

**3. In formal/written register.** Newspapers, academic prose, legal documents reach for *o qual* more freely than spoken Portuguese.

For most everyday cases, **simple *que* is the natural choice** — *o qual* can sound bookish if used where *que* would suffice.

### Onde — locative relative

Onde is the locative relative pronoun for **static location**:

- *A casa **onde moro** é antiga.* — The house where I live is old.
- *O lugar **onde nos conhecemos** está fechado agora.* — The place where we met is closed now.
- *Visitámos a aldeia **onde nasceu o meu avô**.* — We visited the village where my grandfather was born.

With **motion verbs**, the directional preposition combines with onde:

- *Vamos para a praia **aonde** o João foi ontem.* — We're going to the beach (to) where João went yesterday.
- *Esta é a cidade **de onde** sou.* — This is the city I'm from.
- *O caminho **por onde** passámos era estreito.* — The path we passed through was narrow.

Onde can usually be replaced by *em que* in static-location contexts:

- *A casa **onde moro** = A casa **em que moro***.

### Exemplar sentences

- *O acordo **ao qual** chegámos foi satisfatório.* — The agreement we reached was satisfactory. (formal)
- *A universidade **na qual** estudei é em Coimbra.* — The university I studied at is in Coimbra. (alt: *em que*)
- *A época **em que vivemos** é incerta.* — The time we live in is uncertain. (NOT *onde vivemos* — temporal, not locative)
- *O parque **onde brincava** quando era criança ainda existe.* — The park where I played as a child still exists.
- *Não conheço o autor **do qual** falaste.* — I don't know the author you spoke of. (formal alt: *de quem*, since the antecedent is human)

## Traps

- **Don't use onde for time.** *O dia onde nos conhecemos* is dialectal/nonstandard. Use *o dia em que nos conhecemos*. Onde is strictly locative in standard Portuguese.
- **Don't overuse o qual.** Where simple *que* works, prefer *que*. *O qual* sounds heavy in casual contexts. Reserve it for genuine clarification needs or formal writing.
- **O qual agrees with the antecedent (unlike cujo, which agrees with the possessed thing).** *A casa na qual moro* (a qual fem because casa is fem). *Os livros sobre os quais falámos* (os quais masc pl because livros is masc pl).
- **Aonde vs onde.** *Aonde* (= a + onde) is for motion-toward. *Onde* alone is for static location. *Vou aonde queres* (motion); *Estou onde combinámos* (static). Some speakers conflate them; the distinction is prescriptively required and the standard target.
- **Don't double-mark with relative + clitic.** *A casa onde moro nela* — wrong (the *nela* duplicates the locative reference). Just *A casa onde moro*.
- **Em + o qual = no qual / na qual; de + o qual = do qual / da qual.** Standard preposition-article contractions apply (the *o/a* in *o qual* behaves like a definite article for contraction purposes).
