## Outcomes

- Describe ongoing actions in the past (was doing)
- Talk about habitual actions in the past (used to do)
- Describe what things were like in the past
- Tell richer stories by combining preterite and imperfect
- Talk about childhood and past routines

## Vocabulary

- **Time expressions for habitual/repeated actions:**
  - sempre (always)
  - todos os dias (every day)
  - todas as semanas (every week)
  - frequentemente (frequently)
  - normalmente (normally)
  - geralmente (generally)
  - às vezes (sometimes)
  - nunca (never)
  - quando era criança (when I was a child)
  - naquela época (at that time/back then)
  - antes (before)
- **Additional vocabulary:**
  - criança (child)
  - jovem (young)
  - pequeno/a (small/little)

## Grammar

- **Imperfect tense (pretérito imperfeito): for ongoing, habitual, or descriptive past actions**
- **Key distinction from preterite (Units 26-29, 33, 35):**
  - **Preterite:** completed actions (I ate, I went, I did)
  - **Imperfect:** ongoing/habitual actions (I was eating, I used to go, I would do)
- **Regular conjugation patterns for imperfect:**
  - **-AR verbs:** -ava, -ava, -ávamos, -avam
  - **-ER verbs:** -ia, -ia, -íamos, -iam
  - **-IR verbs:** -ia, -ia, -íamos, -iam
- **Good news: -ER and -IR verbs share the SAME imperfect endings!**
- **Better news: Imperfect is VERY regular - almost no irregular verbs!**

### Exemplar sentences

- Morava em São Paulo (I used to live in São Paulo)
- Trabalhava no banco (I used to work at the bank / I was working at the bank)
- Comia muito (I used to eat a lot)
- Estudava português todos os dias (I used to study Portuguese every day)
- Quando era criança, brincava muito (When I was a child, I used to play a lot)
- Sempre gostava de viajar (I always used to like traveling)
- Naquela época, corríamos no parque (At that time, we used to run in the park)
