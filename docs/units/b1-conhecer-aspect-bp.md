## Outcomes

- Distinguish **conheci** (met for the first time / went to a place for the first time) from **conhecia** (was acquainted with, was familiar with)
- Use this pair fluently in life-story narration and travel talk
- Recognize that English "I met" almost always maps to *conheci*, while "I knew" maps to *conhecia*

## Vocabulary

Triggering markers for **conheci** (first encounter, first visit):
- ontem, no ano passado, em [year]
- numa festa, no trabalho, na faculdade (where you met)
- Verb often paired with the question: *Como vocês se conheceram?*

Triggering markers for **conhecia** (familiarity, prior acquaintance):
- há anos, desde criança, há muito tempo
- já (already, in the sense of "had known for a while")
- naquela época, antes de

## Grammar

- **conheci** = met (a person for the first time) / visited (a place for the first time) → punctual event of acquaintance
- **conhecia** = knew, was familiar with → ongoing acquaintance with no first-meeting event in focus

### Exemplar pairs

- *Eu **conheci** o Pedro numa festa, em 2020.* — I met Pedro at a party, in 2020.
- *Eu **conhecia** o Pedro há anos antes de ele se mudar.* — I'd known Pedro for years before he moved.

- *Quando você **conheceu** seu marido?* — When did you meet your husband?
- *Eu **conhecia** o seu marido só de vista.* — I knew your husband only by sight.

- *Eu **conheci** São Paulo na viagem do ano passado.* — I went to São Paulo for the first time on last year's trip.
- *Eu **conhecia** São Paulo bem na época em que morava lá.* — I knew São Paulo well back when I lived there.

- *Você já **conhece** o restaurante novo?* — Have you been to the new restaurant?
- *Eu não **conhecia** esse autor antes.* — I wasn't familiar with this author before.

## Traps

- **English "I met" maps to conheci, never to conhecia.** *Eu o conhecia em 2020* is wrong — it should be *Eu o conheci em 2020*.
- **English "I knew him/her" almost always maps to conhecia** (familiarity). *Eu o conheci há anos* would mean "I met him years ago" (the meeting event), not "I had known him for years."
- *Conhecer* applied to a place takes the first-visit reading in *conheci*. *Eu conheci Lisboa* = I visited Lisbon for the first time, not "I knew Lisbon."
- *Já conhece?* in BP is a polite "Have you been there before?" / "Are you already familiar with it?" — present tense carries the perfect-aspect reading.
