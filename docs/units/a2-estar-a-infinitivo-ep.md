## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the EP progressive aspect using **estar + a + infinitivo** (*Estou a falar*)
- Use the construction across all tenses (*estava a falar, estarei a falar, estive a falar*)
- Recognize that BP uses *estar + gerúndio* (*estou falando*) for the same meaning — the construction is genuinely different between variants

## Vocabulary

- **estar + a + infinitivo** — the EP progressive construction
- Common adverbs of action-in-progress: *agora, neste momento, ainda, atualmente*

## Grammar

The EP progressive uses the **infinitive** (not the gerund) connected to *estar* by the preposition *a*.

### Form: estar + a + infinitivo

| Pessoa | Estar | + a + infinitivo |
|---|---|---|
| eu | estou | a falar |
| tu | estás | a falar |
| você / ele / ela | está | a falar |
| nós | estamos | a falar |
| vocês / eles / elas | estão | a falar |

### Across tenses

The pattern works in any tense — *estar* changes form, *a + infinitivo* stays put:

- *Estou a ler.* — I'm reading. (presente)
- *Estava a ler quando ele chegou.* — I was reading when he arrived. (imperfeito)
- *Estive a trabalhar a manhã toda.* — I was working all morning. (pretérito)
- *Estarei a esperar.* — I'll be waiting. (futuro)
- *Estaria a estudar se tivesse tempo.* — I'd be studying if I had time. (condicional)

### Exemplar sentences

- *O que estás a fazer?* — What are you doing?
- *Eu estou a ler o jornal.* — I'm reading the newspaper.
- *Eles estão a jantar agora.* — They're having dinner now.
- *Ela estava a estudar quando eu cheguei.* — She was studying when I arrived.
- *Estamos a aprender português.* — We're learning Portuguese.
- *Não estou a perceber.* — I don't understand. (lit. "I'm not understanding")

### Use vs simple present

EP progressives are used **less frequently than English** — the simple present often suffices:

- English: "I work in Lisbon." — EP: *Trabalho em Lisboa.* (simple present)
- English: "I'm working on a new project." — EP: *Estou a trabalhar num projeto novo.* (progressive — for a defined period of activity)

The progressive specifically marks **ongoing action at the moment of speaking** or **temporary action over a delimited period**.

## Traps

- **The BP form (estar + gerúndio: estou falando) is wrong in EP.** EP speakers immediately register the BP gerund as foreign. *Estou falando* in EP sounds Brazilian. Use *Estou a falar*.
- **Don't drop the preposition.** *Estou falar* is wrong (no preposition). *Estou a falar* (with *a*) is the correct EP form.
- **Don't confuse with *ir + infinitivo* (immediate future).** *Vou falar* = I'm going to speak (future intent). *Estou a falar* = I'm speaking (current action). Different aspects entirely.
- **Some EP regions and registers do use the gerund occasionally** (especially Alentejo, the Azores), but the standard prescriptive and Lisbon norm is *estar + a + infinitivo*. Teach the standard form for production.
- **Particípio vs infinitivo.** *Estou a falar* (infinitive). *Tenho falado* (past participle, compound tense). Same auxiliary stem (*est-/ter*) but different second-element form for different meanings.
