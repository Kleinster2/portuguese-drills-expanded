## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Distinguish definite-antecedent relative clauses (indicative) from indefinite or non-existent antecedent clauses (subjuntivo)
- Use *procuro alguém que…*, *quero comprar algo que…*, *não há nada que…* with the correct mood
- Recognize that the subjuntivo here marks the antecedent's hypothetical or non-existent status, not a triggering verb

## Vocabulary

Common antecedents that push toward subjuntivo:

- **alguém que** — someone who (indefinite)
- **alguma coisa / algo que** — something that (indefinite)
- **nada que** — nothing that (non-existent)
- **ninguém que** — nobody who (non-existent)
- **um/uma + noun + que** — a/an [noun] that (when not specific)
- **qualquer + noun + que** — any [noun] that
- **o/a + noun + que** + superlative — the [noun] that... (sometimes)

## Grammar

Pattern: **[antecedente] + que + verbo no subjuntivo** when the antecedent is hypothetical, indefinite, or non-existent.

The same relative pronoun (*que*) takes indicative when the antecedent is real and specific. The mood signals existence / specificity:

- *Procuro alguém que **fale** francês.* — I'm looking for someone who speaks French. (indefinite — anyone qualifying)
- *Conheço alguém que **fala** francês.* — I know someone who speaks French. (definite — a specific person)

### Exemplar sentences

- *Procuro alguém que **possa** me ajudar.* — I'm looking for someone who can help me. (any qualifying person)
- *Quero comprar um carro que **seja** barato e econômico.* — I want to buy a car that's cheap and fuel-efficient. (any car meeting criteria)
- *Não há nada que **sirva** para isso.* — There's nothing that fits for this. (non-existent referent)
- *Não conheço ninguém que **saiba** consertar isso.* — I don't know anyone who knows how to fix this. (non-existent within speaker's circle)
- *Preciso de alguém que **fale** português fluentemente.* — I need someone who speaks Portuguese fluently.
- *Diga-me qualquer coisa que **funcione**.* — Tell me anything that works.

### Specific vs non-specific contrast

- *Comprei um carro que **é** barato.* — I bought a car that's cheap. (specific car, exists, indicative)
- *Quero comprar um carro que **seja** barato.* — I want to buy a car that's cheap. (no specific car yet, hypothetical, subjuntivo)

## Traps

- **Don't apply subjuntivo to definite-antecedent clauses.** *O homem que **mora** ao lado é simpático* — indicative, because the man is real and specific. *Subjuntivo* is wrong here.
- **Negation often forces subjuntivo.** *Não há ninguém que entenda* (subjuntivo, because no one is being asserted to exist). *Há alguém que entende* (could be either, depending on whether the speaker means a specific person).
- **Procurar + alguém que** — almost always subjuntivo, because *procurar* implies the person isn't yet identified. *Encontrei alguém que entende* — indicative, because the person is now identified.
