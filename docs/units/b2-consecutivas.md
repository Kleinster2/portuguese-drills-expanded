## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Construct result/consecutive clauses ("so X that Y") using **tão / tanto … que + indicative**
- Distinguish result clauses (consequence, indicative) from purpose clauses (intent, subjuntivo) — same surface conjunctions can read either way
- Recognize the formal-register alternatives (*de tal forma que, de tal modo que, de tal maneira que*)

## Vocabulary

The result-clause inventory:

| Structure | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **tão + adjective + que** | intensifier + adj + que + indic | *tão alto que toca o teto* |
| **tão + adverb + que** | intensifier + adv + que + indic | *tão rapidamente que ninguém viu* |
| **tanto + noun + que** | quantifier + noun + que + indic (with agreement: tanto/tanta/tantos/tantas) | *tanto trabalho que não dorme* |
| **tanto … que** (verb intensity) | verb + tanto + que + indic | *trabalha tanto que está exausto* |
| **de tal forma / modo / maneira que** | manner + que + indic | *falou de tal forma que todos perceberam* |
| **de modo que** (ambiguous) | indicative → result; subjuntivo → purpose | see Traps |

## Grammar

Result clauses express the **consequence** of the main-clause action — the magnitude or manner of the main clause produces the subordinate-clause outcome. Because the consequence is asserted as fact, the subordinate clause takes **indicative**, not subjuntivo.

### Pattern 1 — tão + adjective/adverb + que

The intensifier *tão* (so, such) marks the main-clause adjective or adverb; *que* introduces the consequence:

- *A música está **tão alta que** não consigo dormir.* — The music is so loud that I can't sleep.
- *Ele falou **tão depressa que** ninguém percebeu.* — He spoke so fast that nobody understood.
- *A cidade era **tão bonita que** decidi mudar-me.* — The city was so beautiful that I decided to move.

### Pattern 2 — tanto + noun + que

The quantifier *tanto* agrees with the noun in gender + number (*tanto/tanta/tantos/tantas*):

- *Comeu **tanta comida que** ficou doente.* — She ate so much food that she got sick.
- *Tem **tantos livros que** não cabem na estante.* — He has so many books that they don't fit on the shelf.
- *Há **tanto trabalho que** preciso de ajuda.* — There's so much work that I need help.

### Pattern 3 — verb + tanto + que (verb intensity)

When *tanto* modifies a verb directly (no noun follows), it works as an adverb of intensity:

- *Trabalha **tanto que** está exausto.* — He works so much that he's exhausted.
- *Estudaram **tanto que** passaram com facilidade.* — They studied so much that they passed easily.
- *Chove **tanto que** as ruas estão alagadas.* — It's raining so much that the streets are flooded.

### Pattern 4 — de tal forma / modo / maneira que (formal)

A more formal way to introduce the consequence:

- *Explicou **de tal forma que** todos ficaram convencidos.* — He explained in such a way that everyone was convinced.
- *O concerto correu **de tal modo que** o público pediu repetição.* — The concert went so well that the audience asked for an encore.
- *Comportou-se **de tal maneira que** foi expulso.* — He behaved in such a way that he was expelled.

### The de modo que ambiguity

The conjunction **de modo que** is genuinely ambiguous between RESULT (indicative) and PURPOSE (subjuntivo). The verb mood disambiguates:

| Mood | Reading | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Indicative | result | *Estudo de modo que **aprendo**.* — I study in a way that I learn. (consequence) |
| Subjuntivo | purpose | *Estudo de modo que **aprenda**.* — I study so that I learn. (intent) |

Same surface conjunction, two distinct grammatical paths. Same applies to *de forma que* and *de maneira que*.

### Exemplar set

- *A casa é **tão grande que** se perde a gente lá dentro.* — The house is so big that you get lost inside.
- *Choveu **tanto que** o jogo foi cancelado.* — It rained so much that the game was cancelled.
- *O café estava **tão quente que** queimei a língua.* — The coffee was so hot that I burned my tongue.
- *Comprou **tantos presentes que** não conseguiu carregá-los.* — She bought so many gifts that she couldn't carry them.
- *Ele explicou **de tal forma que** todos ficaram a perceber.* — He explained in such a way that everyone understood.
- *A criança chorou **tanto que** a mãe teve que acalmá-la.* — The child cried so much that the mother had to calm her down.

## Traps

- **Don't use subjuntivo in result clauses.** *Estudou tanto que passe* — wrong (subjuntivo). *Estudou tanto que passou* — right (indicativo). The consequence is asserted as fact, not hypothetical.
- **De modo que + indicative = result; + subjuntivo = purpose.** Same conjunction, two functions. Choose the mood by intent. *Falo de modo que percebes* (you understand — fact) vs *Falo de modo que percebas* (so that you may understand — intent).
- **Tanto agrees with the noun.** *Tanto trabalho* (m sg), *tanta comida* (f sg), *tantos livros* (m pl), *tantas pessoas* (f pl). Only the verb-intensity use (*trabalha tanto*) is invariable.
- **Tão is for adjectives and adverbs only; tanto is for nouns and verbs.** *Tão livros* is wrong; *tantos livros* is right. *Tanta bonita* is wrong; *tão bonita* is right.
- **Don't conflate with comparative tão … como / tanto … como.** Comparative: *tão alto **como** o pai* (as tall as the father). Result: *tão alto **que** toca no teto* (so tall that he touches the ceiling). The conjunction tells you which is which (*como* vs *que*).
- **Causal vs result: porque vs tão … que.** *Não dormi **porque** estava barulhento* (causal — because). *Estava **tão** barulhento **que** não dormi* (result — so much so that). Same situation, different rhetorical framing — causal points back at the cause; result points forward at the consequence.
