## Outcomes

- Replace direct objects with pronouns to sound more natural
- Avoid repetition: "Vejo João. João é meu amigo." → "Vejo João. O conheço bem."
- Use object pronouns with all three tenses
- Distinguish between reflexive pronouns (Unit 35) and direct object pronouns (Unit 36)
- Understand that me/nos can be reflexive OR object pronouns (context determines)
- Form more natural, fluent Portuguese sentences

## Vocabulary

- **Direct object pronouns:**
  - **me** (me - as object)
  - **te** (you - as object, informal)
  - **o / a** (him/it / her/it - masculine/feminine)
  - **nos** (us - as object)
  - **os / as** (them - masculine/feminine plural)
- **New verbs commonly used with direct objects:**
  - ver (to see - already learned in Unit 33)
  - conhecer (to know/meet - people/places)
  - amar (to love)
  - ajudar (to help)
  - esperar (to wait for / to hope)
  - ouvir (to hear/listen)
  - entender (to understand)
  - encontrar (to find / to meet)

## Grammar

- **Direct object pronouns: replace the direct object (the thing/person receiving the action)**
- **In Brazilian Portuguese, pronouns typically go BEFORE the verb (like reflexive pronouns)**
- Pattern: subject + object pronoun + verb
  - Ele **me** vê (He sees me)
  - Eu **te** amo (I love you)
  - Eu **o** conheço (I know him)
  - Ela **nos** ajuda (She helps us)
- Direct object answers "what?" or "whom?"
  - Vejo João → Vejo quem? João → **O** vejo (I see him)
  - Amo Maria → Amo quem? Maria → **A** amo (I love her)
  - Conhecemos as pessoas → **As** conhecemos (We know them)
- Gender and number agreement with o/a/os/as
  - **o** = him/it (masculine singular)
  - **a** = her/it (feminine singular)
  - **os** = them (masculine plural or mixed)
  - **as** = them (feminine plural)

### Exemplar sentences

- Ele me vê todos os dias (He sees me every day)
- Te amo (I love you)
- O conheço bem (I know him well)
- A vejo na escola (I see her at school)
- Ela nos ajuda (She helps us)
- Vocês os conhecem? (Do you all know them?)
- Não as vejo mais (I don't see them anymore - feminine)
- Me entendem? (Do you understand me?)
- Te espero aqui (I wait for you here / I'll wait for you here)
