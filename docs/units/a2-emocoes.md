## Outcomes

- Name 12 common emotions and feelings
- Use ESTAR correctly with emotion adjectives
- Use TER correctly with fome/sede/sono/medo
- Ask how someone is feeling
- Describe why they feel a certain way
- Distinguish temporary feelings (estar) from personality (ser)

## Vocabulary

- **Basic emotions:**
  - feliz (happy)
  - triste (sad)
  - cansado/cansada (tired)
  - animado/animada (excited/energetic)
  - nervoso/nervosa (nervous)
  - com raiva (angry - literally "with anger")
  - preocupado/preocupada (worried)
  - assustado/assustada (scared/frightened)
  - entediado/entediada (bored)
  - com fome (hungry - literally "with hunger")
  - com sede (thirsty - literally "with thirst")
  - com sono (sleepy - literally "with sleep")

## Grammar

- **Emotions with ESTAR (not SER):**
  - Emotions are TEMPORARY states → use ESTAR
  - ✅ "Eu **estou** feliz" (I am happy - right now)
  - ❌ "Eu **sou** feliz" (wrong for current feeling - would mean personality trait)

- **Emotions using TER (to have):**
  - Some emotions/physical feelings use TER + "com" construction:
    - "Eu **tenho** fome" (I'm hungry - literally "I have hunger")
    - "Eu **tenho** sede" (I'm thirsty - literally "I have thirst")
    - "Eu **tenho** sono" (I'm sleepy - literally "I have sleep")
    - "Eu **tenho** medo" (I'm afraid - literally "I have fear")
  - Alternative: Eu estou **com** fome/sede/sono (I am with hunger/thirst/sleep)

- **Asking about feelings:**
  - "Como você está?" (How are you? - asking about current state)
  - "Como você se sente?" (How do you feel?)
  - "Você está bem?" (Are you okay?)
  - "O que você tem?" (What's wrong? - literally "What do you have?")

### Exemplar sentences

- Eu estou feliz. (I'm happy.)
- Ela está triste. (She's sad.)
- Estou muito cansado. (I'm very tired.)
- Você está nervoso? (Are you nervous?)
- Eu tenho fome. (I'm hungry.)
- Estamos animados! (We're excited!)
- Ele está preocupado. (He's worried.)
