## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Transform direct quotations into reported (indirect) speech using verbs of saying (*dizer, comentar, afirmar, contar, responder*)
- Apply **tense backshift** when the reporting verb is in a past tense (presente → imperfeito, pretérito → mais-que-perfeito, futuro → condicional)
- Adjust pronouns and time/place adverbials to match the reporter's perspective
- Recognize when backshift is NOT needed (reporting verb in present, current relevance preserved)

## Vocabulary

Reporting verbs:

- **dizer** — to say (default reporting verb)
- **comentar** — to comment
- **afirmar** — to affirm / state
- **contar** — to tell (a story / fact)
- **responder** — to reply
- **explicar** — to explain
- **declarar** — to declare (formal)

Connector:

- **que** — that (mandatory between reporting verb and the reported clause)

## Grammar

Reported speech transforms a direct quotation into a subordinate `que`-clause attached to a verb of saying. Three things may shift:

1. **Tense** of the reported verb (backshift, when reporting verb is past)
2. **Pronouns** (1st-person typically becomes 3rd-person)
3. **Time and place adverbials** (deictic shift)

### Tense backshift table

When the reporting verb is in a **past tense** (most common), the reported verb shifts:

| Direct (in quotation) | Reported (after past reporting verb) |
|---|---|
| Presente: *"Vou ao cinema."* | Imperfeito: *Disse que ia ao cinema.* |
| Pretérito: *"Comi a pizza."* | Mais-que-perfeito: *Disse que tinha comido a pizza.* |
| Imperfeito: *"Estava cansado."* | Imperfeito (no shift): *Disse que estava cansado.* |
| Futuro: *"Vou viajar."* | Condicional: *Disse que viajaria.* |
| Condicional: *"Iria, mas não posso."* | Condicional (no shift): *Disse que iria, mas não podia.* |
| Subjuntivo presente: *"Talvez chova."* | Imperfeito subjuntivo: *Disse que talvez chovesse.* |

The future-backshift use of condicional is the reason this unit prereqs `b1-condicional`.

### When the reporting verb is in present — no backshift

If the reporting verb is in present (or present perfect with current relevance), tenses are typically preserved:

- Direct: *"Vou ao cinema."* → Reported: *Ele diz que vai ao cinema.* (no shift)
- Direct: *"Comi a pizza."* → Reported: *Ele diz que comeu a pizza.* (no shift)

This is common in news headlines, live commentary, or reports of recent statements.

### Pronoun adjustment

Pronouns shift to match the reporter's perspective:

- Direct: *"Eu vou ao cinema com a minha irmã."*
- Reported: *Ele disse que ele ia ao cinema com a irmã dele.* (or naturally: *Ele disse que ia ao cinema com a irmã.*)

The 1st-person reference in the original becomes a 3rd-person reference, and possessives shift accordingly.

### Time and place adverbials

Deictic adverbs shift to match the new temporal/spatial frame:

| Direct | Reported (past frame) |
|---|---|
| hoje | naquele dia |
| amanhã | no dia seguinte |
| ontem | no dia anterior / na véspera |
| agora | naquele momento |
| aqui | ali / lá |
| este | aquele |

These shifts apply when the reporting context is clearly displaced from the original utterance. In live or near-live reports, they may not apply.

### Exemplar transformations

| Direct | Reported (past) |
|---|---|
| *"Eu estudo português."* | Disse que estudava português. |
| *"Vamos ao restaurante hoje."* | Disse que iam ao restaurante naquele dia. |
| *"Já terminei o trabalho."* | Disse que já tinha terminado o trabalho. |
| *"Vou comprar um carro novo."* | Disse que ia comprar um carro novo. (or: que compraria) |
| *"Talvez chegue tarde."* | Disse que talvez chegasse tarde. |
| *"Comprei o presente ontem."* | Disse que tinha comprado o presente no dia anterior. |

## Traps

- **Forgetting backshift in spoken usage.** Casual BP often skips backshift: *Ele disse que vai ao cinema* (no shift, common in spoken BP) instead of *Ele disse que ia ao cinema*. Teach backshift as the target form for production; recognize the no-shift form as a real spoken pattern.
- **Over-applying backshift when reporting verb is in present.** *Ele diz que ia ao cinema* is wrong if the original was "Vou ao cinema" — present reporting verb keeps present in the reported clause. *Ele diz que vai* is right.
- **Pronoun confusion in nested reports.** "He said that I told him I'd go" — chains of reporters create ambiguity. Be explicit about who *eu* refers to in each layer.
- **Don't drop the connector *que*.** English allows *"He said he'd go"*; Portuguese requires *Ele disse **que** ia*. The *que* is mandatory.
- **Time-adverb shift is context-dependent.** *Ele disse que vinha hoje* can be correct if the reporting and the original utterance are on the same day. The shift to *naquele dia* applies when the reporting frame is clearly displaced.
- **Subjuntivo-leaning EP usage.** EP slightly more often retains subjuntivo in reported clauses where BP allows indicative: *Disse-me que viesse* (EP, subj — a request being reported) vs *Disse-me que vinha* (BP, indicative — a statement being reported). Both forms are correct; the choice tracks whether the original utterance was a directive (subj) or assertion (indic).
