## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Recognize the verbs of desire/wish/volition that trigger subjuntivo presente in a subordinate `que`-clause
- Distinguish *eu quero ir* (same subject → infinitive) from *eu quero que você vá* (different subjects → subjuntivo)
- Form polite requests and indirect commands using *pedir que*, *exigir que*

## Vocabulary

Trigger verbs (ranked roughly by frequency):

- **querer que** — to want (someone) to
- **esperar que** — to hope that / expect that
- **desejar que** — to wish that (more formal than *querer*)
- **preferir que** — to prefer that
- **pedir que** — to ask that (indirect request)
- **exigir que** — to demand that
- **insistir (em) que** — to insist that
- **proibir que** — to forbid

## Grammar

Pattern: **[verb of desire] + que + sujeito + verbo no subjuntivo**.

The subjunctive is triggered because the speaker is projecting a desired state, not asserting a fact. The clause introduces what the speaker *wants*, not what *is*.

### Same-subject vs different-subject

When the subject of the desire and the desired action is the same, use the infinitive — no `que`, no subjunctive:

- *Eu quero **ir** ao cinema.* — I want to go to the movies.
- *Eu quero que você **vá** ao cinema.* — I want you to go to the movies.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Quero que você **venha** à festa.* — I want you to come to the party.
- *Espero que tudo **dê** certo.* — I hope everything works out.
- *Prefiro que ele **fique** em casa hoje.* — I'd rather he stay home today.
- *Peço que você me **ajude** com isso.* — I'm asking you to help me with this.
- *O chefe exigiu que entregássemos o relatório hoje.* — The boss demanded we hand in the report today. (imperfeito subj because triggering verb is past)
- *Insisto em que você **prove** o brigadeiro.* — I insist you try the brigadeiro.

## Traps

- **Same-subject check.** *Eu quero que eu vá* is a beginner overgeneralization — same subject takes the infinitive: *eu quero ir*.
- **BP indicative drift.** In casual BP speech, indicative sometimes appears where the prescriptive rule wants subjuntivo: *quero que você vem* is heard but considered substandard. Teach subjuntivo as the target form; recognize the indicative form when the student hears it in the wild.
- *Pedir que* takes subjuntivo, not infinitive: *peço que você venha*, not *peço você vir*.
