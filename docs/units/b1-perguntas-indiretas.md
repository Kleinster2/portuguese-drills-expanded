## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Convert direct yes/no questions into indirect questions using **se** (whether)
- Convert direct content questions into indirect questions retaining the **wh-word** (*onde, quando, como, por que, quem, o que*)
- Apply non-inversion in indirect questions — word order reverts to declarative even though the meaning is interrogative
- Inherit tense backshift rules from `b1-discurso-direto-indireto` when the reporting verb is past

## Vocabulary

Reporting verbs for questions:

- **perguntar** — to ask (default reporting verb for questions)
- **questionar** — to question (more formal)
- **querer saber** — to want to know (= "asked whether")

Question-clause connectors:

- **se** — whether / if (yes/no questions)
- **onde** — where
- **quando** — when
- **como** — how
- **por que / porquê** — why
- **quem** — who
- **o que / o quê** — what
- **qual / quais** — which

## Grammar

Indirect questions are subordinate clauses introduced by *se* (for yes/no questions) or a wh-word (for content questions). The most distinctive feature is **non-inversion**: the subordinate clause uses declarative word order, even though the meaning is a question.

### Yes/no questions — *se*

Direct yes/no question → *se*-clause:

| Direct | Indirect |
|---|---|
| *"Você quer ir?"* | *Ele perguntou **se** eu queria ir.* |
| *"Está pronto?"* | *Ela quis saber **se** estava pronto.* |
| *"Vens amanhã?"* | *Perguntei **se** vinhas no dia seguinte.* |

The *se* in this construction is **the conjunction "whether"**, not the conditional "if." Functionally similar but syntactically distinct from the *se* in conditional sentences.

### Content questions — wh-word retained

Direct content question → wh-word + declarative-order clause:

| Direct | Indirect |
|---|---|
| *"Onde ele mora?"* | *Ele perguntou **onde** eu morava.* |
| *"Quando vais voltar?"* | *Ela quis saber **quando** voltaria.* |
| *"Como você descobriu?"* | *Perguntou **como** eu tinha descoberto.* |
| *"Por que ela saiu?"* | *Não disse **por que** tinha saído.* |
| *"Quem te chamou?"* | *Perguntou **quem** me tinha chamado.* |
| *"O que aconteceu?"* | *Quis saber **o que** tinha acontecido.* |

### Non-inversion is the key rule

Direct questions in Portuguese can use inversion or intonation:

- *"Onde mora ele?"* (inverted, somewhat formal)
- *"Onde é que ele mora?"* (with *é que*, common)
- *"Onde ele mora?"* (no inversion, intonation only — most common in spoken use)

Indirect questions **always use declarative word order** — subject before verb:

- *Perguntou onde **ele mora**.* ← right (subj-verb order)
- *Perguntou onde **mora ele**.* ← wrong (inversion in indirect question)

### Tense backshift inherits from declarative reported speech

The same backshift rules from `b1-discurso-direto-indireto` apply:

| Direct (present-time question) | Indirect (past reporting) |
|---|---|
| *"Como você está?"* | *Perguntou como eu estava.* |
| *"Onde estavas?"* | *Perguntou onde eu tinha estado.* |
| *"Vais sair amanhã?"* | *Perguntou se eu ia sair no dia seguinte.* |

### Exemplar set

- *Ele perguntou se eu queria um café.* — He asked if I wanted a coffee.
- *Ela quis saber onde eu morava.* — She wanted to know where I lived.
- *Não sei se ele vem.* — I don't know whether he's coming.
- *Pergunto-me como ela conseguiu.* — I wonder how she managed.
- *Perguntei o que ele queria.* — I asked what he wanted.
- *Diz-me quando posso ir.* — Tell me when I can go.

## Traps

- **Don't keep direct-question word order in indirect questions.** *Perguntou onde mora ele* — wrong (inverted). *Perguntou onde ele mora* — right (declarative order).
- **Don't drop *se* in yes/no questions.** *Ele perguntou eu queria ir* — wrong (no connector). *Ele perguntou se eu queria ir* — right.
- **Don't confuse the conjunction *se* with the conditional *se*.** Same word, two functions: *Pergunto-me **se** ele vem* (whether — indirect question) vs *Se ele vem, eu fico* (if — conditional). Disambiguation comes from the matrix verb (*perguntar / saber* → conjunction; without matrix verb of inquiry → conditional).
- **Por que vs por quê vs porque vs porquê.** All four exist in Portuguese, with subtle distinctions:
  - *por que* — interrogative (used in direct and indirect questions): *Por que saíste? / Perguntou por que saíste.*
  - *por quê* — sentence-final or before punctuation: *Saíste por quê?*
  - *porque* — conjunction "because": *Saí porque estava cansado.*
  - *porquê* — noun "the reason": *O porquê ainda é desconhecido.*
  EP often writes *porquê* where BP would write *por quê* — register variation.
- **Wh-word retention can mask backshift.** *Perguntou quando ele vem* (no shift) vs *Perguntou quando ele vinha* (shift). The wh-word stays the same; only the embedded verb tense shifts. Don't lose the backshift just because the question word is preserved.
- **Don't double-mark with both wh-word and *se*.** *Perguntou se onde ele mora* — wrong. Pick one connector based on question type.
