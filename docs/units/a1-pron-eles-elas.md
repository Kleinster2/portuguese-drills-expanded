## Outcomes

- Talk about groups of people not in the conversation
- Distinguish between eles (masculine/mixed) and elas (all feminine) based on gender
- Recognize that vocês/eles/elas all share the same verb forms
- Ask and answer questions about groups of people
- **Complete understanding of ALL Portuguese subject pronouns**

## Vocabulary

- eles (they - masculine or mixed group)
- elas (they - feminine group only)
- os amigos (the friends - masculine)
- as amigas (the friends - feminine)
- a família (the family)

## Grammar

- **eles** = they (masculine or mixed gender group)
- **elas** = they (all feminine group)
- **KEY INSIGHT: eles/elas use the SAME verb forms as vocês!**
- Gender distinction: eles (masculine/mixed) vs elas (all feminine)
- Talking about groups of people not present in the conversation
- Verb forms (identical to vocês):
  - -AR verbs: eles/elas moram, trabalham, andam, falam, gostam (ending in **-am**)
  - -ER verbs: eles/elas comem, bebem, correm (ending in **-em**)
  - Irregular: eles/elas **são**, **estão**, **têm**

### Exemplar sentences

- Eles são brasileiros. (They [masc/mixed] are Brazilian.)
- Elas moram em São Paulo. (They [fem] live in São Paulo.)
- Eles trabalham na universidade. (They work at the university.)
- Elas falam português e inglês. (They speak Portuguese and English.)
- Eles estão cansados. (They are tired.)
- Elas têm um cachorro. (They have a dog.)
- Eles gostam de café. (They like coffee.)
