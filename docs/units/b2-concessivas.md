## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Construct concessive clauses ("although / even though") using the appropriate conjunction + subjuntivo
- Choose between **presente subjuntivo** (present/future hypothetical) and **imperfeito subjuntivo** (past hypothetical) based on time reference
- Use **apesar de + infinitivo** as the subjuntivo-free alternative
- Distinguish concessive subordination from adversative coordination (*embora* vs *mas*)

## Vocabulary

The concessive-conjunction inventory:

| Conjunction | Mood it triggers | Register |
|---|---|---|
| **embora** | subjuntivo | most common, neutral |
| **ainda que** | subjuntivo | common, slightly emphatic |
| **mesmo que** | subjuntivo | strongly hypothetical |
| **conquanto** | subjuntivo | formal / literary |
| **nem que** | subjuntivo | "not even if" |
| **por mais / muito que** | subjuntivo | "no matter how (much)" |
| **apesar de que** | subjuntivo | considered redundant by some prescriptivists |
| **apesar de** + infinitivo | infinitivo (no subj) | the subjuntivo-free alternative |

## Grammar

Concessive clauses contrast with the main clause: the speaker presents a fact or hypothesis that *would* normally block the main-clause action, but the action happens anyway. The subordinate clause carries subjuntivo because the speaker is not asserting the contrast as fact — they're acknowledging it hypothetically.

### Tense choice — present vs imperfect subjuntivo

Time reference of the concessive event determines the tense:

| Time | Subjuntivo tense | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Present / future hypothetical | presente | *Embora **chova** amanhã, vou sair.* — Even if it rains tomorrow, I'll go out. |
| Past hypothetical | imperfeito | *Embora **chovesse**, saímos.* — Even though it was raining, we went out. |
| Counterfactual past | mais-que-perfeito | *Embora **tivesse chovido**, saímos.* — Even though it had been raining, we went out. |

### Three primary patterns

**1. Conjunction + subjuntivo** (the canonical concessive pattern):

- *Embora **esteja** cansado, vou estudar.* — Although I'm tired, I'll study.
- *Ainda que **chova**, fazemos a festa no jardim.* — Even if it rains, we'll have the party in the garden.
- *Mesmo que ele **venha**, não vou ficar.* — Even if he comes, I won't stay.
- *Por mais que tu **tentes**, não vais conseguir.* — No matter how much you try, you won't manage.

**2. Apesar de + infinitivo** (the no-subjuntivo alternative):

This construction uses an infinitive (which can be personal — see [[b2-infinitivo-pessoal-uso]]) and avoids subjuntivo entirely:

- *Apesar de **estar** cansado, vou estudar.* — Despite being tired, I'll study. (same subject — bare infinitive)
- *Apesar de **eles virem**, não vou ficar.* — Despite their coming, I won't stay. (distinct subject — personal infinitive)

**3. Adversative coordination — NOT concessive subordination**

Coordinating conjunctions (*mas, porém, contudo, todavia, entretanto*) express contrast WITHOUT subordination — and take indicative:

- *Estou cansado, **mas** vou estudar.* — I'm tired, but I'll study. (mas + indicativo)
- *Estou cansado; **porém**, vou estudar.* — Same meaning, more formal.

The coordinative pattern is structurally simpler — two independent clauses joined by *mas*. The concessive pattern is more rhetorically marked — the subordinate clause is presented as a backgrounded fact that makes the main-clause action surprising.

### Exemplar set

- *Embora ele **seja** novo, é muito experiente.* — Although he's young, he's very experienced.
- *Ainda que **trabalhasses** mais, não terias passado.* — Even if you had worked more, you wouldn't have passed. (Type-3-like)
- *Mesmo que **chovesse** ontem, fizemos o piquenique.* — Even though it rained yesterday, we had the picnic.
- *Apesar de **chover**, fomos à praia.* — Despite the rain, we went to the beach.
- *Por mais que **estudasse**, ela não percebia a matéria.* — No matter how much she studied, she didn't get the material.
- *Conquanto a proposta **seja** interessante, não a aceitamos.* — Although the proposal is interesting, we're not accepting it. (formal register)

## Traps

- **Don't use indicativo with the concessive conjunctions.** *Embora chove* — wrong; *Embora chova* — right. The subjuntivo is mandatory.
- **Apesar de takes infinitive, NOT subjuntivo.** *Apesar de chova* is wrong. Use *apesar de chover* (bare infinitive, same subject) or *apesar de eles virem* (personal infinitive, distinct subject).
- **Embora ≠ porque.** Embora is concessive (subjuntivo); porque is causal (indicativo). *Embora chova* (even though it rains) vs *Porque chove* (because it rains).
- **Apesar de que** is heard but considered redundant by some prescriptivists — *apesar de* already does the conjunction work, so adding *que* + subjuntivo doubles the marking. Prefer the cleaner forms (*apesar de* + infinitivo OR *embora* + subjuntivo). Both alternatives are widespread in real usage; *apesar de que* won't be marked wrong but isn't elegant.
- **Mesmo se vs mesmo que.** *Mesmo se* (= "even if") takes indicativo or futuro do subjuntivo per Type-1/Type-2 conditional rules. *Mesmo que* takes subjuntivo (concessive). Different conjunctions, different syntactic behavior.
- **Tense alignment with main clause matters.** A past-time main clause (*saímos*) calls for past subjuntivo (*chovesse*) — not present subjuntivo. *Embora chova, saímos* is grammatical but mixes time references; *Embora chovesse, saímos* aligns the past hypothetical with the past main-clause action.
