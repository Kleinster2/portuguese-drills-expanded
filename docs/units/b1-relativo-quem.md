## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use **quem** as the [+human] relative pronoun after a preposition (*de quem, com quem, para quem, sobre quem, a quem, em quem*)
- Recognize that quem WITHOUT a preposition is rare in modern Portuguese (use *que* instead, even for human antecedents)
- Avoid English-influenced "that I work with" constructions — Portuguese fronts the preposition

## Vocabulary

Common preposition + quem combinations:

- **a quem** — to whom
- **com quem** — with whom
- **de quem** — of whom / about whom
- **em quem** — in whom (trust, faith)
- **para quem** — for whom
- **por quem** — for whom (cause / sake)
- **sobre quem** — about whom

## Grammar

**Quem** is the [+human] relative pronoun, almost always used after a preposition. It's invariable in form but encodes the [+human] feature.

### When to use quem

| Context | Pronoun | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Subject of relative clause, [+human] | **que** | *A pessoa **que** veio…* |
| Direct object of relative clause, [+human] | **que** | *A pessoa **que** vi…* |
| After preposition, [+human] | **quem** | *A pessoa **com quem** falei…* |
| After preposition, [-human] | **que** | *A casa **em que** moro…* |

The preposition + quem combination is the canonical [+human] relative construction. Without a preposition, quem alone is essentially restricted to proverbs and fixed expressions (*Quem cala consente* — "Silence implies consent"; *Quem ri por último ri melhor* — "He who laughs last laughs best").

### Exemplar sentences

- *A pessoa **de quem** te falei mora em Coimbra.* — The person I told you about lives in Coimbra.
- *A mulher **com quem** trabalho é portuguesa.* — The woman I work with is Portuguese.
- *O amigo **para quem** comprei o presente já o recebeu.* — The friend I bought the gift for has already received it.
- *A criança **a quem** dei o livro adorou-o.* — The child I gave the book to loved it.
- *O candidato **em quem** voto sempre é honesto.* — The candidate I always vote for is honest.
- *A artista **sobre quem** escrevi a tese expôs em Lisboa.* — The artist I wrote my thesis about exhibited in Lisbon.

### Quem vs que after a preposition

For [+human] antecedents, both *com que* and *com quem* are sometimes possible — but **quem is the strong default**. Que after a preposition with a human antecedent sounds awkward or substandard:

- *A mulher com quem trabalho.* ← natural
- *A mulher com que trabalho.* ← marked / heavy

For [-human] antecedents, *que* dominates and *quem* is wrong:

- *A casa em que moro.* ← right
- *A casa em quem moro.* ← wrong (quem requires [+human])

## Traps

- **Don't use quem without a preposition for animate referents.** *A pessoa quem mora aqui* is wrong; use *A pessoa que mora aqui* (subject position takes que regardless of [+human]). Preposition is the trigger for quem.
- **Quem is invariable.** No gender or number agreement. *As pessoas com quem trabalho* (plural antecedent, quem stays singular form).
- **Preposition-stranding is wrong in Portuguese.** *A pessoa que falei com* — wrong. *A pessoa com quem falei* — right.
- **Standalone quem in modern usage is mostly fixed-expression territory.** *Quem viu, viu* (whoever saw, saw — proverb-like). Don't reach for standalone quem in normal sentence construction.
- **Don't confuse with interrogative quem.** Same word, different role. *Quem veio?* (interrogative — Who came?) vs *A pessoa de quem te falei* (relative — about whom). Both forms are pronounced and spelled identically; context disambiguates.
