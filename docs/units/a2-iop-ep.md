## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use indirect object pronouns (IOP) in EP with correct clitic placement
- Combine DOP + IOP into single contracted forms (*me + o → mo*, *lhe + o → lho*)
- Apply EP enclisis-as-default + proclisis-trigger rules to IOP

## Vocabulary

Indirect object pronouns:

| Pessoa | IOP |
|---|---|
| eu | me |
| tu | te |
| ele / ela / você | lhe |
| nós | nos |
| eles / elas / vocês | lhes |

(*Vos* dead in modern EP; use *lhes* with *vocês* address.)

## Grammar

IOP placement follows the same rules as DOP and reflexives: **enclisis is the default**, **proclisis is triggered** by negação, conjunções subordinativas, advérbios de inclusão, pronomes interrogativos/relativos/indefinidos, and **mesoclisis** appears in futuro / condicional.

### Default enclisis

- *Dou-lhe o livro.* — I give him the book.
- *Telefonas-me amanhã?* — Will you call me tomorrow?
- *Mostrámos-lhes o caminho.* — We showed them the way.

### Proclisis (triggered)

- *Não lhe disse nada.* — I didn't tell him anything.
- *Disse que lhe ligava.* — He said he'd call him.
- *Quem te telefonou?* — Who called you?
- *Também lhes contei a história.* — I told them the story too.

### Mesoclisis (formal)

- *Dar-lhe-ei a resposta amanhã.* — I'll give him the answer tomorrow.
- *Telefonar-te-ia se tivesse o número.* — I'd call you if I had the number.

### Combined pronouns: IOP + DOP

When indirect and direct object pronouns combine, they merge into single forms:

| IOP + DOP | Combined |
|---|---|
| me + o/a | mo / ma |
| me + os/as | mos / mas |
| te + o/a | to / ta |
| te + os/as | tos / tas |
| lhe + o/a | lho / lha |
| lhe + os/as | lhos / lhas |
| nos + o/a | no-lo / no-la |
| lhes + o/a | lho / lha |

Examples:

- *Deu-mo ontem.* — He gave it to me yesterday. (= deu + me + o)
- *Mostrei-lho.* — I showed it to him. (= mostrei + lhe + o)
- *Vou comprá-lo. Vou comprá-lo para te o oferecer.* — I'm going to buy it. I'll buy it to give it to you. (combined forms in proclisis position: *para + te + o*)

### Exemplar sentences

- *Telefonei-lhe ontem.* — I called him yesterday.
- *Não lhe perguntei.* — I didn't ask him.
- *A Ana mostrou-me as fotografias.* — Ana showed me the photos.
- *Mostrou-mas todas.* — She showed them all to me. (= mostrou + me + as)
- *Dou-te o número se quiseres.* — I'll give you the number if you want.
- *Quem te disse isso?* — Who told you that?

## Traps

- **Combined pronouns are EP-prescriptive but rare in spontaneous speech.** *Mostrou-mas* is grammatically correct but most speakers split: *Mostrou-me as fotografias*. Teach combined forms for recognition; don't force production until B1+.
- **Lhe is invariable in modern EP for gender** (unlike Spanish *le/la*). *Dei-lhe* works for "I gave him" or "I gave her." Disambiguation comes from context.
- **BP often uses *para ele/para ela* instead of *lhe*** in spoken usage. EP keeps *lhe* productive: *Dei-lhe o livro* (EP standard) vs *Dei o livro para ele* (BP colloquial). EP students should use *lhe*.
- **Telefonar takes an indirect object in EP.** *Telefonei ao João → telefonei-lhe* (not *telefonei-o*). The verb governs the case: *telefonar a alguém*.
- **Don't confuse with the reflexive *se*.** *Lhe* is 3rd person indirect object; *se* is 3rd person reflexive. *Lavou-se* (he washed himself) vs *Lavou-lhe as mãos* (he washed his/her hands).
