## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the mais-que-perfeito do indicativo (tinha/tinhas/tinha/tínhamos/tinham + particípio) — the **pluperfect**
- Use it to express a past action that occurred BEFORE another past action ("had done" in English)
- Sequence two past events correctly: simpler past + earlier past = pretérito perfeito + mais-que-perfeito

## Vocabulary

- **tinha / tinhas / tinha / tínhamos / tinham + particípio** — auxiliary + past participle
- Common signal words: *já* (already), *antes* (before), *quando* (when, sequencing), *depois que* (after)

## Grammar

Form: imperfeito do indicativo of *ter* + particípio passado.

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | tinha | falado |
| você / ele / ela | tinha | falado |
| nós | tínhamos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | tinham | falado |

The form anchors a past event **prior to** another past event. The reference point sits in the past, and the pluperfect points further back.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Quando cheguei, ele já **tinha saído**.* — When I arrived, he had already left.
- *Eu já **tinha visto** esse filme antes.* — I had already seen that movie before.
- *Eles **tinham terminado** o trabalho quando o chefe ligou.* — They had finished the work when the boss called.
- *Nunca **tínhamos comido** comida tão boa.* — We had never eaten such good food.
- *Vocês já **tinham conhecido** o Pedro antes da festa?* — Had you met Pedro before the party?
- *Ela me disse que **tinha estudado** muito.* — She told me she had studied a lot.

### Two-event sequencing

The pluperfect needs another past event as anchor. Without one, use pretérito perfeito simples instead:

- *Eu **vi** o filme.* — I saw the movie. (no anchor needed)
- *Eu já **tinha visto** o filme quando você me convidou.* — I had already seen the movie when you invited me. (anchored)

## Traps

- **Don't substitute pretérito perfeito for pluperfect when sequencing matters.** *Quando cheguei, ele saiu* loses the "before" relationship — implies he left at the moment you arrived. *Quando cheguei, ele já tinha saído* preserves the "had already left" reading.
- **Mais-que-perfeito simples (saíra, falara, vira) is literary.** In spoken BP and EP, the compound form (*tinha saído, tinha falado, tinha visto*) dominates. The simples form appears in formal writing, journalism, literary prose. Recognize it; don't produce it in conversation.
- **EP register substitution in conditionals.** EP often uses *tinha falado* as a substitute for *teria falado* (condicional composto) in counterfactual main clauses (*Se eu tivesse estudado, tinha passado*). BP keeps *teria passado*. This unit teaches the indicative pluperfect; the EP substitution pattern is covered in `b2-condicional-composto-bp` and its forthcoming EP twin.
- **Don't conflate with pretérito perfeito composto** (*tinha falado* vs *tenho falado*). The pluperfect anchors in the past; the *tenho* form anchors in the recent-up-to-now. Different temporal reference points.
