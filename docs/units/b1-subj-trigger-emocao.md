## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Recognize emotion verbs and adjectival expressions of emotion that trigger subjuntivo presente
- Express positive and negative reactions to a situation or another person's actions
- Distinguish *fico feliz porque você veio* (factual cause → indicative) from *fico feliz que você venha* (emotional reaction → subjuntivo)

## Vocabulary

Trigger expressions:

- **gostar que** — to be glad / like that
- **lamentar que** — to regret / be sorry that
- **sentir que** — to be sorry that / regret
- **ter medo (de) que** — to be afraid that
- **temer que** — to fear that (more formal)
- **ficar feliz/triste/contente que** — to be glad/sad/happy that
- **alegrar-se que** — to rejoice that
- **estranhar que** — to find it strange that
- **surpreender-se que** — to be surprised that

## Grammar

Pattern: **[expression of emotion] + que + sujeito + verbo no subjuntivo**.

The subjunctive is triggered because the speaker is reacting emotionally to a state of affairs, not just asserting it as fact. The `que`-clause carries the trigger, not standalone reactions.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Gosto que você **venha** sempre aos domingos.* — I like that you always come on Sundays.
- *Lamento que ela não **possa** estar conosco.* — I'm sorry she can't be with us.
- *Tenho medo que ele **se machuque** na bicicleta.* — I'm afraid he'll hurt himself on the bike.
- *Fico feliz que vocês **estejam** aqui.* — I'm happy you're here.
- *É uma pena que o restaurante **feche** cedo.* — It's a pity the restaurant closes early.
- *Estranho que ele ainda não **tenha** chegado.* — I find it strange he hasn't arrived yet.

### Causal vs emotional contrast

When the emotion is followed by a *cause* (with *porque*), the verb is indicative — that's an assertion, not a reaction:

- *Fico feliz **que você venha**.* — I'm happy you're coming. (reaction → subjuntivo)
- *Fico feliz **porque você veio**.* — I'm happy because you came. (assertion of cause → indicative)

## Traps

- **BP indicative drift is strongest with emotion verbs.** Native BP speech often uses indicative — *fico feliz que você está aqui* is widely heard. Teach subjuntivo as the target; recognize indicative as common in spontaneous speech.
- *Ter medo que* and *ter medo de que* both occur. The *de* is grammatically preferred but optional in practice. Don't penalize either.
- *Sentir que* in the emotion sense (= "be sorry") triggers subjuntivo. *Sentir que* in the perception sense (= "feel that, sense that") triggers indicative: *sinto que você está cansado* — same form, different reading.
