## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form reflexive verbs in EP (*chamar-se, levantar-se, sentir-se, lavar-se*) with the clitic in correct EP position
- Apply EP clitic placement: **enclisis is the default** (clitic AFTER verb with hyphen), with **proclisis** triggered by specific words/contexts
- Distinguish EP placement from BP placement (EP enclisis as default vs BP proclisis as default in spoken language)

## Vocabulary

Reflexive pronouns:

| Pessoa | Pronome reflexo |
|---|---|
| eu | me |
| tu | te |
| você / ele / ela | se |
| nós | nos |
| vocês / eles / elas | se |

Common reflexive verbs: *chamar-se* (to be called), *levantar-se* (to get up), *sentar-se* (to sit down), *deitar-se* (to lie down), *vestir-se* (to get dressed), *lavar-se* (to wash oneself), *sentir-se* (to feel), *lembrar-se* (to remember).

## Grammar

EP clitic placement is **strict and prescriptive**, more so than BP. The default is **enclisis** (clitic after the verb, joined by hyphen). **Proclisis** (clitic before the verb) is triggered by specific words and contexts.

### Default: enclisis

In the absence of any trigger, the clitic follows the verb:

- *Chamo-me João.* — My name is João.
- *Levantamo-nos cedo.* — We get up early.
- *Ele veste-se rapidamente.* — He gets dressed quickly.
- *Sentas-te aqui?* — Do you sit here?

(Notice the *nos* contraction: *Levantamos + nos* → *Levantamo-nos* — the final *-s* of the verb drops before *-nos*. Same with *-vos*.)

### Proclisis triggers (clitic moves BEFORE the verb)

| Trigger type | Examples | Clitic placement |
|---|---|---|
| Negação | *não, nunca, jamais, nada, ninguém* | *Não me chamo João.* |
| Conjunções subordinativas | *que, quando, se, embora, porque* | *Disse que se chamava João.* |
| Advérbios de inclusão/exclusão | *também, só, apenas, talvez, ainda* | *Também me sento aqui.* |
| Pronomes interrogativos | *quem, o que, onde, como, porquê* | *Quem te disse isso?* |
| Pronomes relativos | *que, quem, cujo* | *A pessoa que se sentou ali...* |
| Pronomes indefinidos | *alguém, algo, todos* | *Alguém te procurou.* |

### Mesoclisis (formal — futuro / condicional)

In **futuro do indicativo** and **condicional simples**, the clitic can split the verb (very formal/written EP):

- *Chamar-me-ei João.* — I will call myself João. (futuro: chamar + me + ei)
- *Sentar-nos-íamos no sofá.* — We would sit on the sofa. (condicional)

In modern spoken EP, mesoclisis is rare and replaced by *vou + infinitivo* + clitic-via-infinitivo: *Vou chamar-me João*. Recognize mesoclisis when reading; produce it only in formal writing.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Como te chamas?* — What's your name? (proclisis: triggered by question word *como*)
- *Chamo-me Marvin.* — My name is Marvin. (enclisis: default)
- *Levantamo-nos sempre às sete.* — We always get up at seven. (enclisis: default; *sempre* doesn't trigger proclisis)
- *Não me lembro do nome dele.* — I don't remember his name. (proclisis: triggered by *não*)
- *Eles deitam-se cedo.* — They go to bed early. (enclisis: default)
- *Quando te sentires bem, avisa-me.* — When you feel well, let me know. (proclisis: triggered by *quando*; second clitic enclitic on imperative)

## Traps

- **BP places clitics PROCLITICALLY by default (Eu me chamo, Eu me levanto). EP places them ENCLITICALLY by default (Chamo-me, Levanto-me).** This is the single biggest BP/EP placement difference for learners. A BP-trained student switching to EP must invert their default.
- **Trigger words flip the placement.** Memorize the trigger list (negação, conjunções subordinativas, etc.). When a trigger is present, the clitic MUST move before the verb. *Não me chamo João.* — never *Não chamo-me João*.
- **Hyphen is mandatory in enclisis.** *Chamo-me*, not *Chamo me*. The hyphen marks the clitic attachment.
- **The *-mos + nos/vos* contraction.** *Levantamo-nos* (not *levantamos-nos*). *Sentamo-vos* would be archaic — in modern EP use *vocês* and avoid *vos* entirely.
- **Tu has its own clitic *te*.** *Sentas-te* (with tu) vs *Senta-se* (with você/ele/ela). Verb agreement carries.
