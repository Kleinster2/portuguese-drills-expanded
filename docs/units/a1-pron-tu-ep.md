## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use **tu** as the standard informal 2nd-person singular in EP — with friends, family, peers, children
- Conjugate verbs in the tu form (-s ending pattern + irregular forms)
- Recognize that **você** in EP is FORMAL/distant (the opposite of BP usage), and that **o senhor / a senhora** is super-formal

## Vocabulary

- **tu** — you (singular, informal) — EP default for any familiar interlocutor
- **você** (EP) — you (formal) — used with strangers, in service contexts, with elders one is not close to
- **o senhor / a senhora** — sir / madam — super-formal address
- **te** — clitic form of *tu* (covered in object-pronoun units)
- **ti** — prepositional form (*para ti, contigo*)

## Grammar

The defining feature: tu takes the **2nd-person-singular ending** in conjugations, distinct from você/ele/ela (3rd-person sg).

### Regular verb endings for tu

| Conjugation | Eu | Tu |
|---|---|---|
| -AR (falar) | falo | falas |
| -ER (comer) | como | comes |
| -IR (partir) | parto | partes |

### Irregular tu forms

| Verbo | Tu |
|---|---|
| ser | és |
| estar | estás |
| ter | tens |
| ir | vais |
| vir | vens |
| fazer | fazes |
| dizer | dizes |
| pôr | pões |
| ver | vês |
| saber | sabes |
| dar | dás |
| querer | queres |
| poder | podes |

### Exemplar sentences

- *Tu **és** português?* — Are you Portuguese?
- *Onde é que tu **moras**?* — Where do you live?
- *Tu **falas** inglês?* — Do you speak English?
- *Tu **queres** um café?* — Do you want a coffee?
- *Tu já **comeste**?* — Have you eaten yet?
- *Tu **tens** tempo amanhã?* — Do you have time tomorrow?
- *Tu **vais** à festa?* — Are you going to the party?

### Register choice in EP

| Pronoun | Register | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| tu | informal | friends, family, peers, children, intimate contexts |
| você | formal/distant | strangers, service interactions, professional contexts |
| o senhor / a senhora | super-formal | elderly strangers, very formal settings |

## Traps

- **BP/EP inversion is the highest-risk error.** *Você* is INFORMAL in BP, FORMAL in EP. A student switching from BP usage to EP must adjust: *Você* with a Portuguese stranger is acceptable formal address, but using it with friends sounds cold or distant. Use *tu* with friends in EP.
- **Tu has its own clitic and prepositional forms.** *Te* (clitic), *ti* (after preposition), *contigo* (with you). These appear in object-pronoun units (`a2-dop-ep`, `a2-iop-ep`).
- **Vós is archaic.** Some grammar tables still list *vós* (with endings *-ais, -eis, -is*) for 2nd-person plural, but it's essentially dead in spoken EP. Use *vocês* for any 2nd-person plural address — even with friends.
- **Verb agreement is rigid.** *Tu falas* — never *tu fala* (which would be the você-form). Native EP speakers immediately register the wrong ending.
