## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the condicional simples in EP (regular -ar/-er/-ir + -ia, -ias, -ia, -íamos, -iam)
- Use it in hypothetical statements (*Eu viajaria para Portugal*) and reported-speech contexts
- Recognize the **imperfeito-de-cortesia substitution**: in informal EP, certain condicional forms are routinely replaced by imperfeito do indicativo (*queria* instead of *quereria*, *gostava* instead of *gostaria*, *podia* instead of *poderia*)

## Vocabulary

Standard condicional simples forms:

| Verb | Eu/ele/ela | Tu | Nós | Eles |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| falar | falaria | falarias | falaríamos | falariam |
| comer | comeria | comerias | comeríamos | comeriam |
| partir | partiria | partirias | partiríamos | partiriam |
| fazer | faria | farias | faríamos | fariam |
| ser | seria | serias | seríamos | seriam |
| ter | teria | terias | teríamos | teriam |

The imperfeito-de-cortesia substitution applies to a small set of high-frequency verbs. The condicional form is grammatical but rarely heard in everyday speech:

| Verb | Prescriptive condicional | Spoken EP form |
|---|---|---|
| querer | quereria | queria |
| gostar | gostaria | gostava |
| poder | poderia | podia |
| dever | deveria | devia |

## Grammar

### Standard condicional formation

Add **-ia, -ias, -ia, -íamos, -iam** to the infinitive. Same rule for all three conjugations:

- *Eu **viajaria** pelo mundo.* — I would travel the world.
- *Tu **comerias** mais se tivesses fome?* — Would you eat more if you were hungry?
- *Nós **partiríamos** amanhã.* — We would leave tomorrow.

Irregular stems (same as futuro do indicativo): **far-** (fazer), **dir-** (dizer), **trar-** (trazer):

- *Eu **faria** isso por ti.* — I'd do that for you.
- *Eu **diria** a verdade.* — I'd tell the truth.

### Use cases

**1. Hypothetical / counterfactual (paired with imperfeito subjuntivo in *se*-clause):**

- *Se eu **fosse** rico, **viajaria** pelo mundo.* — If I were rich, I'd travel the world.

**2. Polite requests (less common in EP than in BP):**

- *Eu **gostaria** de um café, por favor.* — I'd like a coffee, please.

**3. Reported speech / future-in-the-past:**

- *Ele disse que **viria** mais tarde.* — He said he'd come later.

### Imperfeito-de-cortesia substitution

In informal EP, certain condicional forms are routinely replaced by **imperfeito do indicativo** for politeness or softening. The substitution is a register marker — both forms are correct, but the imperfeito sounds more natural in spoken EP.

**The high-frequency substitution set:**

| Prescriptive (rare in speech) | Spoken EP (natural) | English |
|---|---|---|
| Quereria um café. | **Queria** um café. | I'd like a coffee. |
| Gostaria de ver o menu. | **Gostava** de ver a ementa. | I'd like to see the menu. |
| Poderia ajudar-me? | **Podia** ajudar-me? | Could you help me? |
| Deveria estudar mais. | **Devia** estudar mais. | I should study more. |

**Other condicional forms (faria, viria, seria, teria) are NOT routinely substituted** — they appear in hypothetical and reported-speech contexts as expected.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Eu **faria** isso por ti.* — I'd do that for you.
- *Tu **gostarias** de ir ao cinema?* — Would you like to go to the movies? (prescriptive)
- *Tu **gostavas** de ir ao cinema?* — Same meaning, more natural in speech.
- *Eu **queria** falar com a Maria.* — I'd like to speak with Maria. (polite request, EP standard)
- *Ele **diria** que sim.* — He'd say yes. (no substitution — reported speech context)
- *Se **pudesse**, **viajaria** mais.* — If I could, I'd travel more. (hypothetical pair)

## Traps

- **Don't say *quereria* in EP.** It's prescriptively correct but practically nobody uses it. *Queria um café* is the EP standard polite-request form. The Trap-topics file documents this — *quereria* on a worksheet would be a real-session red flag.
- **Don't OVERAPPLY the substitution.** *Faria* doesn't substitute as *fazia* in condicional contexts: *Eu faria isso* (I'd do that) ≠ *Eu fazia isso* (I used to do that — imperfeito habitual). Substitution is restricted to the small high-frequency set.
- **BP/EP shared form, divergent usage.** Form *faria* is the same in both variants, but BP uses condicional more freely in spoken usage where EP gravitates to the imperfeito substitution for the polite-request set.
- **Type 2 conditional (*se eu fosse rico, viajaria*) is intact in formal EP** but in informal EP also substitutable: *Se eu fosse rico, viajava*. The substitution in conditional sentences is covered in `b2-cond-irreal-ep` (sub-batch 5b).
- **Condicional + clitic placement.** Condicional triggers mesoclisis in formal EP (*viajá-lo-ia* — recognize but don't produce). In modern speech, replaced by *Vou viajar com ele* or proclisis when triggered.
