## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Conjugate any verb derived from **pôr** by inheriting the parent's irregular paradigm — present, preterite, imperfeito, futuro, condicional, subjuntivo
- Use the most frequent derivatives in context (*propor, expor, dispor, compor, supor*)
- Recognize less-frequent / formal derivatives (*depor, opor, impor, repor, transpor, pressupor*) without forcing production

## Vocabulary

The pôr family — derivatives are formed by attaching a Latinate prefix to the parent verb. All inherit pôr's full irregular paradigm.

| Verb | Meaning | Frequency / register |
|---|---|---|
| **pôr** | to put, place | foundational |
| **propor** | to propose | high — daily use |
| **expor** | to expose, present | high — daily + formal |
| **compor** | to compose | medium — music/text/figurative |
| **dispor** | to dispose, arrange; to have at one's disposal | medium |
| **supor** | to suppose | medium |
| **impor** | to impose | medium |
| **opor** | to oppose | medium — often as *opor-se* |
| **repor** | to replace, replenish | medium |
| **depor** | to depose; to give testimony (legal) | low — formal/legal |
| **transpor** | to transpose, cross over | low — formal/literary |
| **pressupor** | to presuppose | low — formal/written |
| **antepor** | to place before | rare |
| **contrapor** | to counterpose | rare |

Reflexive forms also exist: *compor-se* (to compose oneself), *dispor-se* (to be willing), *opor-se* (to oppose), *impor-se* (to impose oneself / dominate).

## Grammar

The inheritance rule is total: **every derivative conjugates exactly like pôr in every tense and mood**, with the prefix attached unchanged.

### Pôr paradigm (parent — must know cold)

| Tense | eu | tu | você/ele/ela | nós | vocês/eles/elas |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Presente | ponho | pões | põe | pomos | põem |
| Pretérito | pus | puseste | pôs | pusemos | puseram |
| Imperfeito | punha | punhas | punha | púnhamos | punham |
| Futuro | porei | porás | porá | poremos | porão |
| Condicional | poria | porias | poria | poríamos | poriam |
| Pres. subj. | ponha | ponhas | ponha | ponhamos | ponham |
| Imp. subj. | pusesse | pusesses | pusesse | puséssemos | pusessem |
| Fut. subj. | puser | puseres | puser | pusermos | puserem |
| Particípio | posto | | | | |

### Derivative inheritance — example: propor

Just attach the prefix *pro-* to every form:

| Tense | eu form | 3sg form |
|---|---|---|
| Presente | proponho | propõe |
| Pretérito | propus | propôs |
| Imperfeito | propunha | propunha |
| Futuro | proporei | proporá |
| Pres. subj. | proponha | proponha |
| Imp. subj. | propusesse | propusesse |
| Particípio | proposto | |

The same rule applies to all derivatives — *componho, expomos, dispus, supôs, impuseram, oporia, transpunha, pressuposto*.

### Exemplar sentences

- *O comitê **propôs** uma nova solução.* — The committee proposed a new solution.
- *Eles **expuseram** o problema na reunião.* — They presented the problem at the meeting.
- *A música foi **composta** em 1978.* — The song was composed in 1978.
- *Eu **disponho** de pouco tempo hoje.* — I have little time available today.
- *Ele **se opôs** à proposta.* — He opposed the proposal.
- *A polícia **deteve** o suspeito enquanto a testemunha **depunha**.* — The police detained the suspect while the witness was giving testimony. (deter from ter family + depor from pôr family)
- *Não suponha que ele virá.* — Don't suppose he'll come. (negative imperative = subjuntivo)

## Traps

- **Don't regularize the derivatives.** Students who haven't internalized pôr's irregularity often try *proponhei* or *exponhei* (using -AR endings on the eu-preterite). The correct form is *propus*, *expus* — same irregular pus/puseste/pôs paradigm as the parent.
- **Particípio is *posto*, not *ponhido* or *posto*.** Compounds: *proposto, exposto, composto, disposto, suposto, imposto, oposto, deposto, reposto*. The irregular *posto* form propagates through all derivatives.
- **3sg preterite carries the circumflex.** *Pôs* (he put) — accent distinguishes it from *pos-* prefix. Same for *propôs, compôs, expôs, dispôs, supôs, impôs, opôs, depôs, repôs, transpôs*.
- **Some derivatives are register-restricted.** *Transpor*, *pressupor*, *contrapor* are formal/literary — recognize but don't force production. *Propor* and *expor* are daily-use.
- **Pôr family vs ter family — don't cross-conjugate.** *Compor* uses pôr's pattern (*compus, compôs*). *Conter* uses ter's pattern (*contive, conteve*). Keep the families separate; the prefixes look similar but the parent verbs differ entirely.
