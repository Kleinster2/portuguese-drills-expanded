## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Use impersonal expressions of necessity, possibility, advice, and judgment with subjuntivo presente
- Distinguish impersonals that take subjuntivo (necessity, possibility, judgment) from those that take indicative (assertion of fact)
- Construct generalized claims and recommendations without naming a subject

## Vocabulary

Triggers subjuntivo (uncertainty, necessity, judgment):

- **é importante que** — it's important that
- **é necessário / é preciso que** — it's necessary that
- **é bom que** — it's good that
- **é melhor que** — it's better that
- **é possível que** — it's possible that
- **é provável que** — it's likely that
- **é pena que** — it's a pity that
- **convém que** — it's advisable that
- **basta que** — it's enough that
- **pode ser que** — it might be that

Triggers indicative (assertion of fact — *do not* take subjuntivo):

- **é certo que** — it's certain that
- **é evidente que** — it's evident that
- **é claro que** — it's clear that
- **é óbvio que** — it's obvious that
- **é verdade que** — it's true that

## Grammar

Pattern: **é + adjetivo / substantivo + que + sujeito + verbo no subjuntivo**.

The subjunctive is triggered when the impersonal expression introduces a *judgment, necessity, possibility, or evaluation* — not when it asserts a fact. The form `é certo que ele vem` (assertion) takes indicative; `é possível que ele venha` (possibility) takes subjuntivo.

### Exemplar sentences

- *É importante que você **estude** todos os dias.* — It's important you study every day.
- *É preciso que ele **venha** logo.* — He needs to come soon.
- *É melhor que nós **fiquemos** em casa.* — It's better if we stay home.
- *É possível que **chova** amanhã.* — It might rain tomorrow.
- *É pena que ela não **possa** vir.* — It's a pity she can't come.
- *Pode ser que **dê** certo.* — It might work out.
- *Basta que vocês **avisem** com antecedência.* — All you need to do is let us know in advance.

### Indicative contrast

- *É possível que ele **esteja** cansado.* — He might be tired. (subjuntivo: possibility)
- *É evidente que ele **está** cansado.* — He's clearly tired. (indicative: assertion)

### Personal-infinitive alternative

Many impersonal expressions also accept a **personal infinitive** in place of the subjuntivo *que*-clause. Both forms are correct; the choice is register-driven (personal infinitive is more economical, slightly more formal in writing):

- *É importante **que vocês saibam** disso.* (subjuntivo)
- *É importante **vocês saberem** disso.* (personal infinitive — see [[b2-infinitivo-pessoal-uso]])

The personal-infinitive path skips the *que* and inflects the infinitive for person/number directly. Same set of triggers (importante, necessário, melhor, possível, pena, convém, basta, pode ser) accepts both. See [[b2-infinitivo-pessoal-uso]] for the full inflection paradigm and the choice rules.

## Traps

- **Watch the truth-claiming impersonals.** *É verdade que*, *é certo que*, *é evidente que* trigger indicative because they assert. Default-applying subjuntivo to every "é + adj + que" frame is wrong.
- **Negation flips the indicative ones.** *É verdade que ele vem* (indicative). *Não é verdade que ele venha* (subjuntivo, because negated assertion).
- **BP indicative drift.** Casual BP often says *é importante que você estuda* instead of *estude*. Teach subjuntivo as the target.
- *Pode ser que* always takes subjuntivo: *pode ser que sim*, *pode ser que dê certo*.
