## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Form the pretérito perfeito composto (tenho/tens/tem/temos/têm + particípio passado) in BP
- Use it for **habitual or repeated action over a recent period**, almost always with a temporal adverb (*ultimamente, nesta semana, neste mês, nestes últimos dias*)
- Distinguish it from the pretérito perfeito simples (*comi* = ate once) — the compound is *not* the BP equivalent of English present perfect

## Vocabulary

- **tenho / tens / tem / temos / têm + particípio** — auxiliary + past participle
- Recency anchors that almost always accompany the form in BP:
  - *ultimamente* — lately
  - *nesta semana / neste mês / nestes últimos dias*
  - *recentemente*
  - *nos últimos tempos*

## Grammar

Form: presente do indicativo of *ter* + particípio passado.

| Pessoa | Auxiliar | + particípio |
|---|---|---|
| eu | tenho | falado |
| você / ele / ela | tem | falado |
| nós | temos | falado |
| vocês / eles / elas | têm | falado |

The BP reading is **iterative-recent**: the action has been happening repeatedly over a delimited recent period. It almost always pairs with an adverb that anchors the period. Without the adverb, the form sounds incomplete or EP-flavored to BP ears.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Tenho trabalhado muito ultimamente.* — I've been working a lot lately.
- *Ela tem estudado para a prova nesta semana.* — She's been studying for the exam this week.
- *Não tenho dormido bem nos últimos dias.* — I haven't been sleeping well lately.
- *Vocês têm visto a Maria?* — Have you been seeing Maria around?
- *Temos comido fora demais.* — We've been eating out too much.
- *Ele tem viajado muito a trabalho.* — He's been traveling a lot for work.

### Critical contrast with pretérito perfeito simples

| BP form | Reading | English |
|---|---|---|
| *Eu **comi** pizza ontem.* | one-time past event | I ate pizza yesterday. |
| *Eu **tenho comido** pizza ultimamente.* | recent-iterative | I've been eating pizza lately. |
| *Eu **tenho comido** pizza.* (no anchor) | sounds odd / EP-leaning in BP | (incomplete in BP register) |

## Traps

- **Don't translate English present perfect with this form.** *I have eaten / I have seen the movie* (one-time, single occurrence) → BP pretérito perfeito simples (*comi, vi*), NOT *tenho comido / tenho visto*. The compound carries iterative meaning in BP.
- **Always include the recency anchor in BP production.** *Tenho lido* alone sounds incomplete; *Tenho lido muito ultimamente* is natural.
- **EP usage is much broader.** EP students learn the same form for "I've been doing" without the obligatory adverb anchor — see `b1-pret-perfeito-composto-ep`. Recognize the EP usage when reading or listening; use the BP register in production.
- **Particípios irregulares matter here.** *Tenho dito* (irregular *dito* from *dizer*), *tenho feito* (*feito* from *fazer*), *tenho posto* (*posto* from *pôr*) — review irregular participles before drilling this unit.
