## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Construct **Type 2 (irreal present/future)** conditional sentences in BP using `se + imperfeito subjuntivo + condicional simples`
- Pair the subjuntivo clause and the condicional clause correctly (no indicativo in either slot)
- Use Type 2 to express imagined preferences, hypothetical plans, polite suggestions, and contrary-to-current-reality statements

## Vocabulary

- **se** — if (hypothetical reading)
- Common imperfeito subjuntivo forms (review from `b1-subj-imperfeito`): fosse, tivesse, estivesse, fizesse, pudesse, soubesse, viesse
- Common condicional simples forms (review from `b1-condicional-bp`): seria, teria, faria, poderia, gostaria, viajaria

## Grammar

Type 2 covers situations the speaker treats as **unreal at the moment of speaking** — imagined, hypothetical, or contrary to current circumstance.

The pattern is fixed in BP:

`se + [imperfeito subjuntivo] + [condicional simples]`

Both slots are required. The trigger-hipotetico unit (`b1-subj-trigger-hipotetico`) covers the subjuntivo side; this unit covers the **full sentence pairing** and the condicional side.

### Exemplar sentences

- *Se eu **fosse** rico, **viajaria** pelo mundo.* — If I were rich, I'd travel the world.
- *Se ela **soubesse** a verdade, **me contaria**.* — If she knew the truth, she'd tell me.
- *Se nós **tivéssemos** mais tempo, **terminaríamos** hoje.* — If we had more time, we'd finish today.
- *Se você **estudasse** mais, **passaria** com facilidade.* — If you studied more, you'd pass easily.
- *O que você **faria** se **ganhasse** na loteria?* — What would you do if you won the lottery?
- *Se eles **viessem** amanhã, eu **ficaria** muito feliz.* — If they came tomorrow, I'd be very happy.

### Order is flexible

Either clause can come first; the verb forms don't change:

- *Eu viajaria pelo mundo se fosse rico.*
- *Se fosse rico, eu viajaria pelo mundo.*

### Common error patterns

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| *Se eu **era** rico, viajaria...* | Se eu **fosse** rico, viajaria... | Type 2 needs imperfeito subj, not imperfeito indic |
| *Se eu fosse rico, **vou viajar**...* | Se eu fosse rico, **viajaria**... | Main clause needs condicional, not future |
| *Se eu fosse rico, **viajo**...* | Se eu fosse rico, **viajaria**... | Main clause needs condicional, not present |
| *Se eu **seria** rico, viajaria...* | Se eu **fosse** rico, viajaria... | *se*-clause needs subjuntivo, not condicional |

## Traps

- **The subjuntivo trigger is *se* + hypothetical reading.** Type 1 *se* (real) takes indicativo or futuro do subjuntivo. The hypothetical reading is what triggers imperfeito subjuntivo. See `b1-cond-real-bp` for the contrast.
- **EP-BP divergence is sharp here.** EP commonly says *Se ela soubesse, dizia* (imperfeito indicativo as a condicional substitute). BP says *Se ela soubesse, diria* (condicional simples). When teaching EP, the imperfeito-as-cortesia pattern is the default — this BP unit's *diria* form would sound formal in EP. EP twin will cover this.
- **Polite-request overlap.** `b1-querer-aspect-bp` covers *eu queria um café* (polite-request imperfeito). The Type 2 condicional *eu gostaria de um café* is a more formal alternative. Both are correct; *queria* is more casual.
- **The two slots aren't interchangeable.** *Se eu fosse rico, fosse viajar* — wrong, you can't double the subjuntivo. *Se eu seria rico, viajaria* — wrong, you can't double the condicional. The pair is rigid: subjuntivo + condicional.
