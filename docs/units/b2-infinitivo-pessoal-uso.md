## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Choose **personal infinitive** vs **uninflected (bare) infinitive** based on subject distinctness
- Use the personal infinitive after prepositions (*para, sem, antes de, depois de, ao*) when the subject of the infinitive clause differs from or needs clarification
- Use the personal infinitive with impersonal expressions (*é importante, é necessário, é melhor*) as an alternative to the subjuntivo construction
- Recognize the register effect: personal infinitive is more economical and slightly more formal than subjuntivo equivalents

## Vocabulary

Common triggers / contexts for personal infinitive:

- **Prepositions**: para, sem, antes de, depois de, até, ao, por, em
- **Impersonal expressions**: é importante, é necessário, é melhor, é preciso, é bom, é pena
- **Conjunctions allowing infinitive substitution**: para que (→ para), antes que (→ antes de), sem que (→ sem)
- **Verbs of perception/causation**: ver, ouvir, sentir, deixar, mandar, fazer (with following infinitive clause)

## Grammar

The personal infinitive is **selected over the bare infinitive when the subject of the infinitive clause is distinct from the main-clause subject, or when subject identification needs clarification**. Three primary uses:

### 1. After prepositions, with a distinct subject

The most distinctive use. When the subject of the infinitive clause is different from the main clause's subject, the personal infinitive marks it explicitly:

| Bare infinitive (same subject) | Personal infinitive (distinct subject) |
|---|---|
| *Estudo **para passar** no exame.* — I study to pass the exam. | *Estudo **para os meus pais ficarem** orgulhosos.* — I study so my parents will be proud. |
| *Saí **sem comer**.* — I left without eating. | *Saí **sem eles me verem**.* — I left without them seeing me. |
| *Antes de partir, deixa um recado.* — Before leaving, leave a note. | *Antes de partires, deixa-me um recado.* — Before you leave, leave me a note. |

Even when the subject is the same as the main clause, personal infinitive is sometimes used for clarity, especially in plural contexts:

- *Saímos antes de chegarmos atrasados.* — We left before getting late. (the *-mos* makes plural-subject explicit)

### 2. With impersonal expressions

Personal infinitive competes with subjuntivo presente in impersonal-expression contexts. **Both are correct**; the personal infinitive is more economical and slightly more formal in writing:

| Subjuntivo (`b1-subj-trigger-impessoal`) | Personal infinitive |
|---|---|
| *É importante que eles **saibam** disso.* | *É importante eles **saberem** disso.* |
| *É necessário que vocês **cheguem** cedo.* | *É necessário vocês **chegarem** cedo.* |
| *É melhor que nós **fiquemos** em casa.* | *É melhor nós **ficarmos** em casa.* |
| *É pena que tu não **possas** vir.* | *É pena tu não **poderes** vir.* |

The subjuntivo version is more emphatic and feels more "complete" syntactically; the personal-infinitive version is leaner and reads as slightly more formal in written contexts. Choice is register-driven.

### 3. After verbs of perception and causation

With *ver, ouvir, sentir, deixar, mandar, fazer* + infinitive clause, the personal infinitive is used when the subject of the embedded verb is plural or distinct:

- *Vi **as crianças correrem** pelo parque.* — I saw the children run through the park.
- *Ouvimos **os pássaros cantarem** ao amanhecer.* — We heard the birds sing at dawn.
- *Deixei **os miúdos brincarem** mais um pouco.* — I let the kids play a bit longer.
- *Mandei **o mecânico arranjar** o carro.* — I had the mechanic fix the car.

For singular subjects, both bare and personal infinitive are acceptable:

- *Vi **o João correr**.* / *Vi **o João correr**.* — same form, no plural marking needed.

### When to keep the bare infinitive

When the subject of the infinitive clause is **the same as the main clause** AND **obvious from context**, use the bare infinitive — adding a personal ending sounds redundant:

- *Eu quero **comer**.* — not *eu quero eu comer*.
- *Posso **falar** contigo?* — not *posso eu falar*.
- *Gosto de **viajar**.* — not *gosto de eu viajar*.

After modal-like verbs (*poder, querer, dever, ir, vir*) and aspectual verbs (*começar a, acabar de, continuar a*), the bare infinitive is the natural choice — these verbs are aspectual extensions of the main verb, not clause introducers with their own subject.

### Form-collision disambiguation

Personal infinitive forms often collide with **futuro do subjuntivo** forms (especially -ER/-IR verbs). The trigger context disambiguates:

| Form | Trigger | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| *comermos* | after *para* / impersonal expression | personal infinitive |
| *comermos* | after *quando* / *assim que* | futuro do subjuntivo |

- *Para **comermos** bem, escolhemos um bom restaurante.* — Personal infinitive (preposition *para*).
- *Quando **comermos**, conversamos.* — Futuro do subjuntivo (temporal conjunction *quando*).

### Exemplar set (mixed)

- *Foi bom **tu teres vindo**.* — It was good that you came.
- *Para **eles entenderem**, é preciso explicar devagar.* — For them to understand, you need to explain slowly.
- *Antes de **sairmos**, vamos rever a lista.* — Before we leave, let's review the list.
- *É melhor **vocês ficarem** aqui.* — It's better for you all to stay here.
- *Sem **nós sabermos**, os planos mudaram.* — Without our knowing, the plans changed.
- *Vi **as crianças brincarem** no jardim.* — I saw the children play in the garden.
- *É importante **eles serem** honestos.* — It's important for them to be honest.

## Traps

- **Don't overuse personal infinitive in colloquial speech.** Spoken BP often defaults to the bare infinitive even where personal infinitive would be technically more precise. *É importante eles saber* (colloquial BP, with bare infinitive) is heard, though prescriptively *saberem*. Spoken EP and formal BP/EP both prefer the personal-inflected form.
- **The personal-infinitive vs subjuntivo choice is register, not error.** A B2 student writing an essay should generally prefer personal infinitive after impersonal expressions (more economical, more "writerly"). A student speaking informally with friends might use subjuntivo or even just bare infinitive. None is wrong.
- **Form collision with futuro do subjuntivo is the highest-error point.** Disambiguate by trigger word: *para / sem / antes de / é importante* → personal infinitive. *quando / assim que / se* → futuro do subjuntivo. The forms are identical for many verbs; the trigger is the only signal.
- **Don't use personal infinitive after modal verbs.** *Quero falares* is wrong (or marked); use *quero que tu fales* (subjuntivo with overt subject) or *queres falar?* (different question entirely). Modals take bare infinitive when subjects align, subjuntivo with *que* when distinct.
- **Bare-infinitive default in same-subject contexts is universal.** *Vou comer* (I'm going to eat) is right; *vou eu comer* is wrong (the personal ending is redundant when subject is identical to main clause). Personal infinitive's job is to mark distinctness or clarification — when there's nothing to mark, don't inflect.
- **The cross-link to subjuntivo is real, not just a footnote.** See [[b1-subj-trigger-impessoal]] for the subjuntivo path with impersonal expressions. Both paths exist for the same construction frame; mature B2 production includes both.
