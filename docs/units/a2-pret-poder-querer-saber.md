## Outcomes

- Talk about what they could or couldn't do (pude/não pude)
- Express past desires and intentions (quis/não quis)
- Talk about finding out information (soube)
- Understand the nuanced meaning shifts in the past tense
- Combine with other past tense verbs for richer storytelling

## Vocabulary

- **Three more essential irregular verbs in preterite:**
  - poder (to be able to / can)
  - querer (to want)
  - saber (to know - facts/information)
- **Additional vocabulary:**
  - nada (nothing)
  - tudo (everything)
  - a verdade (the truth)
  - a resposta (the answer)
  - a notícia (the news)
  - comprar (to buy - regular verb for examples)
  - vir (to come - for context, but conjugation comes later)

## Grammar

- **Preterite tense for poder, querer, and saber**
- These three verbs often change meaning slightly in the past:
  - **poder** (present: can/am able to) → (past: could/was able to/managed to)
  - **querer** (present: want) → (past: wanted/tried to)
  - **saber** (present: know) → (past: found out/learned)
- Conjugation patterns:
  - **poder**: pude, pôde, pudemos, puderam
  - **querer**: quis, quis, quisemos, quiseram
  - **saber**: soube, soube, soubemos, souberam
- All three are highly irregular with unique stems

### Exemplar sentences

- Pude ir (I was able to go / I managed to go)
- Não pude trabalhar ontem (I couldn't work yesterday)
- Quis viajar, mas não pude (I wanted to travel, but couldn't)
- Ele não quis comer (He didn't want to eat / He refused to eat)
- Soube a notícia hoje (I found out the news today)
- Quando você soube? (When did you find out?)
- Não soube a resposta (I didn't know the answer)
- Pudemos ver tudo (We were able to see everything)
