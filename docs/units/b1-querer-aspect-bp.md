## Outcomes

- Distinguish **quis** (acted on wanting / attempted / in negation, refused) from **queria** (wanted as an ongoing state, often unrealized)
- Recognize **não quis** as "refused," not "didn't want"
- Use **queria** for polite requests at counters, restaurants, on the phone (imperfeito de cortesia)

## Vocabulary

Triggering markers for **quis** (action taken on a desire):
- ontem, naquele momento, naquela hora
- mas / porém + a frustration: *quis falar, mas...*
- Negative: *não quis* = refused (a strong reading, not a soft "didn't want to")

Triggering markers for **queria** (ongoing desire, often unrealized):
- sempre, há anos, desde criança
- mas + a constraint: *queria viajar, mas não tive tempo*
- Polite request frame: *Eu queria um café, por favor.*

## Grammar

- **quis** = tried to / acted on wanting → punctual action driven by desire; in the negative, "refused"
- **queria** = wanted (state) → ongoing desire that may or may not have been acted on
- **queria** in present-time polite requests → softens the request below "quero" (which sounds blunt)

### Exemplar pairs

- *Eu **quis** falar com ele, mas estava ocupado.* — I tried to talk to him, but he was busy.
- *Eu **queria** falar com ele há semanas.* — I'd been wanting to talk to him for weeks.

- *Ele não **quis** vir.* — He refused to come. (active refusal)
- *Ele não **queria** vir.* — He didn't want to come. (state — may have come anyway)

- *Eu **quis** ajudar, mas ela recusou.* — I tried to help, but she refused.
- *Eu **queria** ajudar, mas não sabia como.* — I wanted to help, but didn't know how.

- *Eu **queria** um café, por favor.* — I'd like a coffee, please. (polite request)
- *Eu **quero** um café.* — I want a coffee. (direct, can sound blunt)

## Traps

- **"He didn't want to" is not "ele não quis."** *Não quis* = "he refused" (active). For the neutral state-reading, use *não queria*. This is the highest-frequency error point.
- **"I wanted X" defaults to queria, not quis.** *Quis* implies an attempt; *queria* describes a desire. Default English "wanted" maps to *queria* in most contexts.
- **Polite-request queria is a register move, not an aspect move.** *Eu queria um café* is the standard counter-order register in BP; *eu quero um café* is grammatical but reads as terse or impatient. EP additionally uses *queria* heavily for politeness — covered in the EP twin.
- *Quis* in the affirmative often pairs with a frustration: *quis comprar, mas estava fechado* (tried to buy, but it was closed). If no frustration is in the air, the speaker probably means *queria*.
