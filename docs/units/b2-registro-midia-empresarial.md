## Outcomes

After this unit, the student can:

- Read and produce **media texts** (news headlines, news ledes, opinion pieces) using the conventional reporting verbs and compressed-syntax patterns
- Read and produce **business correspondence** (letters, emails, reports) using formulaic salutations, sign-offs, and corporate vocabulary
- Recognize the **BP/EP divergences** in business-letter conventions (salutations, sign-offs, vocabulary)
- Avoid register-inversion errors (formal patterns in casual contexts and vice versa)

## Vocabulary

### News reporting verbs

The journalistic register has a stylized inventory of verbs for reporting speech acts:

| Verb | Connotation |
|---|---|
| **afirmar** | to state, assert |
| **declarar** | to declare (formal/official) |
| **sustentar** | to maintain / argue |
| **defender** | to defend a position |
| **anunciar** | to announce |
| **revelar** | to reveal (often with new-information undertone) |
| **garantir** | to guarantee / assure |
| **negar** | to deny |
| **rejeitar** | to reject |
| **refutar** | to refute / counter |
| **alertar para** | to warn about |
| **chamar a atenção para** | to draw attention to |
| **frisar / sublinhar** | to emphasize / underline |

These reporting verbs replace the bland *disse* / *falou* in formal news writing. *O ministro disse* sounds informal/casual; *O ministro afirmou* / *declarou* / *sustentou* sounds journalistically appropriate.

### Headline conventions — compressed syntax

News headlines compress syntax aggressively:

- **Articles dropped**: *(O) Presidente anuncia novas medidas* → *Presidente anuncia novas medidas*
- **Verb dropped (with implied "ser/estar")**: *(É) Crise económica em três frentes* → *Crise económica em três frentes*
- **Present-tense reporting of past events**: *Banco central sobe juros* (the rate hike happened today, but reported in present)
- **Colon to introduce attribution or topic**: *Crise: governo prepara plano de emergência*

These compressions are headline-specific. The article body returns to standard grammar.

### Business-letter formulas

Standard salutation and sign-off conventions for formal business correspondence:

#### Salutations (opening)

| Context | BP convention | EP convention |
|---|---|---|
| Generic formal | *Prezado(a) Sr.(a)…* | *Exmo.(a) Sr.(a)…* |
| Named recipient | *Prezado Sr. Silva* | *Exmo. Sr. Silva* |
| Less formal (known recipient) | *Caro(a) [nome]* | *Caro(a) [nome]* (shared) |
| Plural / unknown recipient | *Prezados Senhores* | *Exmos. Senhores* |

The BP *Prezado* and EP *Exmo. (Excelentíssimo)* differ — direct transfer between variants sounds odd.

#### Sign-offs (closing)

| Register | BP convention | EP convention |
|---|---|---|
| Standard formal | *Atenciosamente,* | *Com os melhores cumprimentos,* / *Atenciosamente,* |
| Slightly more personal | *Cordialmente,* | *Cordialmente,* (shared) |
| Letter close (informal) | *Abraço,* / *Um abraço,* | *Um abraço,* (shared) |

EP *Com os melhores cumprimentos* is the canonical formal sign-off; BP rarely uses this phrase in business letters (it would sound translated or stilted).

#### Body-text formulas

- **Opening reference**: *Em referência ao seu email de [data]* / *Reportando-me ao seu email…* (EP) / *Em resposta ao seu email…*
- **Attaching documents**: *Segue em anexo…* / *Anexo encontra…* (EP) / *Encaminho em anexo…* (BP)
- **Requesting**: *Solicitamos a gentileza de…* / *Agradecemos que…* / *Pedimos que…*
- **Confirming receipt**: *Confirmamos a receção do seu email* (EP) / *Confirmamos o recebimento do seu email* (BP)

### Corporate vocabulary

Modern business Portuguese has accumulated formulaic phrases — many translated from English management-speak:

- **alinhar expectativas** — to align expectations
- **agregar valor** — to add value
- **dar follow-up / fazer follow-up** — to follow up (often used in English even in Portuguese contexts)
- **brainstorming** — same in Portuguese
- **stakeholder** — usually kept in English; sometimes *parte interessada*
- **a curto / médio / longo prazo** — short/medium/long term
- **margem de manobra** — room to maneuver
- **prestar contas** — to be accountable / report back

These phrases are register-marked: they signal "I work in a professional environment" and would sound incongruous in casual conversation.

## Grammar

The media + business register doesn't introduce new grammar so much as it **selects** specific constructions from the standard inventory:

### 1. Passive voice for reporting

News-style reporting often uses passive constructions to depersonalize:

- *Foi anunciado que… / Foi confirmado que…*
- *O acordo foi assinado pelas duas partes.*
- *O projeto foi rejeitado pelo conselho.*

See [[b1-passiva-ser]] for the passive system.

### 2. Impersonal-se for generic reporting

The passive-se construction (see [[b1-passiva-se]]) appears in news-style generic statements:

- *Discutiu-se a proposta no parlamento.*
- *Procura-se solução para o impasse.*
- *Investiga-se a origem do incidente.*

### 3. Condicional for attributed claims (Conditional reporting)

When reporting unverified claims, journalistic Portuguese uses condicional to mark the claim as not-yet-confirmed:

- *Segundo fontes, o ministro **teria** participado da reunião.* — According to sources, the minister allegedly participated. (the *teria* signals attribution, not confirmation)
- *O presidente **teria** dito que…* — The president allegedly said that…

This use of condicional ("conditional reporting") is a well-defined journalistic convention. A reader interprets the *teria* as "according to the source, but unverified."

### 4. Subjuntivo for editorial / opinion register

Opinion-piece writing leans on impersonal subjuntivo for editorial commentary:

- *É lamentável que se tenha chegado a este ponto.*
- *Importa que o governo aja com rapidez.*
- *Convém recordar que existem alternativas.*

### Exemplar texts

**News headline:**
> *Banco central sobe juros: economia em retração no terceiro trimestre*

(Compressed syntax, present-tense reporting of recent events, colon introducing topic.)

**News lede:**
> *O Banco Central de Portugal anunciou ontem uma subida dos juros de referência em 0,25 pontos percentuais. A decisão, segundo o comunicado, visa controlar a inflação que se mantém acima da meta. Analistas alertam para o risco de retração do consumo.*

(Reporting verb *anunciou*, attribution *segundo o comunicado*, impersonal *se mantém*, journalistic verb *alertar para*.)

**Business email opening (BP):**
> *Prezado Sr. Silva,*
>
> *Em resposta ao seu email de ontem, confirmamos o recebimento dos documentos solicitados. Encaminhamos em anexo o relatório atualizado.*

**Business email opening (EP):**
> *Exmo. Sr. Silva,*
>
> *Reportando-me ao seu email de ontem, confirmo a receção dos documentos solicitados. Segue em anexo o relatório atualizado.*

(Note the salutation difference, the *recebimento* / *receção* vocabulary divergence, and the slightly different opening phrases.)

**Business email sign-off (EP):**
> *Com os melhores cumprimentos,*
> *[Nome]*

**Business email sign-off (BP):**
> *Atenciosamente,*
> *[Nome]*

## Traps

- **Don't translate English business clichés literally.** *Touch base* doesn't translate well; use *contactar* or *entrar em contacto*. *Reach out* is similarly awkward in literal translation; use *contactar* or *enviar uma mensagem a*.
- **Salutation register matters.** Using EP *Exmo.* in a BP business letter sounds pompous; using BP *Prezado* in an EP business letter sounds Brazilian-translated. Match the variant of the recipient.
- **Conditional reporting is not the same as personal hedging.** *O ministro teria dito* (he allegedly said — journalistic attribution) ≠ *Eu diria que* (I'd say — personal hedge). Both use condicional but in different rhetorical positions.
- **Headline compression doesn't transfer to body text.** *Presidente anuncia* in a headline is fine; in the article body, restore articles: *O presidente anunciou…*
- **Don't over-use management-speak vocabulary.** *Vamos alinhar expectativas para agregar valor às margens de manobra dos stakeholders* is technically grammatical but signals jargon-overload. Use formulaic phrases sparingly and substantively.
- **BP/EP vocabulary divergence is sharpest in business contexts.** *Recebimento* (BP) vs *receção* (EP), *encaminhar* (BP-leaning for forwarding) vs *enviar* (more shared). When writing for an audience of unknown variant, lean toward shared vocabulary.
- **Reporting verb register match.** *Disse* in news writing is too informal — use *afirmou, declarou, sustentou*. But also: don't overload one piece with stylized verbs (*o presidente sustentou, o ministro defendeu, o porta-voz declarou, o analista alertou para…*) — vary including occasional plain *disse* or paraphrase.
