# Syllabus Components - What Each Progression Introduces

**Last Updated:** 2025-01-07

---

## 1. JOHN - eu (First Person Singular)

### NEW Pronouns
- **eu** (I)

### NEW Verbs (First Person Singular)
- **sou** (am) - from ser
- **moro** (live) - from morar
- **trabalho** (work) - from trabalhar
- **falo** (speak) - from falar
- **gosto** (like) - from gostar
- **vou** (go) - from ir
- **tenho** (have) - from ter
- **estou** (am) - from estar

### NEW Prepositions & Contractions
- **de** (from, of)
- **em** (in, at)
- **na** = em + a (at the, on the - feminine)
- **no** = em + o (at the - masculine)
- **do** = de + o (from the - masculine)
- **das** = de + as (from the - feminine plural)
- **como** (as)
- **para** (to, for)

### NEW Adjectives (Masculine)
- **americano** (American)
- **analista** (analyst - invariable)
- **casado** (married)
- **contente** (happy - invariable)

### NEW Nouns (Masculine)
- **o John** (John)
- **Miami** (Miami - city, no article)
- **analista** (analyst)
- **o cachorro** (dog)
- **os irmãos** (brothers)
- **o trabalho** (work/job)
- **o metrô** (subway)

### NEW Nouns (Feminine)
- **a Sarah** (Sarah)
- **Nova York** (New York - city, no article)
- **a avenida** (avenue)
- **as Américas** (Americas)
- **a Acme** (Acme - company name)
- **a música** (music)
- **a gata** (cat - female)
- **as irmãs** (sisters)
- **a casa** (house, home)

### NEW Adverbs
- **agora** (now)

### NEW Conjunctions
- **e** (and)

### NEW Numbers
- **um/uma** (one)
- **dois/duas** (two)

### NEW Expressions
- **um pouco de** (a little bit of)

### NEW Languages
- **inglês** (English)
- **espanhol** (Spanish)
- **português** (Portuguese)

### NEW Locations
- Miami
- Nova York
- avenida das Américas
- casa (home)

### NEW Verb Phrases
- **vou de metrô** (I go by subway)
- **gosto de** (I like)
- **trabalho como** (I work as)

## 2. SARAH - eu + também (First Person Singular with Variation)

### NEW Adverbs
- **também** (also, too) - introduced in TWO positions:
  - Beginning: **Também** sou de Miami
  - End: Sou americana **também**

### Reinforces from JOHN
- All verbs in first person singular
- Feminine adjective agreement: **americana**, **casada**, **analista**, **contente**
- Same nouns with proper gender articles

### Teaching Point
- Shows "também" can appear at beginning OR end of sentence
- Natural variation in Portuguese word order

---

## 3. A GENTE - we informal (Singular Conjugation for Plural Meaning)

### NEW Pronouns
- **a gente** (we - informal, literally "the people")

### NEW Teaching Point: Singular Form for Plural Meaning
- Uses **SINGULAR** verb conjugations despite meaning "we":
  - a gente **é** (not somos)
  - a gente **mora** (not moramos)
  - a gente **trabalha** (not trabalhamos)
  - a gente **fala** (not falamos)
  - a gente **gosta** (not gostamos)
  - a gente **vai** (not vamos)
  - a gente **tem** (not temos)
  - a gente **está** (not estamos)

### NEW Adjectives (Singular despite plural meaning)
- **americano** (not americanos)
- **analista** (not analistas)
- **casado** (not casados)
- **contente** (not contentes)

### NEW Expressions
- **cada** (each) - "dois irmãos e duas irmãs cada"

### Teaching Point
- A gente = informal "we" in Brazilian Portuguese
- Always conjugates as 3rd person singular (like ele/ela)
- Uses masculine singular adjectives by default

---

## 4. VOCÊ - addressing someone (Second Person, Singular Conjugation)

### NEW Pronouns
- **você** (you - singular, formal)

### NEW Teaching Point: Third Person Forms for "You"
- Uses same conjugations as ele/ela:
  - você **é** (you are)
  - você **mora** (you live)
  - você **trabalha** (you work)
  - você **fala** (you speak)
  - você **gosta** (you like)
  - você **vai** (you go)
  - você **tem** (you have)
  - você **está** (you are)

### Reinforces
- All previous vocabulary
- Feminine agreement when addressing Sarah: **americana**, **casada**

### Teaching Point
- Você is formal/neutral "you" in Brazilian Portuguese
- Conjugates like third person singular, NOT second person
- Context change: from self-introduction → addressing others

---

## 5. VOCÊS - addressing multiple people (Second Person Plural)

### NEW Pronouns
- **vocês** (you all, y'all - plural)

### NEW Verb Forms (Third Person Plural)
- **são** (are) - from ser
- **moram** (live) - from morar
- **trabalham** (work) - from trabalhar
- **falam** (speak) - from falar
- **gostam** (like) - from gostar
- **vão** (go) - from ir
- **têm** (have) - from ter - NOTE: circumflex accent distinguishes from "tem" (singular)
- **estão** (are) - from estar

### NEW Adjectives (Plural)
- **americanos** (Americans - masculine plural)
- **analistas** (analysts - plural)
- **casados** (married - masculine plural)
- **contentes** (happy - plural)

### Teaching Point
- Vocês = "you all" / "y'all"
- Conjugates as third person plural (like eles/elas)
- First introduction of plural verb forms
- Accent on "têm" distinguishes plural from singular

---

## 6. ELE - describing a male (Third Person Singular Masculine)

### NEW Pronouns
- **ele** (he)

### NEW Verb Forms (Third Person Singular)
- **é** (is) - from ser
- **mora** (lives) - from morar
- **trabalha** (works) - from trabalhar
- **fala** (speaks) - from falar
- **gosta** (likes) - from gostar
- **vai** (goes) - from ir
- **tem** (has) - from ter
- **está** (is) - from estar

### Reinforces
- Masculine adjectives: **americano**, **analista**, **casado**, **contente**
- Same conjugations as você and a gente

### Teaching Point
- Third person singular: ele, ela, você, a gente ALL use same conjugations
- Context change: from addressing → describing others

---

## 7. ELA - describing a female (Third Person Singular Feminine)

### NEW Pronouns
- **ela** (she)

### Reinforces
- Same verb conjugations as ele/você/a gente
- Feminine adjectives: **americana**, **analista**, **casada**, **contente**

### Teaching Point
- Only difference from "ele" is feminine agreement on adjectives
- Verb forms stay identical

---

## 8. NÓS - we formal (First Person Plural)

### NEW Pronouns
- **nós** (we - formal/standard)

### NEW Verb Forms (First Person Plural)
- **somos** (are) - from ser
- **moramos** (live) - from morar
- **trabalhamos** (work) - from trabalhar
- **falamos** (speak) - from falar
- **gostamos** (like) - from gostar
- **vamos** (go) - from ir
- **temos** (have) - from ter
- **estamos** (are) - from estar

### NEW Adjectives (Plural)
- **americanos** (Americans - masculine plural)
- **analistas** (analysts - plural)
- **casados** (married - masculine plural)
- **contentes** (happy - plural)

### Teaching Point
- Nós = formal/standard "we"
- First person plural conjugations (different from vocês/eles)
- Can drop pronoun (like eu): "Somos americanos"
- Regular -ar verbs: add -amos (trabalhamos)
- Contrast with "a gente" (informal we, singular conjugation)

---

## 9. ELES - describing multiple people (Third Person Plural)

### NEW Pronouns
- **eles** (they - masculine or mixed gender)

### Reinforces
- Same plural conjugations as vocês:
  - **são, moram, trabalham, falam, gostam, vão, têm, estão**
- Plural adjectives: **americanos**, **analistas**, **casados**, **contentes**

### Teaching Point
- Eles = "they" (masculine or mixed)
- Elas = "they" (all feminine) - not shown but implied
- Uses third person plural (same as vocês)
- Context: describing others (not addressing them)

---

## Summary: Conjugation Patterns

### Singular Conjugations (4 pronouns use these)
- **eu**: sou, moro, trabalho, falo, gosto, vou, tenho, estou
- **você/ele/ela/a gente**: é, mora, trabalha, fala, gosta, vai, tem, está

### Plural Conjugations (3 pronouns use these)
- **nós**: somos, moramos, trabalhamos, falamos, gostamos, vamos, temos, estamos
- **vocês/eles/elas**: são, moram, trabalham, falam, gostam, vão, têm, estão

## Pedagogical Progression

1. **JOHN**: Full foundation - all basic elements
2. **SARAH**: Adds "também" variation (beginning/end positions)
3. **A GENTE**: Introduces singular conjugation for plural meaning
4. **VOCÊ**: Reuses ele/ela conjugations in new context (addressing)
5. **VOCÊS**: First plural conjugations
6. **ELE**: Third person singular (describing others)
7. **ELA**: Reinforces with feminine agreement
8. **NÓS**: First person plural, can drop pronoun
9. **ELES**: Third person plural (describing others)

**Total New Elements Introduced:** ~100+ vocabulary items, 64 unique verb conjugations (8 verbs × 8 forms), multiple grammar patterns
