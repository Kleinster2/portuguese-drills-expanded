## Pronunciation vs. Reduction System

### **Two Distinct Phenomena**

The syllabus separates two fundamentally different types of phonetic changes:

| Type | When It Happens | Notation | Example |
|------|-----------------|----------|---------|
| **PRONUNCIATION** | Always (all registers) | [brackets] in Steps 1-4 | de [dji] Miami |
| **REDUCTION** | Optional (colloquial only) | No brackets, different spelling | sou → sô |

### **Steps 1-4: Standard Orthography + [Compact] Pronunciation Notation**

Write words as they appear in standard Portuguese, but use **compact notation** to show pronunciation:

```
Eu sou de[dji] Miami.           ← [dji] shows only the sound change
Eu tenho[u] um gato[u].          ← [u] shows final o→u change
Eu gosto[u] de[dji] música.      ← Multiple annotations in one sentence
Eu estou contente[tchi].         ← [tchi] shows te→tchi change
```

**Compact notation format**: `word[sound]` - only the changed sound appears in brackets, not the whole word.

**What the brackets mean**: "This part of the word is pronounced differently than written."

### **Step 5: Phonetic Orthography (Brackets Removed)**

Write words as they're pronounced in maximum colloquial speech:

```
Sô dji Miami.                    ← "dji" written directly
Tenhu um gato.                   ← "tenhu" written directly
Gostchi dji música.              ← "gostchi dji" written directly
```

**No brackets needed**: The spelling represents the pronunciation.

### **What Gets Annotated (Using Compact Notation)**

✅ **Final unstressed o→u** (obligatory, ALL instances):
- americano[u], brasileiro[u], casado[u], solteiro[u]
- moro[u], trabalho[u], falo[u], gosto[u], tenho[u]
- cachorro[u], gato[u], pouco[u], escritório[u]
- como[u], São Paulo[u]

✅ **Palatalization** (obligatory in Brazilian Portuguese):
- `de` + /i/ sound → `de[dji]`: de[dji] Miami, de[dji] música, de[dji] espanhol
- `te` + /i/ sound → `[tchi]`: contente[tchi]
- Coalescence: de[djô]nibus (Carlos)

✅ **Final unstressed e→i** (when applicable):
- nome[i], fome[i] (examples for future use)
- Plural: nomes[is] (future use)

✅ **Epenthetic [i] on consonant-final words** (borrowed words):
- Words ending in consonants add [i] in Portuguese pronunciation
- **If creates ti/di → palatalization applies**:
  - Internet (ends in -t) → Interne**ti** → Internet[chi] (palatalized)
  - iPad (ends in -d) → iPa**di** → iPad[ji] (palatalized)
- **Otherwise, just [i]**:
  - Facebook (ends in -k) → Faceboo**ki** → Facebook[i]
  - York (ends in -k) → Yor**ki** → York[i] (as in "Nova York[i]")
  - Whatsapp (ends in -p) → Whatsa**pi** → Whatsapp[i]

❌ **NOT annotated** (per pedagogical decision):
- Sibilant sounds (s → sh)
- R-sound variations

### **Why This System Works**

1. **Linguistic accuracy**: Separates obligatory phonetics from optional reductions
2. **Prevents fossilization**: Students learn correct pronunciation from Step 1
3. **Clear progression**: Standard writing → Phonetic representation
4. **Compact & readable**: word[sound] format doesn't clutter text
5. **Systematic coverage**: Annotating ALL instances makes patterns visible
6. **Pattern recognition**: Students see that o→u is obligatory everywhere
7. **Transparent**: [brackets] explicitly show "this part sounds different"

## 1. JOHN - eu (First Person Singular)

### NEW Pronouns
- **eu** (I)

### NEW Verbs (First Person Singular)
- **sou** (am) - from ser
- **moro** (live) - from morar
- **trabalho** (work) - from trabalhar
- **falo** (speak) - from falar
- **gosto** (like) - from gostar
- **vou** (go) - from ir
- **tenho** (have) - from ter
- **estou** (am) - from estar

### NEW Prepositions & Contractions
- **de** (from, of)
- **em** (in, at)
- **na** = em + a (at the, on the - feminine)
- **no** = em + o (at the - masculine)
- **do** = de + o (from the - masculine)
- **das** = de + as (from the - feminine plural)
- **como** (as)
- **para** (to, for)

### NEW Adjectives (Masculine)
- **americano** (American)
- **analista** (analyst - invariable)
- **casado** (married)
- **contente** (happy - invariable)

### NEW Nouns (Masculine)
- **o John** (John)
- **Miami** (Miami - city, no article)
- **analista** (analyst)
- **o cachorro** (dog)
- **os irmãos** (brothers)
- **o trabalho** (work/job)
- **o metrô** (subway)

### NEW Nouns (Feminine)
- **a Sarah** (Sarah)
- **Nova York** (New York - city, no article)
- **a avenida** (avenue)
- **as Américas** (Americas)
- **a Acme** (Acme - company name)
- **a música** (music)
- **a gata** (cat - female)
- **as irmãs** (sisters)
- **a casa** (house, home)

### NEW Adverbs
- **agora** (now)

### NEW Conjunctions
- **e** (and)

### NEW Numbers
- **um/uma** (one)
- **dois/duas** (two)

### NEW Expressions
- **um pouco de** (a little bit of)

### NEW Languages
- **inglês** (English)
- **espanhol** (Spanish)
- **português** (Portuguese)

### NEW Locations
- Miami
- Nova York
- avenida das Américas
- casa (home)

### NEW Verb Phrases
- **vou de metrô** (I go by subway)
- **gosto de** (I like)
- **trabalho como** (I work as)

### Step 4: Dropping/Elision (rule-based)
- **sou** → **sô** (vowel reduction)
- **estou** → **tô** (vowel reduction)
- **para o** → **pro** (preposition+article contraction)
- **vou** → **vou** (no change, but prepares for Step 5)
- Infinitive -r dropping: **falar** → **falá**, **viajar** → **viajá**

### Step 5: Full Colloquial Speech (all phonetic processes)
- **vou** → **vô** (diphthong reduction)
- **tenho** → **tenhu** (nasal vowel + /o/ → /u/)
- **de + word starting with /i/** → **dji**:
  - **de Miami** → **dji Miami**
  - **de espanhol** → **djispanhol**
- **gosto de** → **gostchi** + **de** → **dji** (palatalization + coalescence)
- **um pouco de** → **um pocu dji** (/o/ reduction, palatalization)
- Article elision: **sou o John** → **sô John**
- **Vowel sandhi (word boundary coalescence)**:
  - **na avenida** → **navenida** (vowel merges across word boundary)
  - **na Acme** → **nAcme** (identical vowels merge into one)

## 2. SARAH - eu + também (First Person Singular with Variation)

### NEW Adverbs
- **também** (also, too) - introduced in TWO positions:
  - Beginning: **Também** sou de Miami
  - End: Sou americana **também**

### Reinforces from JOHN
- All verbs in first person singular
- Feminine adjective agreement: **americana**, **casada**, **analista**, **contente**
- Same nouns with proper gender articles

### Teaching Point
- Shows "também" can appear at beginning OR end of sentence
- Natural variation in Portuguese word order

### Step 4: Dropping/Elision (rule-based)
Same reductions as JOHN:
- **sou** → **sô**
- **estou** → **tô**
- **para o** → **pro**
- Infinitive -r dropping: **falar** → **falá**, **viajar** → **viajá**

### Step 5: Full Colloquial Speech (all phonetic processes)
Same phonetic processes as JOHN, with "também" variations:
- **vou** → **vô**
- **tenho** → **tenhu**
- **de + /i/** → **dji** (de Miami → dji Miami, de espanhol → djispanhol)
- **gosto de** → **gostchi dji**
- **um pouco de** → **um pocu dji**
- Article elision: **sou a Sarah** → **sô Sarah**
- **Vowel sandhi**: **na avenida** → **navenida**, **na Acme** → **nAcme**

## 3. CARLOS - eu (Brazilian Colleague)

### NEW Character
- **o Carlos** (Carlos)
- Brazilian male colleague at Acme

### NEW Adjectives (Masculine)
- **brasileiro** (Brazilian)
- **solteiro** (single/unmarried)

### NEW Nouns (Masculine)
- **o colega** (colleague)
- **São Paulo** (city in Brazil)
- **Brooklyn** (neighborhood in NYC)
- **o futebol** (soccer/football)
- **o gato** (cat - male)
- **o ônibus** (bus)
- **o escritório** (office)

### NEW Nouns (Feminine)
- **uma irmã** (one sister - singular)

### NEW Expressions
- **colega do John e da Sarah** (colleague of John and Sarah)
- Reinforces **do** = de + o (masculine)
- Reinforces **da** = de + a (feminine)

### Transportation Variation
- **Vou de ônibus** (I go by bus) - contrasts with John/Sarah's "vou de metrô"
- **para o escritório** (to the office) - introduces "escritório" vs "trabalho"

### Step 4: Dropping/Elision (rule-based)
Same reductions as JOHN and SARAH:
- **sou** → **sô**
- **estou** → **tô**
- **para o** → **pro** (para o escritório → pro escritório)
- Infinitive -r dropping: **falar** → **falá**

### Step 5: Full Colloquial Speech (all phonetic processes)
Same phonetic processes as JOHN and SARAH, with Carlos-specific vocabulary:
- **vou** → **vô**
- **tenho** → **tenhu**
- **de ônibus** → **djônibus** (coalescence /de/ + /o/ → /djo/)
- **gosto de** → **gostchi** + **de** → **dji**
- **de São Paulo** → **dji São Paulo**
- Article elision: **sou o Carlos** → **sô Carlos**
- **Vowel sandhi**:
  - **na Acme** → **nAcme**
  - **pro escritório** → **proscritório** (o+e coalescence)
  - **no escritório** → **noscritório** (o+e coalescence)
  - **uma irmã** → **umirmã** (a+i coalescence)

### Teaching Point
- Third character creates trio dynamics
- Native Portuguese speaker (Brazilian)
- Introduces Brazilian Portuguese context
- Different marital status (solteiro vs casado/casada)
- Different neighborhood (Brooklyn vs Manhattan implied)
- Different transportation (ônibus vs metrô)

## 4. A GENTE - we informal (Singular Conjugation for Plural Meaning)

### NEW Pronouns
- **a gente** (we - informal, literally "the people")

### NEW Teaching Point: Singular Form for Plural Meaning
- Uses **SINGULAR** verb conjugations despite meaning "we":
  - a gente **é** (not somos)
  - a gente **mora** (not moramos)
  - a gente **trabalha** (not trabalhamos)
  - a gente **fala** (not falamos)
  - a gente **gosta** (not gostamos)
  - a gente **vai** (not vamos)
  - a gente **tem** (not temos)
  - a gente **está** (not estamos)

### NEW Adjectives (Singular despite plural meaning)
- **americano** (not americanos)
- **analista** (not analistas)
- **casado** (not casados)
- **contente** (not contentes)

### NEW Expressions
- **cada** (each) - "dois irmãos e duas irmãs cada"

### Teaching Point
- A gente = informal "we" in Brazilian Portuguese
- Always conjugates as 3rd person singular (like ele/ela)
- Uses masculine singular adjectives by default

## 5. VOCÊ - addressing someone (Second Person, Singular Conjugation)

### NEW Pronouns
- **você** (you - singular, formal)

### NEW Teaching Point: Third Person Forms for "You"
- Uses same conjugations as ele/ela:
  - você **é** (you are)
  - você **mora** (you live)
  - você **trabalha** (you work)
  - você **fala** (you speak)
  - você **gosta** (you like)
  - você **vai** (you go)
  - você **tem** (you have)
  - você **está** (you are)

### Reinforces
- All previous vocabulary
- Feminine agreement when addressing Sarah: **americana**, **casada**

### Teaching Point
- Você is formal/neutral "you" in Brazilian Portuguese
- Conjugates like third person singular, NOT second person
- Context change: from self-introduction → addressing others

## 6. VOCÊS - addressing multiple people (Second Person Plural)

### NEW Pronouns
- **vocês** (you all, y'all - plural)

### NEW Verb Forms (Third Person Plural)
- **são** (are) - from ser
- **moram** (live) - from morar
- **trabalham** (work) - from trabalhar
- **falam** (speak) - from falar
- **gostam** (like) - from gostar
- **vão** (go) - from ir
- **têm** (have) - from ter - NOTE: circumflex accent distinguishes from "tem" (singular)
- **estão** (are) - from estar

### NEW Adjectives (Plural)
- **americanos** (Americans - masculine plural)
- **analistas** (analysts - plural)
- **casados** (married - masculine plural)
- **contentes** (happy - plural)

### Teaching Point
- Vocês = "you all" / "y'all"
- Conjugates as third person plural (like eles/elas)
- First introduction of plural verb forms
- Accent on "têm" distinguishes plural from singular

## 9. NÓS - we formal (First Person Plural)

### NEW Pronouns
- **nós** (we - formal/standard)

### NEW Verb Forms (First Person Plural)
- **somos** (are) - from ser
- **moramos** (live) - from morar
- **trabalhamos** (work) - from trabalhar
- **falamos** (speak) - from falar
- **gostamos** (like) - from gostar
- **vamos** (go) - from ir
- **temos** (have) - from ter
- **estamos** (are) - from estar

### NEW Adjectives (Plural)
- **americanos** (Americans - masculine plural)
- **analistas** (analysts - plural)
- **casados** (married - masculine plural)
- **contentes** (happy - plural)

### Teaching Point
- Nós = formal/standard "we"
- First person plural conjugations (different from vocês/eles)
- Can drop pronoun (like eu): "Somos americanos"
- Regular -ar verbs: add -amos (trabalhamos)
- Contrast with "a gente" (informal we, singular conjugation)

## 10. ELES - describing multiple people (Third Person Plural)

### NEW Pronouns
- **eles** (they - masculine or mixed gender)

### Reinforces
- Same plural conjugations as vocês:
  - **são, moram, trabalham, falam, gostam, vão, têm, estão**
- Plural adjectives: **americanos**, **analistas**, **casados**, **contentes**

### Teaching Point
- Eles = "they" (masculine or mixed)
- Elas = "they" (all feminine) - not shown but implied
- Uses third person plural (same as vocês)
- Context: describing others (not addressing them)

## Pedagogical Progression

1. **JOHN**: Full foundation - all basic elements + phonetic reduction (Steps 4-5)
2. **SARAH**: Adds "também" variation (beginning/end positions) + phonetic reduction (Steps 4-5)
3. **CARLOS**: Brazilian colleague, introduces new nationality/context + phonetic reduction (Steps 4-5)
4. **A GENTE**: Introduces singular conjugation for plural meaning
5. **VOCÊ**: Reuses ele/ela conjugations in new context (addressing)
6. **VOCÊS**: First plural conjugations
7. **ELE**: Third person singular (describing others)
8. **ELA**: Reinforces with feminine agreement
9. **NÓS**: First person plural, can drop pronoun
10. **ELES**: Third person plural (describing others)

**Step 4: Dropping/Elision (Rule-Based Reductions)**
- Introduced in JOHN, SARAH, and CARLOS progressions
- **sou** → **sô**, **estou** → **tô** (vowel reduction)
- **para o** → **pro** (preposition+article contraction)
- Infinitive -r dropping: **falar** → **falá**, **viajar** → **viajá**
- Teaches systematic dropping/elision patterns

**Step 5: Full Colloquial Speech (All Phonetic Processes)**
- Introduced in JOHN, SARAH, and CARLOS progressions
- All Step 4 reductions plus:
- **vou** → **vô**, **tenho** → **tenhu** (vowel/diphthong reduction)
- Palatalization: **gosto de** → **gostchi dji**, **de + /i/** → **dji**
- Vowel coalescence: **de ônibus** → **djônibus**
- Article elision: **sou o/a...** → **sô...**
- **Vowel sandhi (word boundary coalescence)**:
  - **na avenida** → **navenida**, **na Acme** → **nAcme**
  - **no escritório** → **noscritório**, **pro escritório** → **proscritório**
  - **uma irmã** → **umirmã**
- Teaches natural spoken Brazilian Portuguese

**Total New Elements Introduced:** ~110+ vocabulary items, 64 unique verb conjugations (8 verbs × 8 forms), phonetic reductions, multiple grammar patterns
