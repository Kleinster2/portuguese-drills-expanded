Unit 1

Saying "I am"

Your first Portuguese sentence

1

What You'll Learn

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to introduce yourself in Portuguese. You'll say things like:

Eu sou a Maria.

H-oo SH ah mah-REE-ah

I am Maria.

Tip: Hover over Portuguese words to see their meaning. Click the speaker button to hear pronunciation.

STEP 1

Learn "Eu sou"

Eu sou

H-oo SH

= I am

Eu = I (the person speaking)
sou = am (from the verb "ser" - to be)
Why "sou"? Portuguese verbs change based on who's doing the action. "Sou" is the "I" form of "ser" (to be).
STEP 2

Say Your Name

When to use this: Meeting someone for the first time, introducing yourself.

Eu sou o/a + [your name]

o = for male names • a = for female names

Why "o" or "a"? In Brazilian Portuguese, names are treated as third-person nouns, so they take the definite article (o/a) - even when talking about yourself!

Examples:

Eu sou o Daniel.

STEP 3

Say What City You're From

When to use this: When someone asks where you're from, or when introducing yourself more fully.

Eu sou de + [city]

de = from, of

Pronunciation: In Brazilian Portuguese, "de" sounds like "djee" (the "d" before "e" or "i" becomes a "dj" sound).

Examples:

Eu sou de Miami.

H-oo SH jee mee-ah-MEE

I am from Miami.

Eu sou de Nova York.

H-oo SH jee NH-vah Hrk

I am from New York.

H-oo SH jee SH-oo PAH-oo-loo

I am from S茫o Paulo.
