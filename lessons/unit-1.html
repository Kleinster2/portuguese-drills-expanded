Meet Daniel - Character Progression

Follow Daniel as he introduces himself in three steps: explicit, implicit, and natural flow.

1

Step 1: With "eu" (explicit)

Beginner level - subject pronoun included

Eu sou o/u/ Daniel.
Eu sou americano/u/.
Eu sou de/dji/ Miami.
Eu sou analista.
Eu sou casado/u/ com/coun/ a Sofia.
Translation: I am Daniel. I am American. I am from Miami. I am an analyst. I am married to Sofia.
2

Step 2: Without "eu" (implicit)

Intermediate level - subject pronoun dropped

Sou o/u/ Daniel.
Sou americano/u/.
Sou de/dji/ Miami.
Sou analista.
Sou casado/u/ com/coun/ a Sofia.
üí° Note: In Portuguese, the subject pronoun "eu" is often dropped because the verb conjugation /-ou ending/ already indicates first person.
3

Step 3: Concatenated (natural flow)

Advanced level - sentences flow naturally together

Sou o/u/ Daniel. Sou americano/u/, de/dji/ Miami. Sou analista e/i/ sou casado/u/ com/coun/ a Sofia.

Translation: I'm Daniel. I'm American, from Miami. I'm an analyst and I'm married to Sofia.
