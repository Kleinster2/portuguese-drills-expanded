STEP 1

Greetings

Start any conversation with these greetings. Use the right one based on time of day!

OlÃ¡!

Hello! / Hi!

Use anytime - the universal greeting

Bom dia!

Good morning!

Use until around noon (literally "good day")

Boa tarde!

Good afternoon!

Use from noon until about 6pm

Boa noite!

Good evening! / Good night!

Use after 6pm - works for both greeting AND saying goodnight

Pattern: Notice bom (good, masculine) with dia (day, masculine), but boa (good, feminine) with tarde/noite (feminine).
STEP 2

Farewells

Different ways to say goodbye - from casual to more formal.

Tchau!

Bye!

Casual - from Italian "ciao"

Ate logo!

See you soon!

Literally "until soon"

Ate amanha!

See you tomorrow!

When you'll see them the next day

Ate mais!

See you later!

Very casual, common in Brazil

Tip: You can combine greetings with farewells! "Boa noite" works as both "Good evening" (greeting) and "Good night" (farewell).
STEP 3

Polite Expressions

Essential words for being polite in any situation.

Por favor

Please

Obrigado

male speaker

Obrigada

female speaker

Thank you

Com licenca

Excuse me (to pass / get attention)

Desculpe

Sorry / Excuse me (apology)

Important: Obrigado/Obrigada changes based on YOUR gender (the person saying thanks), not who you're thanking!
STEP 4

Common Responses

How to respond when someone thanks you or greets you.

De nada

You're welcome (literally "of nothing")

Tudo bem?

How are you? / Everything okay?

Tudo bem!

I'm fine! / All good!

Fun fact: "Tudo bem?" (question) and "Tudo bem!" (answer) are the same words - just change your intonation!
